[Turn 25 — 15:29:18 — StudyBuddy AI Tutor]

Of course! Implicit differentiation is definitely something I can help with. It's a calculus technique used when you have an equation that defines y as an implicit function of x, rather than in the explicit form y = f(x). Common examples include equations of circles (x² + y² = r²), ellipses, and other curves where it's difficult or impossible to solve for y explicitly.

The basic idea is:

1. Differentiate both sides of the equation with respect to x
2. Whenever you differentiate a term containing y, apply the chain rule and multiply by dy/dx
3. Solve the resulting equation for dy/dx

Here's a simple example:

**Find dy/dx for the equation x² + y² = 25**

Step 1: Differentiate both sides with respect to x:
d/dx(x²) + d/dx(y²) = d/dx(25)

Step 2: Apply the chain rule to the y² term:
2x + 2y(dy/dx) = 0

Step 3: Solve for dy/dx:
2y(dy/dx) = -2x
dy/dx = -2x / 2y
dy/dx = -x/y

So for the circle x² + y² = 25, the derivative at any point (x, y) on the circle is -x/y. You can verify this makes sense: at the point (3, 4), the slope would be -3/4, which is indeed the slope of the tangent line to the circle at that point.
