PROTOCOL STATUS HEADER AND ENROLLMENT METRICS

The National Cancer Center site for the ONC-204 study has maintained active status throughout the monitoring period ending August 12, 2023. The study design remains consistent with the Phase IIb protocol specifications approved by the Institutional Review Board and the relevant regulatory authorities. The principal investigator, Dr. T. Brown, continues to oversee all clinical operations, patient safety monitoring, and data integrity checks at this location. The site has demonstrated adherence to the Good Clinical Practice (GCP) guidelines and the protocol-specific requirements for the management of Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer patients.

Enrollment metrics for the ONC-204 study at the National Cancer Center site are documented as follows. The total number of subjects screened for eligibility criteria prior to inclusion in the study population stands at 420. Screening activities included review of medical history, physical examination, performance status assessment (ECOG 0-2), and confirmation of Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer diagnosis via histological or cytological verification. Additional screening procedures involved mandatory biomarker testing, including PD-L1 expression analysis and circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) quantification, to ensure subject stratification aligned with the study's inclusion criteria.

Of the 420 subjects initially screened, 115 subjects met all inclusion criteria and were formally enrolled into the ONC-204 study. Enrollment was conducted sequentially, with informed consent obtained prior to any study-related procedures. All enrolled subjects underwent a baseline assessment period to establish pre-treatment metrics, including laboratory values, radiographic imaging, and performance status.

Following enrollment, 112 subjects proceeded to the randomization phase of the study. The randomization process was performed using a centralized interactive web response system (IWRS) to ensure allocation concealment. The randomization ratio for the Phase IIb design was maintained at 1:1 between the active treatment arm and the control arm, as specified in the statistical analysis plan. The three subjects who were enrolled but not randomized were excluded from the randomization analysis set due to protocol deviations identified prior to the randomization window, specifically related to the timing of the first dose administration relative to the screening window. These subjects were managed according to the protocol-specified withdrawal procedures.

The current active treatment population consists of 112 randomized subjects. All randomized subjects have been assigned to a specific treatment arm and are receiving study medication in accordance with the dosing schedule. The site has maintained a high standard of documentation for all randomized subjects, with complete case report forms (CRFs) submitted for the majority of study visits. The enrollment rate has remained stable, with no significant deviations from the projected enrollment timeline for the Phase IIb stage. The site has reported no issues regarding subject recruitment capacity or retention rates during the current monitoring period.

The principal investigator, Dr. T. Brown, has confirmed that all study personnel involved in the ONC-204 trial have completed the required training modules, including GCP training, protocol-specific training, and emergency response procedures. The site has also maintained up-to-date investigator brochures and regulatory correspondence files. Regular monitoring visits have been conducted by the clinical research associates (CRAs) to verify source data, ensure compliance with the protocol, and assess the safety of the study participants. No critical findings or major deviations have been reported that would impact the integrity of the data or the safety of the subjects.

The National Cancer Center site continues to serve as a key enrollment hub for the ONC-204 study. The site's performance metrics indicate a robust operational capacity to manage the complexities of a Phase IIb trial in the setting of Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer. The site has successfully navigated the logistical requirements of coordinating multiple treatment arms, managing adverse events, and conducting the necessary biomarker analyses. The data generated at this site is considered reliable and suitable for inclusion in the final study database for regulatory submission.

ADVERSE EVENT REPORTS AND SAFETY MONITORING LOGS

The safety monitoring component of the ONC-204 Phase IIb study at the National Cancer Center site has been conducted in accordance with the protocol-specified safety reporting requirements. Adverse events (AEs) were defined as any untoward medical occurrence in a subject or clinical investigation subject administered a pharmaceutical product, which did not necessarily have to have a causal relationship with this treatment. All adverse events were collected from the time of the first dose of study medication until the final follow-up visit or study completion.

Two specific adverse event reports have been documented for the current monitoring snapshot period. These reports represent the most recent safety updates for the subjects under active treatment at the National Cancer Center site. The details of these reports are provided below with full adherence to MedDRA coding standards and protocol-specific severity grading criteria.

Adverse Event Report AE-204-013:
Subject ID: S-1220
Onset Date: 2023-08-01
Severity: Grade 1
Coded Term: Headache (MedDRA Preferred Term)
Relationship Assessment: Unlikely

The subject identified as S-1220 presented with an adverse event characterized by headache on August 1, 2023. The event was classified as Grade 1 according to the National Cancer Institute Common Terminology Criteria for Adverse Events (NCI CTCAE) version 5.0. Grade 1 severity indicates that the event was mild, asymptomatic, or mild symptoms, and clinical or diagnostic observations were indicated, but intervention was not required. The subject reported the headache during a routine follow-up visit. The onset of the headache was noted to be mild in intensity, described by the subject as a tension-type sensation without associated neurological deficits, visual disturbances, or nausea.

The relationship assessment for this adverse event was determined to be "Unlikely" in relation to the study drug XR-7491. This assessment was based on the temporal relationship between the dosing of the study medication and the onset of the symptom, as well as the known safety profile of the drug. The headache occurred several days after the administration of the previous cycle dose, and the subject had a history of occasional tension headaches prior to enrollment in the study. The medical monitor reviewed the case and determined that the event was consistent with the subject's baseline health status and did not warrant any specific intervention beyond standard supportive care. The subject continued to receive the study medication without interruption, and the headache resolved spontaneously within 48 hours without the need for pharmacological intervention.

Adverse Event Report AE-204-014:
Subject ID: S-1235
Onset Date: 2023-08-05
Severity: Grade 2
Coded Term: Erythema (MedDRA Preferred Term)
Relationship Assessment: Related

The subject identified as S-1235 presented with an adverse event characterized by erythema on August 5, 2023. The event was classified as Grade 2 according to the NCI CTCAE version 5.0. Grade 2 severity indicates that the event was moderate and required minimal, local, or non-invasive intervention. The subject reported the appearance of erythema on the upper extremities during a scheduled clinic visit. The erythema was observed to be localized to the forearms and hands, with mild redness and no associated blistering or ulceration. The subject described the sensation as mild itching but denied any pain or systemic symptoms such as fever or chills.

The relationship assessment for this adverse event was determined to be "Related" to the study drug XR-7491. This determination was based on the temporal association between the administration of the study medication and the onset of the skin reaction, as well as the known dermatological side effects associated with JAK inhibitors. The onset of the erythema occurred within the expected timeframe for such reactions following the administration of the cycle 8 dose. The medical monitor reviewed the case and confirmed that the event was consistent with a known safety signal for the drug class.

Management of the adverse event involved the initiation of topical corticosteroid therapy and the administration of antihistamines as per the protocol-specified guidelines for Grade 2 dermatological events. The subject was instructed to avoid direct sun exposure and to apply the topical medication as directed. The subject was monitored closely for progression of the reaction or the development of additional symptoms. The study medication was held temporarily for 48 hours pending the resolution of the erythema. Upon confirmation that the erythema had improved to Grade 1 or resolved, the subject was permitted to resume the study medication at the same dose level. No dose reductions were required for this subject. The event was reported to the Data Safety Monitoring Board (DSMB) and the regulatory authorities in accordance with the safety reporting timelines.

Both adverse events have been fully documented in the subject case report forms and the central safety database. The investigators have reviewed all new adverse events for causality, severity, and required actions. No serious adverse events (SAEs) or unexpected serious adverse reactions (USARs) have been reported for these subjects during the current monitoring period. The overall safety profile of the study drug at the National Cancer Center site remains consistent with the data observed in earlier phases of the ONC-204 trial.

LABORATORY PANELS AND BIOMARKER ANALYSIS

Comprehensive laboratory testing was performed on subjects S-1220 and S-1235 on July 30, 2023, as part of the Cycle 8 monitoring schedule. The laboratory panels included standard hematology and chemistry tests, as well as specific biomarker assays required for the stratification and efficacy analysis of the ONC-204 study. All laboratory results were generated using validated assays at the central reference laboratory accredited for the handling of oncology specimens.

Laboratory Panel for Subject S-1220:
Test Date: 2023-07-30
PD-L1 Expression: 95%
ctDNA Copy Number: 5 copies/mL
TSH: 1.6 mIU/L

Subject S-1220 underwent a comprehensive biomarker assessment on July 30, 2023. The PD-L1 expression level was determined to be 95%, indicating a high level of programmed death-ligand 1 expression on tumor cells. This value was derived from immunohistochemistry (IHC) staining of the tumor biopsy specimen, with the percentage representing the proportion of tumor cells showing membranous staining of any intensity. The high PD-L1 expression level is consistent with the subject's baseline characteristics and aligns with the inclusion criteria for the high-expression cohort in the Phase IIb study.

The circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) copy number for subject S-1220 was measured at 5 copies/mL. This value was obtained via next-generation sequencing (NGS) of plasma samples. The ctDNA copy number reflects the burden of tumor-derived genetic material circulating in the blood. A level of 5 copies/mL indicates a low circulating tumor burden at the time of sampling. This finding is consistent with the subject's clinical response and supports the efficacy of the study drug in reducing tumor load. The ctDNA analysis was performed using a validated digital PCR assay, and the results were confirmed by a second independent run to ensure accuracy.

The Thyroid Stimulating Hormone (TSH) level for subject S-1220 was recorded as 1.6 mIU/L. This value falls within the standard reference range of 0.4 to 4.0 mIU/L. The TSH level indicates normal thyroid function, with no evidence of hypothyroidism or hyperthyroidism. This is a relevant parameter given the potential for immune-mediated endocrine adverse events associated with JAK inhibitors. The normal TSH level suggests that the subject's thyroid function remains stable and is not adversely affected by the study medication.

Laboratory Panel for Subject S-1235:
Test Date: 2023-07-30
PD-L1 Expression: 35%
ctDNA Copy Number: 110 copies/mL
TSH: 2.5 mIU/L

Subject S-1235 underwent a comprehensive biomarker assessment on July 30, 2023, concurrent with subject S-1220. The PD-L1 expression level for this subject was determined to be 35%. This value was also derived from IHC staining of the tumor biopsy specimen. The 35% expression level indicates a moderate level of PD-L1 expression, which is consistent with the subject's stratification in the study. The subject falls within the intermediate expression cohort, which is a key subgroup for the efficacy analysis of the ONC-204 trial.

The circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) copy number for subject S-1235 was measured at 110 copies/mL. This value was obtained via the same validated NGS assay used for subject S-1220. A level of 110 copies/mL indicates a higher circulating tumor burden compared to subject S-1220. This finding is consistent with the subject's baseline characteristics and suggests a more active disease state at the time of sampling. The elevated ctDNA level is an important marker for monitoring treatment response and may influence the assessment of efficacy in this subject. The results were verified through a second independent run to ensure the accuracy of the high copy number measurement.

The Thyroid Stimulating Hormone (TSH) level for subject S-1235 was recorded as 2.5 mIU/L. This value falls within the standard reference range of 0.4 to 4.0 mIU/L. The TSH level indicates normal thyroid function for this subject as well. The stability of the TSH level suggests that the subject's thyroid function is not compromised by the study medication. This is a positive indicator of the safety profile of the drug in this patient population.

In addition to the specific biomarkers listed above, standard laboratory panels including complete blood count (CBC) with differential, comprehensive metabolic panel (CMP), and liver function tests (LFTs) were performed on both subjects. All values for CBC, CMP, and LFTs were within normal limits or showed no clinically significant deviations from baseline. No signs of hepatotoxicity, hematologic toxicity, or metabolic abnormalities were observed in the laboratory results for either subject. The laboratory data supports the continued administration of the study medication to both subjects.

The biomarker data collected on July 30, 2023, will be integrated into the final efficacy analysis of the ONC-204 study. The correlation between PD-L1 expression levels, ctDNA copy numbers, and clinical response will be evaluated to determine the predictive value of these biomarkers in the treatment of Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer. The data from these two subjects contribute to the overall dataset used for stratification and subgroup analysis.

The PK parameters were calculated using non-compartmental analysis methods and are reported with standard statistical precision.

Pharmacokinetic Summary for Subject S-1220:
Subject ID: S-1220
Cmax (Maximum Concentration): 152.0 ng/mL
Tmax (Time to Maximum Concentration): 1.9 hours
Half-Life (t1/2): 19.2 hours

The maximum plasma concentration (Cmax) for subject S-1220 was determined to be 152.0 ng/mL. The Cmax value is consistent with the expected exposure levels for the drug in this patient population and falls within the range observed in previous cycles and earlier phases of the study. The high Cmax indicates adequate drug exposure at the time of administration.

The time to maximum concentration (Tmax) for subject S-1220 was recorded as 1.9 hours. This parameter reflects the time required for the drug to reach its peak concentration in the plasma after administration. The Tmax value of 1.9 hours is consistent with the rapid distribution phase of the drug following intravenous infusion. The quick attainment of peak concentration is typical for intravenous administration and supports the dosing schedule of every three weeks.

The half-life (t1/2) for subject S-1220 was calculated to be 19.2 hours. This parameter indicates the time required for the plasma concentration of the drug to decrease by 50% during the elimination phase. The half-life of 19.2 hours suggests that the drug is eliminated from the body relatively quickly, supporting the dosing interval of every three weeks. The half-life value is consistent with the pharmacokinetic profile observed in other subjects in the study and aligns with the known properties of the drug class.

The pharmacokinetic data for subject S-1220 indicates that the drug exposure is within the therapeutic range and that the dosing regimen is appropriate for maintaining effective drug levels throughout the treatment cycle. The PK parameters provide a quantitative basis for understanding the drug's behavior in the body and support the safety and efficacy findings of the study. The data will be used to refine the dosing recommendations for future phases of the ONC-204 trial.
