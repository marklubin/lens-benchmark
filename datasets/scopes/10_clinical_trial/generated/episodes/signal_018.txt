STUDY OVERVIEW AND BACKGROUND

This clinical trial data bundle represents a routine snapshot of the Phase IIb study of XR-7491, an oral JAK inhibitor indicated for the treatment of rheumatoid arthritis. The study design is a randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, multicenter trial. The primary objective is to evaluate the efficacy and safety of XR-7491 at multiple dosage levels compared to placebo. The study is currently in the active enrollment and treatment phase. All procedures are conducted in accordance with the Declaration of Helsinki and Good Clinical Practice (GCP) guidelines. The protocol version 2.0 is active for all participating sites. Site activation approved for SITE-018 and SITE-001.

The study population consists of adult patients diagnosed with moderate to severe rheumatoid arthritis who have failed to respond to at least one disease-modifying antirheumatic drug (DMARD). The study involves stratification by disease activity score and prior treatment history. Randomization is stratified by center to ensure balance across study arms. The randomization code is managed by an interactive response technology (IRT) system. The randomization code: ARM-03 (30mg) is assigned to specific subjects based on the IRT algorithm.
