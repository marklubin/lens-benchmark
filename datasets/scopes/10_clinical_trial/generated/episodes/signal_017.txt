1. PROTOCOL STATUS HEADER AND ADMINISTRATIVE OVERVIEW

This document serves as the consolidated monitoring snapshot for the XR-7491 Phase IIb study. The data contained herein reflects the status of the study as of 2025-04-20. All procedures described herein were conducted in accordance with the Good Clinical Practice guidelines as defined by the International Conference on Harmonisation and the Declaration of Helsinki. The study protocol, version 2.0, is active and governs all activities at the participating sites. The primary objective of this study is to evaluate the efficacy of XR-7491, an oral JAK inhibitor, compared to placebo in reducing the signs and symptoms of rheumatoid arthritis. Secondary objectives include the evaluation of safety and tolerability of XR-7491.

The study sites are categorized by geographic region and enrollment capacity. This specific bundle focuses on data received from SITE-017 and SITE-001, along with central laboratory data and the Data Safety Monitoring Board (DSMB) review. The Principal Investigator for SITE-017 is PI_Garcia. The Principal Investigator for SITE-001 is PI_Johnson. Both sites are currently open for enrollment. The site activation approval was granted on 2024-11-15. The randomization system is managed centrally by the Interactive Web Response System (IWRS). The IWRS ensures that treatment allocation is concealed from the investigator and study staff until the point of assignment.

Regulatory compliance is monitored through regular audits and inspections. The current regulatory status for SITE-017 is compliant. The current regulatory status for SITE-001 is compliant. All deviations from the protocol are documented in the Deviation Log. There are no major protocol deviations recorded for the period 2025-04-01 to 2025-04-20. The informed consent process is verified through the signed copies stored in the Investigator Site File. The informed consent form used is the current version approved by the Institutional Review Board (IRB). The IRB for SITE-017 is IRB_CENTRAL_WEST. The IRB for SITE-001 is IRB_CENTRAL_EAST.

The study drug, XR-7491, is manufactured under Good Manufacturing Practice conditions. The investigational product account is maintained at each site. The inventory of XR-7491 is tracked by lot number and expiration date. The chain of custody for the investigational product is maintained in the Investigational Product Log. The medication counts are reconciled monthly. The reconciliation process ensures that the number of tablets dispensed matches the number of tablets returned plus the number of tablets consumed. The dispensing log is reviewed by the Compliance Officer at the end of each month. The Compliance Officer is responsible for verifying the accuracy of the medication logs.

The study duration for each subject is approximately 52 weeks. The follow-up period is 8 weeks post-treatment. The primary endpoint is the proportion of subjects achieving ACR20 response at Week 12. The secondary endpoints include DAS28 score reduction and ACR50 response. The statistical analysis plan defines the methods for handling missing data. The imputation method used is Last Observation Carried Forward. The analysis population includes all randomized subjects who received at least one dose of study medication. The safety population includes all subjects who received at least one dose of study medication. The efficacy population includes all subjects who have at least one post-baseline efficacy assessment.

The Data Safety Monitoring Board (DSMB) meets at scheduled intervals to review unblinded safety and efficacy data. The DSMB composition includes independent experts in rheumatology, biostatistics, and pharmacology. The DSMB provides recommendations to the Sponsor regarding the continuation of the study. The DSMB reviews are confidential and the data is presented in an aggregated format. The DSMB notes are recorded in the meeting minutes and stored in the secure document management system. The DSMB Chair is Dr. Smith. The DSMB Vice-Chair is Dr. Jones. The Sponsor Representative is Dr. Williams.
