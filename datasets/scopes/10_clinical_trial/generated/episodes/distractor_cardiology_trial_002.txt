SECTION 2: INCLUSION AND EXCLUSION CRITERIA REFERENCE

The following criteria were applied during the screening process for all potential subjects at Site-002. These criteria define the study population.

Inclusion Criteria:
1. Male or female subjects 18 years of age or older.
2. Diagnosis of Rheumatoid Arthritis according to the American College of Rheumatology (ACR) 1987 revised criteria.
3. Active disease defined as a Disease Activity Score in 28 joints (DAS28) greater than or equal to 3.2.
4. Failure to respond to at least one disease-modifying antirheumatic drug (DMARD).
5. Ability to provide written informed consent.
6. Female subjects of childbearing potential must have a negative serum pregnancy test.
7. Male subjects must agree to use a barrier method of contraception.

Exclusion Criteria:
1. History of myocardial infarction within 6 months of screening.
2. History of stroke within 6 months of screening.
3. Unstable angina.
4. New York Heart Association (NYHA) Class III or IV heart failure.
5. Left ventricular ejection fraction less than 40%.
6. Active infection requiring systemic antibiotics.
7. History of malignancy within 5 years.
8. Known hypersensitivity to XR-7491 or its components.
9. Concomitant use of other JAK inhibitors.
10. Concomitant use of strong CYP3A4 inhibitors.

These criteria were strictly adhered to during the enrollment process. The enrollment count of 145 reflects subjects who met these criteria at the time of randomization.
