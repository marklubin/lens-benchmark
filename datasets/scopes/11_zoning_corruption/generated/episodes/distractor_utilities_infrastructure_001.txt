SECTION C: PROJECT SCOPE AND DESCRIPTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Project Title: North District Water Treatment Plant Upgrades - Phase II
Project Type: Infrastructure Replacement and Modernization
Location: North District Water Treatment Plant, Parcel NW-8892.

Detailed Description of Work:
The Applicant requests approval for construction permits to facilitate the replacement of aging high-volume pumping infrastructure and the installation of advanced water sterilization technology. The proposed scope of work is limited to the existing utility footprint and does not involve the expansion of the facility's building envelope beyond the designated equipment pad.

1. High-Volume Pump Replacement:
   The Applicant proposes the removal and disposal of three existing centrifugal pumps, manufactured in 1998, which have exceeded their operational lifecycle. These pumps are currently designated as Units 1, 2, and 3 in the main pump house.
   The proposed replacement involves the installation of three new high-efficiency, variable-frequency drive (VFD) pumps. The new units are specified to provide increased flow capacity while maintaining the existing discharge pressure requirements.
   Demolition of existing pump foundations is required only to the extent necessary for the installation of new anchor bolts and piping interfaces. All debris generated from the removal of the three existing pumps and associated piping will be hauled to the Oakhaven Regional Landfill, Transfer Station B, in accordance with hazardous waste disposal protocols.

2. UV Sterilization Unit Installation:
   The project includes the installation of a new ultraviolet (UV) sterilization system to enhance water quality treatment standards. This system consists of three parallel treatment banks, each equipped with high-intensity UV lamps and quartz sleeves.
   The new UV units will be installed in a dedicated mechanical room adjacent to the existing pump house. The installation requires minor structural reinforcement of the existing floor slab to support the weight of the equipment and associated fluid reservoirs.
   Piping modifications will be made to the existing water main to integrate the new sterilization units into the flow path. All new piping will be constructed of Schedule 40 PVC, rated for potable water use, and insulated to prevent thermal loss.

3. Equipment Enclosure Construction:
   An above-ground equipment enclosure is required to house the control panels and auxiliary components of the new UV sterilization system.
   Dimensions: 120 square feet (10 feet by 12 feet).
   Height: 14 feet maximum, including roof overhang.
   Material Specification: The enclosure shall be constructed of galvanized steel with a minimum gauge of 12. The exterior finish shall be a high-reflectivity, white enamel coating to minimize heat absorption and reduce the urban heat island effect.
   The enclosure will be bolted to a new concrete pad measuring 12 feet by 14 feet, poured to a depth of 6 inches with a 4-inch layer of compacted gravel sub-base.
   Ventilation: Two passive louvered vents (24 inches by 24 inches each) will be installed on the north and south walls to ensure adequate airflow for the control electronics.
   Security: The enclosure will be fitted with a single-point locking mechanism and a tamper-resistant security door.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION D: ARCHITECTURAL AND ENGINEERING SUMMARIES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Architectural Summary:
The proposed construction of the 120 sq. ft. above-ground equipment enclosure is designed to be fully compatible with the existing industrial aesthetic of the North District Water Treatment Plant. The galvanized steel structure will feature a single-pitch roof with a 1/4 inch per foot slope to ensure proper drainage. The roof material shall be standing seam metal, color-matched to the existing facility roofs.
The enclosure will not require any foundation excavation deeper than 18 inches below the existing grade. No changes to the building's exterior lighting or signage are proposed. The structure is classified as an accessory structure and does not require a separate Certificate of Occupancy, provided it remains within the designated utility zone.

Engineering Summary:
The hydraulic design for the new pump configuration has been completed by the Engineering Division. The new pumps are sized to handle a peak flow rate of 4,500 gallons per minute (GPM) each, with a combined maximum capacity of 13,500 GPM.
The piping network has been redesigned to accommodate the new flow dynamics. All new valves will be butterfly valves with pneumatic actuators to allow for remote operation and integration into the central SCADA system.
The electrical load for the new pumps and UV units has been calculated. The total connected load is estimated at 185 kilowatts (kW). The existing electrical service panel at the plant has been verified to have sufficient capacity to accommodate this load with the addition of a new 200-amp feeder circuit.

SUBJECT: Hydraulic Modeling for Phase II Upgrades - North District Water Treatment Plant
REFERENCE: Permit Application 2024-P-0214-NW8892
PARCEL ID: NW-8892

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. PURPOSE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The purpose of this memorandum is to provide the Engineering Division's analysis regarding the hydraulic modeling results for the proposed North District Water Treatment Plant Upgrades - Phase II. This analysis supports the review of Permit Application 2024-P-0214-NW8892 and informs the Director of Public Works regarding the system impacts of the proposed pump replacement and UV sterilization installation.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. BACKGROUND
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The North District Water Treatment Plant (NDWTP) serves a population of approximately 45,000 residents in the northern quadrant of the City of Oakhaven. The current infrastructure, installed in the late 1990s, is approaching the end of its useful life. The Engineering Division has conducted a comprehensive audit of the system, identifying the three high-volume pumps (Units 1, 2, and 3) as critical components requiring immediate replacement to prevent system failure and ensure compliance with the State of Oregon Department of Environmental Quality (DEQ) water quality standards.

The proposed project involves the replacement of these three pumps and the installation of new UV sterilization units. The Engineering Division has completed hydraulic modeling simulations to evaluate the impact of the new equipment on system pressure, flow rates, and overall network stability.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. HYDRAULIC MODELING RESULTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The hydraulic model was developed using EPANET 2.2 software, calibrated against field data collected from the NDWTP during the last fiscal year. The model incorporates the proposed pump curves for the new variable-frequency drive (VFD) units and the head loss characteristics of the new piping and UV sterilization system.

3.1 System Pressure Analysis
The primary objective of the Phase II upgrades is to maintain adequate pressure throughout the distribution network during peak usage hours. The modeling results indicate that the new pump configuration will increase system pressure by 15 psi during peak hours (typically between 6:00 AM and 9:00 AM, and 5:00 PM and 8:00 PM).

Current System Pressure:
   Minimum Pressure at Peak Hour: 45 psi
   Maximum Pressure at Peak Hour: 65 psi
   Average Pressure: 55 psi

Projected System Pressure (Post-Upgrade):
   Minimum Pressure at Peak Hour: 60 psi
   Maximum Pressure at Peak Hour: 80 psi
   Average Pressure: 70 psi

The increase of 15 psi is consistent with the design specifications for the new pumps. This pressure increase will ensure that the upper elevations of the North District, which currently experience marginal pressure during peak demand, will receive adequate water service. The modeling also confirms that the pressure will not exceed the maximum allowable limit of 100 psi for the residential distribution pipes, thereby preventing pipe bursts or fitting failures.

3.2 Flow Rate Analysis
The new pumps are designed to operate at a higher efficiency curve than the existing units. The hydraulic model predicts a flow rate increase of approximately 12% during non-peak hours, allowing for more rapid filling of storage tanks. During peak hours, the flow rate will remain within the design capacity of the existing transmission mains, as the increased pressure compensates for the friction losses associated with the new UV sterilization units.

3.3 Energy Consumption
The use of variable-frequency drives on the new pumps allows for precise control of the motor speed based on real-time demand. The modeling indicates a 22% reduction in energy consumption compared to the existing fixed-speed pumps. This reduction will offset a portion of the initial capital investment over the lifecycle of the project.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. EASEMENT AND RIGHT-OF-WAY IMPACTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A critical component of the review process involves the assessment of any changes to existing easements or rights-of-way. The Engineering Division has conducted a site survey and cross-referenced the proposed construction plans with the City's easement database.

Findings:
   1. No changes to existing easements are required for the pump replacement or the UV sterilization installation.
   2. All new equipment will be located within the existing utility footprint of the NDWTP, which is fully enclosed by the current property lines of Parcel ID: NW-8892.
   3. The 120 sq. ft. equipment enclosure will be constructed entirely within the designated equipment pad area, which is already zoned for industrial utility use.
   4. No excavation will extend beyond the 50-foot construction zone boundary established in the environmental review.
   5. No new easements are needed for the installation of the new piping or electrical feeders.

Conclusion:
The project can proceed without the need for any legal modifications to existing easements. The current property boundaries and easement agreements are sufficient to accommodate the proposed infrastructure upgrades.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. BUDGET ALLOCATION AND FINANCIAL IMPLICATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The total estimated cost for the project is $4,250,000. This amount has been allocated from the following funding sources:
   - General Obligation Bond, Series 2023-A: $2,500,000.00
   - Utility Rate Surcharge, Account 9001: $1,250,000.00
   - Reserve Fund, Capital Improvement Program: $500,000.00

The Engineering Division recommends that the budget allocation of $4,250,000 be approved as part of the Capital Improvement Plan (CIP) for Fiscal Year 2024. This allocation includes all costs for equipment, labor, materials, engineering design, and contingency.

5.1 Cost Contingency
A contingency fund of 10% has been included in the total budget to account for unforeseen conditions, such as unexpected soil conditions during excavation or price fluctuations in raw materials (e.g., steel, copper). The Engineering Division advises that this contingency be managed centrally by the Public Works Finance Department.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. RECOMMENDATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Based on the hydraulic modeling and site analysis, the Engineering Division recommends the following:
   1. Approval of the permit application for the replacement of three high-volume pumps and installation of new UV sterilization units at Parcel NW-8892.
   2. Authorization for the construction of the 120 sq. ft. above-ground equipment enclosure constructed of galvanized steel.
   3. Confirmation that no changes to existing easements are required.
   4. Allocation of the budget of $4,250,000 for the project.
   5. Scheduling of the construction phase to begin no earlier than March 1, 2024, to allow for the completion of the environmental review and public notification process.

PRELIMINARY ENVIRONMENTAL ASSESSMENT
PROJECT: North District Water Treatment Plant Upgrades - Phase II
PARCEL ID: NW-8892
DATE OF REVIEW: March 5, 2024
REFERENCE: PERMIT 2024-P-0214-NW8892

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. PROJECT DESCRIPTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The City of Oakhaven, through the Oakhaven Utilities Management Group, proposes to undertake infrastructure upgrades at the North District Water Treatment Plant (NDWTP). The project involves the replacement of three high-volume pumps and the installation of new ultraviolet (UV) sterilization units. The scope of work also includes the construction of a 120 sq. ft. above-ground equipment enclosure constructed of galvanized steel. The project is located at Parcel ID: NW-8892, within the I-2 (Heavy Industrial) zoning district.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. PURPOSE AND NEED
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The purpose of this environmental assessment is to evaluate the potential environmental impacts of the proposed project. The need for the project is driven by the aging condition of the existing water treatment infrastructure, which poses a risk to system reliability and compliance with water quality standards. The upgrades are necessary to ensure the continued delivery of safe, potable water to the residents of the North District.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. METHODOLOGY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The Environmental Protection Office conducted a preliminary assessment based on the following:
   - Review of the permit application submitted on February 14, 2024.
   - Site inspection conducted on February 28, 2024.
   - Analysis of historical environmental data for the NDWTP site.
   - Consultation with the City's Water Quality Division.
   - Review of the Engineering Division's hydraulic modeling report.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACT ANALYSIS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4.1 Noise Impact Assessment
Construction activities associated with the replacement of the high-volume pumps and the installation of the UV sterilization units will generate noise. The primary sources of noise include:
   - Demolition of existing pump foundations.
   - Operation of heavy machinery (excavators, cranes, concrete mixers).
   - Installation of new equipment and piping.

The Environmental Protection Office has projected noise levels during the construction phase. Based on the distance of the nearest residential property (approximately 450 feet from the construction zone) and the duration of the work, the noise levels are projected to remain under 65 dBA at the property line. This level is consistent with the City's noise ordinance for industrial construction activities during standard working hours (7:00 AM to 6:00 PM).

No significant noise impacts are anticipated during the operation of the new equipment. The new pumps are equipped with noise-dampening enclosures, and the UV sterilization units are designed to operate quietly. The projected operational noise level is less than 55 dBA.

4.2 Biological Resources and Habitats
The site inspection revealed no wetlands or protected habitats within the 50-foot construction zone. The NDWTP is located in a previously developed industrial area, and the surrounding land has been modified for utility purposes for over two decades. No endangered or threatened species have been identified in the immediate vicinity of the project site.

The 50-foot construction zone boundary was established to ensure a buffer around the existing infrastructure. No vegetation removal is required within this zone, as the area is currently covered with asphalt and gravel. Therefore, no adverse impacts on biological resources are anticipated.

4.3 Water Quality and Waste Water Discharge
The project involves minor modifications to the water treatment process. The installation of the UV sterilization units will enhance the disinfection process, thereby improving water quality. There are no proposed changes to the waste water discharge permits.

The Environmental Protection Office has reviewed the current waste water discharge permit held by the NDWTP. The permit remains in compliance with EPA standards. The construction activities will not introduce new pollutants into the water system. Any wastewater generated during the construction phase (e.g., from concrete washing or equipment cleaning) will be treated and disposed of in accordance with City of Oakhaven waste management protocols.

4.4 Air Quality
Construction activities will generate minor amounts of dust and particulate matter. The Environmental Protection Office requires the contractor to implement dust control measures, including the use of water sprays and the covering of stockpiled materials. The new pumps and UV units are electrically powered and will not generate emissions during operation. The project is not expected to have a significant impact on local air quality.

4.5 Hazardous Materials
The existing pumps and associated piping may contain trace amounts of hazardous materials, such as lead-based paint or asbestos in insulation. The demolition plan includes protocols for the safe removal and disposal of these materials. The contractor, Apex Heavy Industrial Solutions, is required to follow all OSHA and EPA guidelines for hazardous material handling. No new hazardous materials will be introduced to the site.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. MITIGATION MEASURES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
To minimize potential environmental impacts, the following mitigation measures are required:
   1. Noise Control: Construction activities must be limited to standard working hours (7:00 AM to 6:00 PM). Noisier activities, such as jackhammering, are prohibited on weekends and holidays.
   2. Dust Control: The contractor must use water sprays to suppress dust during excavation and demolition.
   3. Waste Management: All construction debris must be hauled to the Oakhaven Regional Landfill, Transfer Station B. Hazardous waste must be disposed of at a licensed facility.
   4. Spill Prevention: A spill containment plan must be in place to prevent the release of fluids (e.g., hydraulic oil, cleaning solvents) into the storm drain system.
   5. Vegetation Protection: No vegetation within the 50-foot construction zone will be removed or damaged.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. CONCLUSIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Based on the preliminary assessment, the proposed project at Parcel NW-8892 will not result in significant adverse environmental impacts. The project is consistent with the City's Comprehensive Plan and the State of Oregon Environmental Policy Act. The construction noise levels are projected to remain under 65 dBA. No wetlands or protected habitats are identified within the 50-foot construction zone. The waste water discharge permit remains in compliance with EPA standards.

The Environmental Protection Office recommends the issuance of the necessary permits, subject to the implementation of the mitigation measures outlined in this report.

Subject: Concern Regarding Construction Noise at North District Water Treatment Plant

To the Zoning Board and City Council:

I am writing to express my concern regarding the proposed construction of the new pumps and UV sterilization units at the North District Water Treatment Plant. My residence is located approximately 450 feet from the construction site at Parcel NW-8892.

PROJECT: North District Water Treatment Plant Upgrades - Phase II

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. PROJECT DESCRIPTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The City of Oakhaven proposes to undertake infrastructure upgrades at the North District Water Treatment Plant (NDWTP). The project involves the replacement of three high-volume pumps and the installation of new ultraviolet (UV) sterilization units. The scope of work also includes the construction of a 120 sq. ft. above-ground equipment enclosure constructed of galvanized steel. The project is located at Parcel ID: NW-8892, within the I-2 (Heavy Industrial) zoning district.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. PURPOSE AND NEED
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The purpose of this environmental assessment is to evaluate the potential environmental impacts of the proposed project. The need for the project is driven by the aging condition of the existing water treatment infrastructure, which poses a risk to system reliability and compliance with water quality standards. The upgrades are necessary to ensure the continued delivery of safe, potable water to the residents of the North District.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. PUBLIC COMMENT PERIOD
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A public comment period has been established for this project. The period begins on March 5, 2024, and ends on March 19, 2024.

Members of the public are invited to submit comments regarding the proposed project. Comments may be submitted in writing, via email, or in person at the Zoning and Building Department.

Contact Information for Comments:
   Address: 1200 Main Street, Suite 100, Oakhaven, OR 97001
   Email: comments@oakhaven-city.gov
   Phone: (555) 019-3344

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACT SUMMARY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4.1 Noise Impact
Noise levels during construction are projected to remain under 65 dBA. The Environmental Protection Office has determined that this level is acceptable given the industrial zoning of the site and the distance to the nearest residential properties.

4.2 Biological Resources
No wetlands or protected habitats have been identified within the 50-foot construction zone. The site is located in a previously developed industrial area, and no endangered species are present.

4.3 Water Quality
The waste water discharge permit remains in compliance with EPA standards. The project will not introduce new pollutants into the water system.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Zoning Board will review the public comments and the environmental assessment at the meeting scheduled for March 12, 2024. Following the meeting, a final decision will be made regarding the issuance of permits.

3. PERFORMANCE REQUIREMENTS
   - The pumps must meet the hydraulic performance curves provided in the engineering drawings.
   - The pumps must be tested at the manufacturer's facility before shipment.
   - A performance test must be conducted on-site after installation.

4. INSTALLATION
   - The pumps must be installed by certified technicians.
   - All piping connections must be made using flanged connections.
   - The pumps must be anchored to the concrete foundation using epoxy anchors.

5. MAINTENANCE
   - The pumps must be designed for easy maintenance.
   - All moving parts must be accessible without the need for special tools.
   - A maintenance manual must be provided with each pump.
