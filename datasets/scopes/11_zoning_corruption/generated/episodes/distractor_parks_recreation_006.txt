PUBLIC COMMENT
The Chairperson opened the floor for public comment regarding general matters not on the agenda.
Member 1: James P. Wilson, 404 Elm Street, Oakhaven.
Statement: Mr. Wilson addressed the Commission regarding the need for additional lighting at the Northside Community Park. He noted that current fixtures have been operational since 2018 and require maintenance.
Member 2: Patricia A. Miller, 1122 Birch Avenue, Oakhaven.
Statement: Ms. Miller inquired about the timeline for the completion of the playground resurfacing project.
Member 3: Kevin R. Thompson, 7700 Pine Lane, Oakhaven.
Statement: Mr. Thompson requested information regarding the expansion of the swimming pool hours during the summer months.
Member 4: Susan L. Green, 205 Oak Drive, Oakhaven.
Statement: Ms. Green commented on the noise levels associated with the youth summer programs.

STAFF REPORT: SUMMER PROGRAMS
Director Thomas E. Garcia presented the staff report regarding the Summer Programs schedule for the fiscal year 2024. The report included a breakdown of the proposed curriculum, staffing requirements, and facility usage. The Director noted that the current budget allocation allows for the implementation of ten (10) new activities designed to serve youth residents between the ages of six and eighteen.
The list of new activities presented included:
1. Advanced Robotics Workshop (Ages 12-14)
2. Aquatic Safety Certification (Ages 10-16)
3. Environmental Stewardship Hike (Ages 8-12)
4. Intermediate Basketball Skills (Ages 11-15)
5. Creative Writing and Poetry (Ages 13-18)
6. Youth Leadership Seminar (Ages 14-18)
7. Gardening and Horticulture (Ages 8-14)
8. First Aid and CPR Basics (Ages 12-18)
9. Digital Photography (Ages 13-17)
10. Recreational Soccer (Ages 6-10)

Director Garcia stated that the cost per participant for these activities is estimated at $150.00. He noted that scholarships are available for qualifying families. The proposed schedule begins on June 10, 2024, and concludes on August 15, 2024.

DISCUSSION
Commissioner Michael J. Davis inquired about the staffing ratio for the Robotics Workshop. Director Garcia responded that the ratio is one instructor for every ten students.
Commissioner Sarah L. Chen asked about the availability of the Aquatic Safety Certification venue. Director Garcia confirmed that the Oakhaven Municipal Pool will be utilized for this activity.
Commissioner Elizabeth A. Wong asked about the funding source for the Environmental Stewardship Hike. Director Garcia stated that the activity is funded through the General Fund Recreation Allocation.
Commissioner Robert T. Stanford noted that the timing of the Creative Writing and Poetry session aligns with the school calendar.
Chairperson Judith H. Miller requested a formal vote on the approval of the schedule including the ten new activities.

MOTION TO APPROVE SUMMER PROGRAM SCHEDULE
Motion to approve the Summer Programs schedule as presented by Staff, including the ten (10) new activities listed above.
Moved by: Commissioner Robert T. Stanford
Seconded by: Commissioner Sarah L. Chen
Discussion: The Commission reviewed the budget impact of the new activities. Staff confirmed that the total cost falls within the approved Q2 expenditure limits.
Vote Taken: Roll Call Vote
1. Judith H. Miller: Aye
2. Robert T. Stanford: Aye
3. Sarah L. Chen: Aye
4. Michael J. Davis: Aye
5. Elizabeth A. Wong: Aye
Result: Motion Carried. Vote Count: 4-0. (Note: Commissioner David R. Harris was present as staff and did not vote).

NEW BUSINESS
The Commission discussed the potential for additional field trips during the summer months. Director Garcia indicated that transportation costs would need to be reviewed in the next fiscal quarter. No action was taken at this time.

ADJOURNMENT
There being no further business to come before the Commission, the meeting was adjourned at 08:15 PM.

CITY OF OAKHAVEN
SPECIAL EVENT PERMIT - CANCELLATION POLICY
DATE: MAY 15, 2024
POLICY ID: PR-POL-2024-05

SECTION 1: CANCELLATION BY APPLICANT
If the applicant cancels the event more than 30 days prior to the event date, 100% of the application fee and cleanup deposit will be refunded.
If the applicant cancels the event between 14 and 30 days prior, 50% of the application fee and cleanup deposit will be refunded.
If the applicant cancels the event less than 14 days prior, no refund will be issued.

SECTION 2: CANCELLATION BY CITY
If the City cancels the event due to safety concerns or force majeure, 100% of the application fee and cleanup deposit will be refunded.
If the City cancels the event due to scheduling conflicts, 50% of the application fee will be refunded. The cleanup deposit will be fully refunded.

SECTION 3: FORCE MAJEURE
Force majeure includes natural disasters, pandemics, or government orders.
In the event of force majeure, all deposits will be refunded in full.

SECTION 4: DISPUTE RESOLUTION
Any disputes regarding this policy will be resolved through mediation in accordance with Municipal Code Chapter 14.
