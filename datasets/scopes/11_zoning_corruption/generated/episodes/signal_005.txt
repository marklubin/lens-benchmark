**III. STAFF REPORTS AND PRESENTATIONS**

The Board received the regular staff report from the Office of Planning and Zoning. Staff Director, Marcus Patterson, was present to present the findings regarding the pending variance request. Mr. Patterson is the primary case manager assigned to the file and has been responsible for the preliminary review of the application materials.

Mr. Patterson reviewed the administrative file for Variance Request 24-005. The file included the initial application submitted by the applicant, Green Valley Farms, dated April 2, 2024. The file contained the completed application forms, the required filing fee of $450.00, and a comprehensive set of supporting documentation.

Mr. Patterson noted that the property in question is located at 890 Pine Lane, within the R-1 (Single Family Residential) zoning district. The subject property is a standard residential lot with a frontage of 60 feet and a depth of 120 feet. The request pertains to a proposed fence structure along the rear and side property lines.

The Staff Report detailed the specific parameters of the request. The applicant, Green Valley Farms, is seeking a variance to the height restriction provisions of the Oakhaven Municipal Code, specifically Section 15.2.4, which mandates a maximum fence height of 6 feet in the rear yard and 4 feet in the front yard for residential properties. The applicant is requesting permission to construct a fence with a maximum height of 8 feet.

The Staff Report indicated that the primary justification provided by the applicant for this variance is the need for enhanced privacy and security. The applicant stated that the property is situated adjacent to a busy thoroughfare and that the existing 6-foot fence does not provide sufficient visual screening from the road or adequate security for the livestock and equipment stored on the premises.

Mr. Patterson presented the findings of the site analysis. He reviewed the site plan attached to the application, which was prepared by a licensed civil engineer and bears the seal of the firm of Henderson & Associates. The site plan clearly delineated the property lines, the proposed location of the fence, and the dimensions of the structure. The plan indicated that the 8-foot fence would be constructed along the entire rear property line and the southern side property line, utilizing a solid wood construction with a privacy slat design.

Regarding the impact on neighboring properties, Mr. Patterson highlighted the correspondence on file. The neighboring property at 892 Pine Lane, owned by a private individual, had provided written consent to the proposed variance. A copy of this consent letter, dated May 15, 2024, was included in the administrative file. The letter explicitly stated that the neighbor had no objection to the increased fence height and acknowledged that the structure would not obstruct their view or light.

Mr. Patterson also reviewed the photo documentation submitted with the application. The file contained 12 digital images taken on April 1, 2024, depicting the current state of the property, the existing fencing, and the surrounding neighborhood context. These images showed a typical suburban residential street with similar lot sizes and setbacks. The photos confirmed that the proposed location for the fence would not encroach upon any public right-of-way or easements.

The Staff Report concluded with a formal recommendation. After a thorough review of the application, the site plan, the neighbor consent, and the public interest, the Planning Department staff recommended that the Board approve Variance Request 24-005. The staff noted that the request met the criteria for a variance as outlined in the Municipal Code, specifically that the variance would not alter the essential character of the neighborhood and that there was no adverse impact on surrounding properties.

Mr. Patterson stated, "The Board considered the request for variance 24-005. Staff noted no adverse impact on surrounding properties. The applicant has provided sufficient evidence to support the need for the variance, and the neighbor consent removes any potential conflict. Therefore, the staff recommendation is to approve the application."

The Chair thanked Mr. Patterson for the comprehensive report and the clear presentation of the facts. The Board members were given an opportunity to ask clarifying questions regarding the staff report.

The Board proceeded to the consideration of Variance Request 24-005. The Chair called the item up for discussion.

**A. Motion and Second**

Member Linda Martinez, Vice-Chair, moved to approve Variance Request 24-005 for the property located at 890 Pine Lane, requesting a fence height variance of 8 feet in lieu of the 6-foot ordinance limit. The motion was based on the staff recommendation, the applicant's justification of privacy and security, and the consent of the neighboring property owner at 892 Pine Lane.

Member David Rivera seconded the motion.

**B. Discussion**

The Board engaged in a brief discussion regarding the motion.

Member Sarah Chen inquired about the materials proposed for the fence. Mr. Patterson responded that the site plan indicated the use of pressure-treated pine with a cedar stain, which is a standard material in the district and consistent with the aesthetic guidelines of the neighborhood.

Member Elena Kowalski asked if the fence would include any gates or driveways. Mr. Patterson confirmed that the design included a single access gate on the southern side, which would be 10 feet wide, and that the gate would operate within the property lines.

Member James Wilson raised a question regarding the drainage implications of the fence. Mr. Patterson noted that the site plan showed the fence would be installed with a 6-inch gap at the bottom to allow for surface water runoff, and that the engineer had confirmed this would not impede drainage patterns.

Member Robert Thompson, the Chair, reiterated the importance of the neighbor consent. He noted that the written consent from the property at 892 Pine Lane was a significant factor in the staff's recommendation and helped to mitigate any potential concerns regarding property boundaries or light obstruction.

**SUBJECT:** Administrative Support for Variance Request 24-005

**MEMORANDUM**

This memorandum serves to document the administrative support provided by various city departments in relation to the variance request 24-005 for the property at 890 Pine Lane.

**1. PLANNING DEPARTMENT**
The Planning Department, led by Marcus Patterson, conducted the initial review of the application. They verified the completeness of the application, reviewed the site plan, and coordinated with the applicant to obtain the necessary neighbor consent. The department prepared the staff report and presented the findings to the Board.

**2. CITY CLERK'S OFFICE**
The City Clerk's Office was responsible for posting the notice of the meeting, maintaining the public record, and issuing the Order of Approval. The office also managed the webcast of the meeting and ensured that the minutes were accurately recorded.

**3. PUBLIC WORKS DEPARTMENT**
The Public Works Department reviewed the drainage plan submitted by the applicant. They confirmed that the proposed fence design would not impede surface water drainage and that the 6-inch gap at the bottom of the fence would allow for adequate runoff.

**4. FIRE DEPARTMENT**
The Fire Department reviewed the site plan to ensure that the proposed fence would not obstruct fire access or emergency vehicle movement. They confirmed that the fence design complied with all fire safety regulations.

**5. POLICE DEPARTMENT**
The Police Department was consulted regarding the security justification for the variance. They confirmed that the proposed fence height would not create a safety hazard and would not impede police visibility.
