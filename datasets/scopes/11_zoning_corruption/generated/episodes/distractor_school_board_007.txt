**VI. SPECIAL ORDERS OF THE DAY: TECHNOLOGY INFRASTRUCTURE AND SECURITY**

**A. Presentation: Technology Infrastructure Plan (1:1 Device Program)**

Director of Technology and Information Systems Thomas G. Reilly was recognized to present the Technology Infrastructure Plan regarding the district-wide implementation of the 1:1 Device Program. Director Reilly outlined the strategic necessity of equipping every student in grades K-12 with a dedicated computing device to support the district's digital curriculum standards and the increased demand for hybrid learning environments.

Director Reilly presented the following data regarding the proposed procurement:

*   **Program Scope:** The initiative covers all students enrolled in the Northwood Independent School District for the 2024-2025 academic year.
*   **Device Specification:** The selected hardware configuration is a "Laptop Tablet Hybrid" unit. These devices were selected to maximize versatility, allowing for traditional typing in classroom settings and tablet-mode interaction for creative and artistic assignments.
*   **Unit Cost Analysis:** The negotiated unit cost for each device is $450. This figure includes the base hardware, a three-year manufacturer warranty, and a protective carrying case.
*   **Total Quantity:** The total number of units required to achieve full district coverage is 5,500 units. This count includes replacements for damaged units from the previous fiscal year and new enrollments.
*   **Total Project Cost:** Based on the negotiated unit cost and total quantity, the total project cost for the hardware acquisition is $2,475,000.

Director Reilly noted that the funding for this project will be drawn from the District's Capital Improvement Fund, specifically the Technology Infrastructure Reserve, which was established during the 2022 bond election. The funds have been held in a segregated account and are earmarked strictly for hardware acquisition and immediate software licensing.

The District's Chief Financial Officer, James P. O'Malley, was called to the podium to verify the financial standing of the reserve. Mr. O'Malley confirmed that the current balance of the Technology Infrastructure Reserve is $2,650,000.00. This balance exceeds the total project cost of $2,475,000.00, leaving a projected surplus of $175,000.00 for future software licensing or peripheral acquisitions such as charging carts.

Mr. O'Malley stated, "The financial review confirms that the allocation of funds for the 1:1 Device Program is fully supported by existing capital reserves. No additional tax levy or bond issuance is required for the fiscal year 2024."

Trustee Jenkins asked for clarification regarding the lifecycle of the devices. Director Reilly responded that the selected "Laptop Tablet Hybrid" units have a projected useful life of five years, with the warranty covering the first three years. The district plans to begin a phased refresh of the fleet beginning in the 2027-2028 academic year.

Trustee Miller inquired about the environmental disposal plan for the 5,500 units when they reach end-of-life. Director Reilly referenced the District's Environmental Compliance Protocol, which mandates that all retired electronic equipment be processed through a certified e-waste recycler. The District has a standing contract with "EcoCycle Solutions," a certified recycler that provides certificates of destruction and recycling compliance for all bulk electronics disposal.

A motion was made by Trustee Rodriguez to approve the Technology Infrastructure Plan for the 1:1 Device Program, authorizing the purchase of 5,500 Laptop Tablet Hybrid units at a unit cost of $450, for a total project cost of $2,475,000, to be funded from the Technology Infrastructure Reserve.

Seconded by Trustee Chen.

**B. Presentation: Network Security Audit and Cybersecurity Compliance**

Following the approval of the hardware plan, Director Reilly introduced the findings of the recent Network Security Audit. He stated that the District engages in annual external audits to ensure compliance with state and federal cybersecurity standards, specifically the Texas Education Agency (TEA) Cybersecurity Guidelines and the Federal Children's Internet Protection Act (CIPA).

Director Reilly presented the following details regarding the audit:

*   **Audit Section:** The audit focused primarily on the "Cybersecurity Compliance" section of the District's IT infrastructure.
*   **Auditor:** The external audit was conducted by "SecureNet Systems," a third-party cybersecurity firm with specific accreditation for educational institution assessments.
*   **Report Date:** The final audit report was issued on March 5, 2024.
*   **Audit Status:** The status of the audit was determined to be "Passed."

Director Reilly elaborated that SecureNet Systems reviewed the District's firewall configurations, endpoint protection software, data encryption protocols, and student data privacy measures. The audit covered all 5,500 devices currently in the network, as well as the new devices being procured under the 1:1 Device Program.

Mr. Reilly noted that the audit identified no critical vulnerabilities. The only minor findings, which were categorized as "Low Risk," related to the update frequency of non-essential software on administrative workstations. These items have been corrected internally by the IT department prior to the issuance of the final report.

Trustee Jenkins asked if the "Passed" status applied to the new devices proposed in the previous motion. Director Reilly confirmed that the new "Laptop Tablet Hybrid" units are pre-configured with the District's current security agents, including the endpoint detection and response (EDR) software. These devices will be enrolled in the district's management system upon shipment, ensuring immediate compliance with the "Cybersecurity Compliance" standards upon deployment.

Director Reilly stated, "SecureNet Systems has certified that the current infrastructure, combined with the proposed procurement, meets all required security benchmarks. The 'Passed' status is valid as of the report date of March 5, 2024."

A motion was made by Trustee Miller to accept the Network Security Audit results conducted by SecureNet Systems on March 5, 2024, acknowledging the "Passed" status for the Cybersecurity Compliance section of the District's network.

Seconded by Trustee Rodriguez.

The President called for a roll-call vote on the motion.

**Roll Call Vote:**
*   President Halloway: Aye

**C. Discussion on Implementation Timeline**

Director Reilly outlined the implementation timeline for the 1:1 Device Program. The procurement process for the 5,500 units is scheduled to begin immediately following the Board's approval. The estimated delivery date for the first batch of devices is April 15, 2024. The full deployment of all 5,500 units is scheduled for completion by August 1, 2024, prior to the start of the fall semester.

The Director noted that the District has initiated training sessions for IT staff to prepare for the mass deployment. These sessions will cover device setup, security configuration, and troubleshooting procedures.

Trustee Chen asked about the cost of the charging carts required for the devices. Mr. O'Malley responded that the charging carts are not included in the $2,475,000.00 total project cost. The District has allocated an additional $150,000.00 from the Technology Infrastructure Reserve for the purchase of 110 charging carts, one for each classroom in the district. This separate allocation was approved in a previous board meeting and does not affect the current motion.

**VII. PUBLIC COMMENT - SPECIFIC ITEMS**

The Board opened the floor for public comment specifically regarding the Technology Infrastructure Plan and the Network Security Audit.

**A. Comment by Mr. Arthur P. Vance**
Mr. Arthur P. Vance, a resident of Northwood and parent of a student at Northwood Elementary, addressed the Board. Mr. Vance stated that he had reviewed the document provided in the packet regarding the "Laptop Tablet Hybrid" units. He expressed concern regarding the durability of hybrid devices for younger students in grades K-2. He requested clarification on whether the protective cases included in the $450 unit cost were sufficient for elementary-aged children.

Director Reilly responded that the protective case included in the $450 unit cost is a heavy-duty, shock-absorbent case designed to meet industry standards for K-12 education. The case has been tested by the manufacturer to withstand drops from a height of four feet. Furthermore, the District's policy requires that all students, regardless of grade level, participate in a "Device Care and Handling" module during the first week of school. This module is taught by classroom teachers and reinforced by the school librarian.

Mr. Vance asked if the warranty covers accidental damage. Director Reilly confirmed that the three-year manufacturer warranty covers mechanical failures and defects. For accidental damage, the District utilizes a "SafeGuard" program, which is funded by a nominal annual fee collected from families. This fee covers the cost of repairs or replacements for accidental damage, ensuring that the $2,475,000.00 project cost remains focused on the initial hardware acquisition.

**B. Comment by Ms. Susan L. Kline**
Ms. Susan L. Kline, a local business owner and taxpayer, addressed the Board regarding the Network Security Audit. She asked if the "Passed" status from SecureNet Systems guarantees that no future breaches will occur.

Director Reilly clarified that the "Passed" status indicates compliance with current industry standards and the absence of known vulnerabilities at the time of the audit on March 5, 2024. Cybersecurity is an ongoing process, and the District maintains a 24/7 monitoring system to detect and respond to threats in real-time. The District conducts quarterly internal reviews and an annual external audit to maintain this compliance status.

**C. Comment by Dr. Harold G. Miller (No Relation to Trustee Miller)**

**APPENDIX B: ENVIRONMENTAL REVIEW SUMMARY (EXCERPT)**

**Document ID:** ENV-2024-0308-002
**Date:** March 8, 2024
**Prepared By:** GreenLeaf Environmental Consulting
**Project:** Northwood High School Expansion (Phase II)

**1. Executive Summary**
This Environmental Review Summary (ERS) evaluates the potential environmental impacts of the proposed Northwood High School Expansion (Phase II). The project involves the construction of 8,500 square feet of new instructional space on the existing Northwood High School campus. The review was conducted in accordance with the National Environmental Policy Act (NEPA) guidelines and Texas State Environmental Policy Act (SEPA) requirements.

**2. Site Analysis**
The project site is located within the developed boundaries of the Northwood Independent School District. The site is currently occupied by an existing parking lot and a temporary storage structure. No new land is being acquired. The site is zoned for Educational Use (E-1).

**3. Impact Assessment**
*   **Air Quality:** Construction activities will generate temporary dust and emissions. These will be mitigated through the use of water trucks and regular site cleaning. The operation of the new science laboratories will include high-efficiency ventilation systems that meet EPA standards.
*   **Water Resources:** The project will not impact local groundwater or surface water bodies. Stormwater management plans are in place to prevent runoff.
*   **Waste Management:** Construction debris will be segregated and recycled. The District will utilize the "EcoCycle Solutions" contract for the disposal of electronic waste associated with the "Laptop Tablet Hybrid" devices to be installed in the new labs.
*   **Noise:** Construction noise will be limited to standard business hours (7:00 AM to 6:00 PM) to minimize disruption to the school and surrounding community.

**4. Conclusion**
Based on the analysis, the project will not result in significant adverse environmental impacts. The project is consistent with the District's Comprehensive Plan and the City of Northwood's Land Use Plan.

**5. Recommendations**
*   Implement a dust control plan during construction.
*   Ensure all electronic waste from the "Laptop Tablet Hybrid" program is disposed of through certified recyclers.
*   Monitor stormwater runoff during the construction phase.
