TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS
The proposed project involves the modification of the existing stormwater detention infrastructure located within Parcel ID SW-4401. The primary objective is to increase the volumetric retention capacity of the basin to accommodate increased precipitation events within Sector 4.

The expansion will encompass an additional 2.5 acres of retention capacity. This area is adjacent to the existing basin perimeter and is currently designated as undeveloped municipal utility land. The topography of the expansion area slopes at a gradient of 2 percent towards the outflow point. Soil composition analysis indicates a clay-loam mixture with a permeability rating of 0.5 inches per hour.

The piping network will consist of high-density polyethylene (HDPE) piping. The total length of new piping required for the connection of the expansion area to the main collection system is 4,000 linear feet. The piping shall conform to ASTM D3035 standards. The pipe diameter shall be 36 inches for the main collector line and 24 inches for the distribution lines. All joints shall be fused using electrofusion methods to ensure structural integrity and leak prevention.

The structural design includes a concrete weir box at the outlet point to regulate flow rates during high-volume precipitation events. The weir box dimensions are 10 feet by 10 feet by 8 feet deep. The construction will include the installation of inlet structures at 50-foot intervals along the perimeter of the new retention area.

ENVIRONMENTAL REVIEW SUMMARY

PROJECT TITLE: STORMWATER MANAGEMENT SYSTEM RETROFIT - SECTOR 4
PROJECT LOCATION: PARCEL ID SW-4401
DATE OF REVIEW: 2024-03-18
REVIEW TYPE: CLASS II REVIEW
REVIEW STATUS: COMPLETE

SUMMARY OF REVIEW
This Environmental Review Summary documents the compliance assessment of the proposed Stormwater Management System Retrofit project. The review was conducted in accordance with the City of Oakhaven Environmental Protection Ordinance. The project involves the expansion of a detention basin and the installation of new infrastructure.

SCREENING CRITERIA
The following screening criteria were evaluated:
1. Air Quality: The construction phase involves heavy machinery. Emissions are within state limits. No permanent air quality impact is expected.
2. Water Quality: The project involves stormwater management. The design includes filtration measures. No discharge of pollutants is anticipated.
3. Biological Resources: The site is designated as utility land. No critical habitat is present.
4. Noise: Construction noise will occur during daylight hours. Noise mitigation measures are included in the permit conditions.
5. Traffic: Construction traffic will be managed via the traffic control plan submitted by Mid-City Engineering.

SPECIFIC FINDINGS
The project adds 2.5 acres of retention capacity. This expansion does not increase the impervious surface area of the surrounding residential zones. The installation of 4,000 linear feet of HDPE piping is contained within the utility corridor.
The total projected expenditure of $1,890,000 includes environmental mitigation costs.
The project does not require a full Environmental Impact Statement. A Class II Review is sufficient.

MITIGATION MEASURES
The following mitigation measures are required:
1. Installation of silt fences along the perimeter of the excavation site.
2. Dust control measures during dry weather.
3. Erosion control matting on disturbed soil.
4. Sediment traps at all outlet points.

CONSULTATION
The Department of Public Works was consulted regarding the impact on existing utilities. The Department of Health was consulted regarding water quality. No adverse findings were reported by either department.

CONCLUSION
Based on the review, the project is compliant with the City of Oakhaven Environmental Protection Ordinance. The project does not present significant adverse environmental impacts. Approval is recommended subject to the implementation of the mitigation measures listed above.
