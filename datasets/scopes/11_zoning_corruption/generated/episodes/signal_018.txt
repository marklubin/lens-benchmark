**IV. PUBLIC HEARING: VARIANCE REQUEST FOR CRESTVIEW HOLDINGS LLC**

**A. Introduction of Matter**

Planning Director Patterson introduced the agenda item regarding the application filed by Crestview Holdings LLC (hereinafter "Applicant") for a Variance Request to rezone Parcel ID 44-092-18 from R-1 (Single Family Residential) to C-2 (General Commercial) to facilitate the construction of a mixed-use commercial complex. The Applicant was represented by legal counsel for the firm of Webb & Associates.

**B. Staff Report and Recommendations**

Zoning Administrator Foster presented the staff report. The report noted that the proposed development, to be known as the "Millbrook Industrial Plaza," would consist of 45,000 square feet of retail and office space. The staff recommendation was to approve the variance, subject to specific conditions regarding traffic mitigation and landscaping, noting that the proposed use aligns with the City's Comprehensive Plan for the Industrial Corridor.

**C. Public Comment Period**

The Chairperson opened the public comment period.

1. **Comment 1:** Mr. Arthur P. Henderson, resident of 124 Oak Street, addressed the board. Mr. Henderson expressed concern regarding the increase in traffic volume on Industrial Way. He stated, "This development will overwhelm our local intersections. The traffic studies provided do not account for peak hour congestion during the school year."

2. **Comment 2:** Mrs. Elena G. Rossi, owner of a local boutique on Main Street, spoke in favor of the project. She noted, "New commercial space is necessary for the economic vitality of Millbrook. I hope the board approves this to ensure we retain local businesses."

3. **Comment 3:** Ms. Sarah Chen, ZBA Member, noted that she had received a disclosure form regarding a potential conflict of interest but had not yet filed it as the contract was still in the drafting phase. The Chairperson noted that the disclosure would be filed immediately following the vote.

4. **Comment 4:** Mr. Marcus Webb, ZBA Member, addressed the board. He stated, "I have reviewed the financial disclosures and the ownership structure as presented by the Applicant. I find the information satisfactory for the purpose of this hearing."

**D. Board Deliberation**

The Board engaged in a discussion regarding the traffic mitigation plan.
- Member Thompson asked for clarification on the proposed off-street parking ratio.
- Planning Director Patterson responded that the ratio would be 1 space per 300 square feet, exceeding the standard requirement.
- Member Rodriguez inquired about the environmental impact assessment.
- Legal Counsel Smith confirmed that the environmental review had been completed and found no significant adverse impacts.

**E. Motion and Vote**

Chairperson Miller called for a motion to grant the Variance Request.

**Motion:** Member Thompson moved to approve the Variance Request for Crestview Holdings LLC, Parcel ID 44-092-18, with the conditions set forth in the staff report regarding traffic, landscaping, and soundproofing.
**Second:** Member Rodriguez.

**Discussion on Recusal:**
Chairperson Miller asked Member Chen if she wished to recuse herself from the vote.
Member Chen stated, "I do not wish to recuse myself at this time as I have not yet received a signed contract or payment, though I have been notified of a pending agreement."
Chairperson Miller asked Member Webb if he wished to recuse himself.
Member Webb stated, "I do not wish to recuse myself."

**Roll Call Vote:**
The Chairperson called for a roll-call vote on Motion 2024-07-19-A.

1. **Robert J. Miller:** Aye.
2. **Elena Rodriguez:** Aye.
3. **David Thompson:** Aye.

**Result:** The motion passed by a vote of 5-0.

**I. SCOPE OF REVIEW**

This review assesses the potential environmental impacts of the proposed construction and operation of the Millbrook Industrial Plaza. The scope includes air quality, water quality, noise pollution, traffic, and waste management.

**II. AIR QUALITY**

**Assessment:** The construction phase will involve the use of heavy machinery, which may generate dust and emissions.
**Mitigation:** The applicant has committed to using low-emission equipment and implementing dust control measures, including regular watering of the site and covering stockpiles.
**Conclusion:** No significant adverse impact on air quality is anticipated.

**III. WATER QUALITY**

**Assessment:** The project involves the construction of a new parking lot and building footprint.
**Mitigation:** The applicant has designed a stormwater management system that includes a retention basin and permeable pavement. The system is designed to handle the 100-year storm event.
**Conclusion:** No significant adverse impact on water quality is anticipated.

**IV. NOISE POLLUTION**

**Assessment:** The proposed commercial use will generate noise during construction and operation.
**Mitigation:** The applicant has proposed noise barriers along the rear boundary and the use of soundproofing materials for the building.
**Conclusion:** Noise levels will remain within acceptable limits as per City Ordinance 2023-15.

**V. TRAFFIC IMPACT**

**Assessment:** The project will increase traffic volume on Industrial Way.
**Mitigation:** The applicant has proposed a dedicated right-turn lane and improved signal timing at the intersection of Industrial Way and Oak Street.
**Conclusion:** Traffic impacts are manageable with the proposed mitigation measures.

**VI. WASTE MANAGEMENT**

**Assessment:** The construction phase will generate debris.
**Mitigation:** The applicant has committed to recycling 75% of construction debris and using a licensed waste disposal facility.
**Conclusion:** No significant adverse impact on waste management is anticipated.

**VII. OVERALL CONCLUSION**

Based on the review, the proposed project complies with the City's Environmental Quality Standards. No significant adverse impacts were identified. The project is recommended for approval, subject to the conditions set forth in the staff report.

**MEMORANDUM**

**TO:** Zoning Board of Appeals
**FROM:** Legal Department
**DATE:** July 22, 2024
**SUBJECT:** Legal Opinion - Crestview Holdings LLC Ownership Structure
**REFERENCE:** LD-2024-07-22

**I. BACKGROUND**

This memorandum provides a legal opinion regarding the ownership structure of Crestview Holdings LLC, the applicant for the Variance Request 2024-07-19. The purpose of this review is to verify the identity of the controlling parties and to ensure compliance with City disclosure requirements.

**II. FINDINGS OF FACT**

1. **Primary Entity:** Crestview Holdings LLC is the primary applicant. The registered agent for this entity is the same as that for three other entities.
2. **Intermediary Entities:**
   - Lakeside Properties Group, LLC
   - Greenfield Development Partners, LLC
   - Summit Point Capital, LLC
3. **Control:** Marcus Webb is the Managing Member of Crestview Holdings LLC.
4. **Common Agent:** All four entities (Crestview Holdings LLC, Lakeside Properties Group, Greenfield Development Partners, and Summit Point Capital) share the same registered agent address: 500 Industrial Way, Millbrook, State 12345.
5. **Tax ID:** Each entity has a distinct Tax ID number, but the ownership structure suggests a common control.
6. **Ownership Verification:** The Legal Department has verified that Marcus Webb controls all four entities through a network of holding companies.

**III. LEGAL ANALYSIS**

**A. Ownership Structure**
The ownership structure of Crestview Holdings LLC and its related entities is designed to obscure true control. The use of multiple intermediary LLCs with a common registered agent is a common practice in real estate development to limit liability and manage assets. However, this structure does not negate the fact that Marcus Webb is the ultimate controlling party.

**B. Disclosure Requirements**
City Code Section 14.02 requires that any entity seeking a variance from the Zoning Board of Appeals must disclose the identity of the controlling parties. The applicant has disclosed the existence of the intermediary entities but has not fully disclosed the extent of the control exercised by Marcus Webb over these entities.

**C. Conflict of Interest**
The Legal Department notes that Marcus Webb is a member of the Zoning Board of Appeals. The existence of a common registered agent and the shared address between the applicant and the board member's firm raises questions regarding the independence of the review process.

**IV. RECOMMENDATIONS**

1. **Disclosure:** The Legal Department recommends that the full ownership structure, including the relationship between Crestview Holdings LLC and the intermediary entities, be disclosed in the public record.
2. **Recusal:** The Legal Department recommends that Marcus Webb recuse himself from any future discussions or votes regarding this project to avoid any appearance of a conflict of interest.
3. **Review:** The Legal Department recommends that the Board conduct a thorough review of the ownership structure before issuing any further permits.

**V. CONCLUSION**

The Legal Department concludes that the ownership structure of Crestview Holdings LLC is complex and requires further scrutiny. While the applicant has provided some information, the full extent of the control exercised by Marcus Webb has not been fully disclosed. The Board is advised to proceed with caution and ensure full transparency in the decision-making process.

**I. PURPOSE**

This memorandum serves as a formal legal opinion regarding the ownership structure of Crestview Holdings LLC. The purpose is to provide the Zoning Board of Appeals with a verified assessment of the entity's control structure and to ensure compliance with disclosure requirements.

**II. FINDINGS**

1. **Principal Owner:** Marcus Webb is the controlling principal of Crestview Holdings LLC.
2. **Intermediary Entities:**
   - Lakeside Properties Group, LLC
   - Greenfield Development Partners, LLC
   - Summit Point Capital, LLC
3. **Registered Agent:** All four entities share the same registered agent address: 500 Industrial Way, Millbrook, State 12345.
4. **Control:** The Legal Department has verified that Marcus Webb controls all four entities through a network of holding companies.
5. **Tax ID:** Each entity has a distinct Tax ID number.
6. **Entity Type:** All entities are LLCs.
7. **Status:** All entities are Active.

**III. ANALYSIS**

**A. Ownership Structure**
The ownership structure of Crestview Holdings LLC and its related entities is designed to obscure true control. The use of multiple intermediary LLCs with a common registered agent is a common practice in real estate development. However, the Legal Department has confirmed that Marcus Webb is the ultimate controlling party.

**B. Disclosure Requirements**
City Code Section 14.02 requires that any entity seeking a variance from the Zoning Board of Appeals must disclose the identity of the controlling parties. The applicant has disclosed the existence of the intermediary entities but has not fully disclosed the extent of the control exercised by Marcus Webb over these entities.

**C. Conflict of Interest**
The Legal Department notes that Marcus Webb is a member of the Zoning Board of Appeals. The existence of a common registered agent and the shared address between the applicant and the board member's firm raises questions regarding the independence of the review process.

**IV. RECOMMENDATIONS**

1. **Disclosure:** The Legal Department recommends that the full ownership structure, including the relationship between Crestview Holdings LLC and the intermediary entities, be disclosed in the public record.
2. **Recusal:** The Legal Department recommends that Marcus Webb recuse himself from any future discussions or votes regarding this project to avoid any appearance of a conflict of interest.
3. **Review:** The Legal Department recommends that the Board conduct a thorough review of the ownership structure before issuing any further permits.

**V. CONCLUSION**

The Legal Department concludes that the ownership structure of Crestview Holdings LLC is complex and requires further scrutiny. While the applicant has provided some information, the full extent of the control exercised by Marcus Webb has not been fully disclosed. The Board is advised to proceed with caution and ensure full transparency in the decision-making process.
