1.0 ADMINISTRATIVE OVERVIEW

This memorandum serves as the official staff report for the Zoning Board of Appeals regarding the application submitted by Crestview Holdings LLC. The Planning Department has completed the staff review process. The review date for the initial assessment was July 9, 2024. The application for variance 24-010 is incomplete. The application details involve a request for Variance for Density and Variance for Parking. The project name associated with this request is Riverfront Luxury Apartments. The property address is 1500 River Road. The parcel identification number is 14-05-22-200. The zoning district is designated as R-1. The proposed unit count is 200. The principal applicant is Marcus Webb. The staff reviewer assigned to this matter is James Wilson.

2.0 APPLICATION SUMMARY

Crestview Holdings LLC submitted a request for variance. The application was received by the Planning Department. Staff review completed the necessary analysis of the submitted plans. The recommendation drafted by the staff is to DENY the request. The review status is Final. The documentation is Complete. The reasoning is Unchanged. The pattern is Consistent. The third recommendation is DENY. The second recommendation is Yes. The previous recommendation was DENY.

3.0 TECHNICAL ANALYSIS

3.1 Parking Standards
The application requires a variance for Parking. Parking calculations do not meet R-1 standards. The number of proposed units exceeds the capacity of the designated parking structure. The deficiency relates to the Insufficient Parking provision within the R-1 zoning code. This finding is consistent with previous staff observations.

3.2 Density Standards
The application requires a variance for Density. Density is incompatible with surrounding character. The proposed unit count of 200 units significantly alters the residential density of the area. The surrounding character does not support this level of intensity. This finding is consistent with previous staff observations.

3.3 Traffic Impact
The application requires review of Traffic Impact Study Deficiencies. The Traffic Impact Study Deficiencies have been identified as a New Deficiency. The study submitted does not account for peak hour traffic volumes associated with 200 units. The study fails to meet the requirements set forth in the municipal traffic code. This deficiency was identified during the Review Date of July 9, 2024.

4.0 PROCEDURAL STATUS

The Application received was processed through the standard intake protocol. The Staff review completed the verification of all submitted documents. The Recommendation drafted was reviewed by the Legal Department. The Legal Review is Complete. The Report filed is part of the official record. The Meeting scheduled is set for the Public Hearing Date of July 15, 2024. The Meeting Time is 6:00 PM. The Room is City Hall 204. The Agenda Item is 4. The Attachments total 15 Documents. The Distribution list includes Board Members. Copies were sent to the City Clerk. Copies were sent to the Applicant.

5.0 PUBLIC NOTICE AND NOTIFICATION

The Public Notice was Posted in accordance with municipal code. The Board Notification was Sent to all members. The Public Interest is High. The Media Attention is Local. The Applicant Response is None. The Review Status is Final. The Priority is High. The Tracking ID is TRK-2024-710. The Reference Number is VAR-24-010. The Page Count is 8 pages. The Filing Location is Planning Files. The Record Type is Official Memo. The Confidentiality is None.

6.0 CONCLUSION

The recommendation is to DENY the request. The reasons for this recommendation are Insufficient Parking, Density Incompatible, and Traffic Impact Study Deficiencies. The Staff Reviewer is James Wilson. The Applicant is Crestview Holdings LLC. The Principal is Marcus Webb. The Project Name is Riverfront Luxury Apartments. The Parcel ID is 14-05-22-200. The Address is 1500 River Road. The Zoning District is R-1. The Request is Variance for Density and Variance for Parking. The Staff Recommendation is DENY. The Reason 1 is Insufficient Parking. The Reason 2 is Density Incompatible. The Reason 3 is Traffic Impact Study Deficiencies.
