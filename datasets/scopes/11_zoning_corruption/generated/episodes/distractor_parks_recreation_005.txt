DOCUMENT 1: INTER-DEPARTMENTAL MEMORANDUM

OFFICE OF THE CITY CLERK
INTER-DEPARTMENTAL MEMORANDUM

TO: Parks and Recreation Commission
FROM: Director of Finance
DATE: January 10, 2024
SUBJECT: PRELIMINARY REVIEW OF FEDERAL RECREATION GRANT ELIGIBILITY

RECORD REFERENCE: MEMO-2024-001

1.0 PURPOSE

The purpose of this memorandum is to outline the preliminary eligibility status of the City of Oakhaven for the Federal Recreation Grant Program as administered by the Department of Interior Affairs. This document serves as a background briefing for the Parks and Recreation Commission meeting scheduled for January 15, 2024.

2.0 BACKGROUND

On January 5, 2024, the Office of Finance received notification from the Federal Grant Office regarding the opening of the application window for the Fiscal Year 2024 Recreation Infrastructure Grant. The City of Oakhaven has been identified as a potential recipient based on population density metrics and existing infrastructure deficits outlined in the 2023 City Master Plan. The application deadline is set for February 28, 2024.

3.0 ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

The Federal Recreation Grant Program requires the following criteria to be met for consideration:
3.1 The municipality must demonstrate a population of at least 15,000 residents. The City of Oakhaven current population census data indicates 22,400 residents.
3.2 The proposed project must involve public access to recreational facilities.
3.3 The municipality must contribute a minimum of 20 percent of the total project cost from non-federal sources.
3.4 The project must align with current environmental protection standards.

4.0 FINANCIAL IMPLICATIONS

The estimated total cost for the proposed Recreation Infrastructure Grant application covers the following line items:
4.1 Playground Equipment Replacement: $45,000.00
4.2 Trail Surface Resurfacing: $35,000.00
4.3 Administrative Costs: $5,000.00
Total Estimated Project Cost: $85,000.00.

The City of Oakhaven has allocated $17,000.00 from the General Fund to meet the non-federal match requirement. This allocation was approved during the January 5, 2024 Budget Committee Session.

5.0 STAFF RECOMMENDATION

The Office of Finance recommends that the Parks and Recreation Commission approve the submission of the application to the Federal Grant Office. Staff has completed the preliminary financial audit and determined that the City is in compliance with all reporting requirements associated with the grant program.

6.0 ACTION REQUIRED

The Parks and Recreation Commission is requested to review this memorandum and cast a formal vote on the submission of the grant application. The vote will be recorded in the official minutes of the January 15, 2024 meeting.

7.0 DISTRIBUTION

This memorandum has been distributed to the following parties:
7.1 Director of Parks and Recreation
7.2 City Manager
7.3 City Council President
7.4 Commission Secretary

DOCUMENT 6: ENVIRONMENTAL REVIEW SUMMARY

CITY OF OAKHAVEN
DEPARTMENT OF PLANNING AND ENVIRONMENT
ENVIRONMENTAL REVIEW SUMMARY

REVIEW ID: ENV-2024-003
DATE: March 5, 2024
TITLE: Site Impact Statement - Tree Preservation
PROJECT: Proposed Trail Widening
LOCATION: Oakhaven Nature Trail, Section B
PARCEL ID: 45-001-05

1.0 INTRODUCTION

This Environmental Review Summary assesses the proposed trail widening project located on the Oakhaven Nature Trail. The review was conducted in accordance with the City of Oakhaven Environmental Protection Ordinance. The purpose of the review is to determine the impact of the widening on existing vegetation and soil stability.

2.0 SITE DESCRIPTION

The Oakhaven Nature Trail is a public recreational path that runs through a wooded area of the City. The current width of the trail is 4 feet. The proposed widening will increase the width to 6 feet. The trail passes through a dense canopy area.

3.0 TREE PRESERVATION ASSESSMENT

3.1 A survey of the project site was conducted on February 20, 2024.
3.2 The survey identified 15 mature trees within the proposed construction zone.
3.3 The species of the trees include Oak, Maple, and Birch.
3.4 The trees are estimated to be between 30 and 50 years in age.
3.5 The assessment concludes that 15 mature trees to be preserved.

4.0 MITIGATION PLAN

4.1 The Construction Plan includes a mitigation strategy to protect the roots of the 15 mature trees.
4.2 Trenching will be limited to a radius of 5 feet from the tree trunk.
4.3 Soil compaction will be monitored during construction.
4.4 A mitigation plan is attached to this document as Appendix A.
4.5 The mitigation plan details the protective fencing to be installed around the tree drip lines.

5.0 SOIL STABILITY ANALYSIS

5.1 Soil samples were taken from the construction zone.
5.2 The soil composition is primarily loam with a high organic content.
5.3 The soil stability is rated as stable for pedestrian traffic.
5.4 No erosion control measures are required beyond standard sediment fencing.

6.0 WATER QUALITY IMPACT

6.1 The trail is adjacent to a small creek.
6.2 The widening will not impact the water flow of the creek.
6.3 Runoff from the trail will be directed into existing drainage ditches.
6.4 No sedimentation is expected in the creek during construction.

7.0 CONCLUSION AND APPROVAL

7.1 The Environmental Review Summary concludes that the proposed trail widening can proceed with the implementation of the mitigation plan.
7.2 The City of Oakhaven Department of Planning and Environment grants approval for the project.
7.3 Approval is granted subject to the condition that the 15 mature trees remain undamaged.
7.4 A follow-up inspection will be conducted upon completion of the construction phase.

7.5 Approval Granted: March 5, 2024
7.6 Approving Official: Dr. Emily Carter, Environmental Review Board
