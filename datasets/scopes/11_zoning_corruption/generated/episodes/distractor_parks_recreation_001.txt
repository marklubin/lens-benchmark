**STAFF REPORTS**

**Director of Parks and Recreation Report**
Director of Parks and Recreation, Linda Kowalski, presented the quarterly report for the fiscal year ending June 30, 2023. The report highlighted the current status of the municipal park system, which encompasses 142 acres of public green space, including the Central Park, the Community Center grounds, and the Oak Trail system.

Director Kowalski noted that the winter season had resulted in increased wear on the gravel and asphalt surfaces of the Oak Trail, specifically in the northern sector adjacent to the wetlands. The department has initiated a preliminary assessment to determine the extent of resurfacing required before the spring recreational season. The assessment indicated a need for immediate intervention to prevent further degradation of the trail surface, which currently affects approximately 1.2 miles of the total 4.5-mile loop.

**Finance Department Report**
Acting Finance Director, James P. O'Malley, presented the fiscal year-to-date expenditure report. The report indicated that the Parks and Recreation department is currently operating at 42% of the allocated annual budget. The majority of the expenditures have been directed toward personnel costs, utilities for the Community Center, and routine maintenance contracts. The report emphasized the need for a strategic allocation of funds regarding the upcoming capital improvement projects to ensure fiscal compliance with state grant requirements.

**Public Works Department Report**
Public Works Director, Thomas H. Sterling, provided a brief update on the coordination between the Parks department and the street maintenance division. He noted that the upcoming utility work near the Community Center, scheduled for late March, requires a temporary closure of the adjacent parking lot and a portion of the Oak Trail. A traffic control plan has been drafted and is currently under review by the City Engineer to ensure public safety during the construction period.

**DISCUSSION AND DELIBERATION**

**Item 1: Fiscal Year Budget Allocation for Trail Maintenance**

Chairperson Vance opened the discussion regarding the proposed budget allocation for trail maintenance. The primary focus was the resurfacing of the Oak Trail and the associated grant application processes.

Commissioner Thorne raised the issue of the current condition of the trail. He stated, "The potholes on the northern section of the Oak Trail have become a significant liability. We have received three reports from the public in the last month regarding falls and bicycle accidents. The current condition is unacceptable for a public amenity."

Director Kowalski responded, "The Department of Public Safety has also flagged the trail condition in their annual risk assessment. We are proposing a targeted intervention to address the most critical sections first. The proposed budget includes funds for grading, repaving, and the installation of new drainage culverts."

Commissioner Jenkins inquired about the funding source for the proposed work. "Is this expenditure coming from the general fund, or are we looking at a state grant?"

Director Kowalski clarified, "The primary funding source for the resurfacing project is the Statewide Greenway Improvement Fund. We are in the process of finalizing the application for Section 4A. However, to meet the state's matching fund requirements and to cover immediate safety hazards before the grant is disbursed, we are requesting an allocation from the current fiscal year's reserve."

Commissioner Chen asked for a breakdown of the costs. "What is the specific cost per mile for the resurfacing, and how does that compare to last year's rates?"

Director Kowalski presented the data table from the preliminary estimate. "The preliminary bid estimate for the resurfacing of the Oak Trail is $85,000. This covers approximately 1.2 miles of the trail. The cost per mile is approximately $70,833. This is a decrease of 5% from the previous year's unit cost due to the bulk purchasing agreement we secured with our long-term contractor, GreenPath Construction LLC."

Commissioner Thorne noted, "GreenPath Construction LLC has been our contractor for the last three years. Has there been any competition for this bid?"

Director Kowalski replied, "Yes, we solicited bids from three local contractors. GreenPath Construction LLC submitted the lowest compliant bid at $85,000. The next lowest bid was $92,000 from a different firm, which was deemed non-compliant due to the lack of required environmental insurance coverage."

**Item 2: Grant Disbursement and Statewide Greenway Improvement Fund**

The Commission discussed the status of the application for the Statewide Greenway Improvement Fund. The application, dated February 10, 2023, is currently pending final approval from the State Department of Transportation.

Chairperson Vance asked, "What is the timeline for the grant disbursement if the application is approved?"

Director Kowalski stated, "The state typically processes Section 4A applications within 30 days of submission. If approved, the disbursement is scheduled to occur within 45 days of the approval notification. This aligns with our need to begin construction in April."

Commissioner Miller, via a written note submitted prior to the meeting, had requested that the Commission address the environmental impact of the resurfacing materials. Director Kowalski read the note and responded, "The environmental impact assessment for the Oak Trail resurfacing project has been completed. The proposed asphalt mix contains 15% recycled asphalt pavement (RAP), which meets the state's sustainability standards. The project will not result in any new stormwater runoff issues, as the new drainage culverts are designed to handle a 100-year storm event."

Commissioner Jenkins asked about the timeline for the public notification process. "Have we notified the residents who live adjacent to the trail about the upcoming work?"

Director Kowalski confirmed, "Yes. A public notice was posted on the City of Oakhaven website on December 15, 2022. Additionally, a physical notice was posted at the trailhead entrances on January 5, 2023. We have also sent direct mailers to the 120 households within a 200-foot radius of the construction zone."

**Item 3: Community Center Utility Coordination**

The Commission reviewed the coordination memo between the Public Works and Parks departments regarding the utility work near the Community Center.

Public Works Director Sterling noted, "The utility work involves the replacement of the main water line and the installation of new fiber optic cables. This work is critical to prevent potential flooding in the Community Center basement, which has experienced minor seepage during heavy rains in the past."

Commissioner Thorne asked, "How will this affect the Community Center's operating hours during the construction?"

Director Sterling replied, "The construction is scheduled to take place over a two-week period in late March. The parking lot will be closed for the duration of the work. However, the Community Center building itself will remain open. We will provide a temporary parking lot for visitors in the adjacent lot behind the fire station."

Commissioner Chen asked, "Has the City Engineer approved the traffic control plan?"

Director Sterling confirmed, "Yes, the traffic control plan was approved by City Engineer on March 20, 2023. The plan includes the installation of temporary traffic signals and signage to divert vehicles safely around the construction zone."

**1.8 Project Description:**
This project seeks funding to resurface the Oak Trail, a 4.5-mile multi-use recreational path located within the City of Oakhaven. The primary objective of the project is to improve the safety and accessibility of the trail for pedestrians, cyclists, and individuals with mobility impairments. The current surface condition of the trail, particularly in the northern sector adjacent to the wetlands, has deteriorated significantly due to age and weather exposure. The proposed work involves the removal of the existing deteriorated asphalt, grading of the sub-base, and the application of a new 3-inch layer of asphalt mix containing 15% recycled asphalt pavement (RAP).

**1.9 Project Justification:**
The Oak Trail serves as a critical link in the city's recreational network, connecting the Central Park to the Community Center and the residential neighborhoods to the north. The deteriorated condition of the trail poses a safety hazard to users. Recent reports from the City of Oakhaven Public Safety Department indicate an increase in minor accidents related to uneven surfaces and potholes. The resurfacing project is necessary to restore the trail to a safe and functional condition. Additionally, the project aligns with the City of Oakhaven's Comprehensive Plan, which prioritizes the expansion and improvement of greenway infrastructure to promote active transportation and public health.

**1.10 Project Scope:**
The scope of work includes:
1.  Demolition and removal of the existing 1.2 miles of deteriorated asphalt surface.
2.  Excavation and grading of the sub-base to ensure proper drainage.
3.  Installation of new drainage culverts at three critical low points along the trail.
4.  Application of a new 3-inch layer of hot-mix asphalt containing 15% RAP.
5.  Restoration of trail shoulders and adjacent landscaping.
6.  Installation of new trail signage and lighting fixtures.

SUBJECT: COORDINATION BETWEEN PUBLIC WORKS AND PARKS REGARDING UTILITY WORK NEAR COMMUNITY CENTER

**1. PURPOSE**

The purpose of this memorandum is to coordinate the upcoming utility work near the Community Center and to ensure the alignment of schedules between the Public Works Department and the Parks and Recreation Department. This coordination is necessary to minimize disruption to park activities and to ensure the safety of the public during the construction period.

**2. BACKGROUND**

The Public Works Department has identified a need to replace the main water line and install new fiber optic cables in the area adjacent to the Community Center. This work is critical to prevent potential flooding in the Community Center basement, which has experienced minor seepage during heavy rains in the past. The work is scheduled to take place over a two-week period in late March 2023.

The Parks and Recreation Department manages the Community Center grounds and the adjacent Oak Trail. The construction zone will impact the parking lot and a portion of the trail.

**3. SCOPE OF WORK**

**3.1 Public Works Department Responsibilities:**
a) Replace the main water line from the street to the Community Center.
b) Install new fiber optic cables along the east side of the parking lot.
c) Restore the asphalt surface of the parking lot and trail after construction.
d) Coordinate with the City Engineer to ensure compliance with all safety standards.

**3.2 Parks and Recreation Department Responsibilities:**
a) Notify park users and the public of the construction schedule and closures.
b) Provide temporary parking arrangements for visitors to the Community Center.
c) Monitor the construction site to ensure that park landscaping and trail surfaces are not damaged by construction equipment.
d) Coordinate with the City Engineer regarding the traffic control plan.

**4. SCHEDULE ALIGNMENT**

**4.1 Construction Dates:**
The utility work is scheduled to commence on March 27, 2023, and is expected to be completed by April 7, 2023.

**4.2 Parking Lot Closure:**
The parking lot adjacent to the Community Center will be closed to all public vehicles from March 27, 2023, to April 7, 2023. A temporary parking lot has been identified in the adjacent lot behind the fire station. Signage will be placed at all entrances to the Community Center to direct visitors to the temporary parking area.

**4.3 Trail Closure:**
A portion of the Oak Trail adjacent to the construction zone will be closed to pedestrians and cyclists from March 27, 2023, to April 7, 2023. Detour signage will be installed to guide users around the construction zone.

**5. TRAFFIC CONTROL PLAN**

The Public Works Department has developed a traffic control plan for the construction zone. The plan includes the installation of temporary traffic signals and signage to divert vehicles safely around the construction zone. The plan has been reviewed and approved by the City Engineer on March 20, 2023.

**5.1 Traffic Control Measures:**
a) Installation of temporary traffic signals at the intersection of Maple Street and Oak Trail.
b) Placement of barricades and cones to delineate the construction zone.
c) Deployment of flaggers during peak traffic hours (7:00 A.M. to 9:00 A.M. and 4:00 P.M. to 6:00 P.M.).
d) Installation of temporary lighting for night work, if necessary.

**6. SAFETY AND ENVIRONMENTAL CONSIDERATIONS**

**6.1 Safety:**
All construction personnel must adhere to the safety protocols outlined in the City of Oakhaven Public Works Safety Manual. Personal protective equipment (PPE) is required for all workers in the construction zone. The Parks and Recreation Department will conduct daily inspections of the construction site to ensure that safety measures are being followed.

**6.2 Environmental:**
The construction work will be conducted in a manner that minimizes the impact on the surrounding environment. Dust control measures will be implemented to prevent air pollution. Stormwater runoff will be managed to prevent erosion and sedimentation of nearby waterways.

**7. COMMUNICATION AND COORDINATION**

**7.1 Communication Plan:**
A joint communication plan has been developed to ensure that all stakeholders are informed of the construction schedule and any changes. The plan includes:
a) Posting notices on the City of Oakhaven website.
b) Sending email notifications to registered park users.
c) Distributing flyers to local businesses and residents.
d) Posting physical notices at the Community Center and trailheads.

**7.2 Coordination Meetings:**
A weekly coordination meeting will be held between the Public Works and Parks and Recreation Departments throughout the construction period. The first meeting is scheduled for March 24, 2023, at 10:00 A.M. in the City Engineer's office.

**8. COST ALLOCATION**

The cost of the utility work will be borne by the Public Works Department. The cost of the temporary parking arrangement and the signage will be shared equally between the Public Works and Parks and Recreation Departments. The total estimated cost for the coordination measures is $2,500.00.

**9. CONCLUSION**

The coordination between the Public Works and Parks and Recreation Departments is essential to ensure the successful completion of the utility work while minimizing disruption to park activities. The schedules have been aligned, and the traffic control plan has been approved by the City Engineer. The Departments will work together to ensure that the construction is completed safely and efficiently.
