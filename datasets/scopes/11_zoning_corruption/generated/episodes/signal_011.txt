**SUBJECT:** Staff Report - Crestview Holdings Variance #24-010
**DATE:** July 1, 2024

**1.0 EXECUTIVE SUMMARY**

This memorandum serves as the formal staff report regarding the application for Variance #24-010 submitted by Crestview Holdings LLC. The application, filed under the reference number VAR-24-010, seeks approval for two primary deviations from the Millbrook Municipal Zoning Code: a variance for density and a variance for parking requirements. The subject property is located at 1500 River Road, identified by Parcel ID 14-05-22-200, within the R-1 Single Family Residential Zoning District. The proposed project, designated as "Riverfront Luxury Apartments," is a high-density residential development intended to contain 200 residential units.

The Planning Department has completed a comprehensive review of the submitted documentation as of June 30, 2024. The review was conducted by Staff Reviewer James Wilson, who is responsible for the technical analysis of the application materials. Following the completion of the review, a formal recommendation has been drafted and filed with the Zoning Board of Appeals. The application for variance 24-010 is incomplete. The submitted materials fail to address critical statutory requirements necessary for the granting of variances under Section 8.4 of the Municipal Code.

The primary deficiencies identified during the review process involve the parking calculations, which do not meet R-1 standards. Furthermore, the proposed density is incompatible with the surrounding character of the district. A third critical deficiency involves the Traffic Impact Study, which contains significant omissions and methodological errors. Consequently, the recommendation is to DENY the request.

This memorandum is distributed to all Board Members as part of the pre-hearing packet. A copy has been sent to the City Clerk for archival purposes and to the Applicant, Crestview Holdings LLC, for their records. The public hearing for this matter is scheduled for July 15, 2024, at 6:00 PM in City Hall, Room 204. This matter is listed as Agenda Item 4. The review status is Final. The document consists of 8 pages of text and data.

**2.0 PROJECT DESCRIPTION AND LOCATION**

**2.1 Property Details**
The subject property is located at 1500 River Road, Millbrook, State. The legal description of the parcel is recorded in the County Recorder's Office under the following metes and bounds: Beginning at the northeast corner of Section 14, Township 5, Range 22; thence South 850 feet; thence West 400 feet; thence North 850 feet; thence East 400 feet to the point of beginning. The total acreage of the parcel is 15.2 acres. The current zoning classification is R-1, which permits single-family detached residences with a maximum density of 4.5 units per acre.

**2.2 Applicant Information**
The applicant is Crestview Holdings LLC, a limited liability company registered in the State of Millbrook. The principal officer listed on the application is Marcus Webb. The entity was formed on March 12, 2023. The legal representative for the application is the firm of Harrison & Associates, located at 550 Commerce Drive, Suite 300.

**2.3 Proposed Development**
The project is titled "Riverfront Luxury Apartments." The development plan proposes the construction of four (4) multi-story residential buildings. The total unit count for the project is 200 units. The proposed unit mix includes 40 one-bedroom units, 100 two-bedroom units, 50 three-bedroom units, and 10 four-bedroom units. The proposed building heights range from 24 feet to 42 feet. The total floor area of the proposed structures is 245,000 square feet. The site plan indicates a building coverage ratio of 32 percent.

**3.0 ZONING COMPLIANCE ANALYSIS**

**3.1 Density Variance Request**
The current R-1 zoning district permits a maximum density of 4.5 units per acre. Based on the 15.2-acre parcel, the maximum allowable density is 68.4 units. The proposed development of 200 units represents a density of 13.16 units per acre. This constitutes a variance request for a density increase of 192 percent over the permitted maximum.

The applicant has requested this variance citing the "unique physical characteristics" of the site, specifically the topographical slope and the presence of a wetland buffer. The Planning Department has reviewed the topographical survey submitted with the application. The survey indicates a slope gradient of approximately 8 percent across the northern portion of the site. However, the analysis by the Planning Department determines that the slope is not unique in the Millbrook area, as similar gradients exist in the adjacent River Valley Subdivision. Furthermore, the wetland buffer, while present, covers only 1.2 acres of the total parcel, leaving 14 acres available for development. The staff review concludes that the density is incompatible with surrounding character. The surrounding area consists of single-family homes with significant setbacks and large lot sizes. Introducing a high-density structure of this magnitude would fundamentally alter the visual and functional character of the neighborhood.

**3.2 Parking Variance Request**
The R-1 zoning district requires a minimum of 1.5 parking spaces per residential unit for multi-family developments. For a 200-unit project, the code requires a minimum of 300 parking spaces. The applicant's site plan proposes a total of 210 parking spaces. This represents a shortfall of 90 spaces, or a 30 percent reduction from the required minimum.

The applicant has submitted a parking analysis report. The report argues that the "luxury" nature of the apartments and the proximity to the downtown transit hub will reduce the need for private vehicle ownership. The staff review of the parking calculations does not meet R-1 standards. The methodology used in the applicant's report relies on a 40 percent reduction in parking demand based on national averages for urban centers. Millbrook is a mid-sized city with a car-dependent infrastructure. The Transit Authority of Millbrook (TAM) has indicated that the proposed site is not within the immediate walking radius of a high-frequency transit line. The staff analysis utilizes the Institute of Transportation Engineers (ITE) Trip Generation Manual for residential developments in suburban environments. The ITE data suggests a parking demand of 1.6 spaces per unit for this type of development. Even using the lower bound of the applicant's own data, the requirement remains unmet.

**3.3 Traffic Impact Study Deficiencies**
The application included a Traffic Impact Study (TIS) prepared by the firm of Traffic Solutions, Inc. The study was dated June 15, 2024. The Planning Department has identified critical deficiencies in this document. The study fails to account for the peak hour traffic generation of a 200-unit development during the evening commute window. The study assumes a 15 percent reduction in vehicle trips due to "shared mobility services," a factor not supported by local data.

Furthermore, the study does not adequately analyze the intersection of River Road and Oak Street, which is the primary access point for the site. The study models traffic flow at a Level of Service (LOS) of "C." However, the current LOS at the intersection is already "D" during peak hours. Adding 200 units would degrade the LOS to "F," resulting in severe congestion. The study also fails to consider the impact of emergency vehicle access and fire lane requirements. The new deficiency identified in this review is the Traffic Impact Study Deficiencies. The study is insufficient to support the variance request.

**4.0 PUBLIC INTEREST AND NOTIFICATION**

**4.1 Public Interest**
The public interest in this matter is High. The development of 200 units in a single-family zone is a significant departure from the comprehensive plan. The site is located adjacent to the Riverfront Park, a public recreational area. There is concern regarding the strain on public utilities, specifically water and sewer capacity. The Millbrook Water Authority has indicated that the current infrastructure is operating at 85 percent capacity. The addition of 200 new units would require a capacity expansion that has not been funded or approved.

**4.2 Public Notice**
Public Notice was posted on the official website of the City of Millbrook on June 25, 2024. The notice was also mailed to all property owners within a 300-foot radius of the site on June 20, 2024. The notice included a summary of the application, the proposed variance, and the date of the public hearing. The notice was displayed at City Hall for the required 14-day period.

**4.3 Media Attention**
Media attention regarding this application has been Local. The Millbrook Gazette published an article on June 28, 2024, regarding the potential density increase. The article quoted several residents expressing concern about the impact on property values and traffic. The local television station, Channel 5, scheduled a segment for the evening of July 1, 2024, to discuss the upcoming hearing.

**5.0 STAFF RECOMMENDATION**

**5.1 Recommendation Statement**
Based on the review of the application, the analysis of the zoning code compliance, and the assessment of the public interest, the Planning Department recommends that the Zoning Board of Appeals DENY the request. The application for variance 24-010 is incomplete. The submitted materials fail to demonstrate that the hardship is unique to the property. The parking calculations do not meet R-1 standards. The density is incompatible with surrounding character. The Traffic Impact Study contains deficiencies that prevent an accurate assessment of the project's impact on the public roadway system.

**5.2 Reasoning**
The recommendation to DENY the request is based on the following specific findings:
1.  **Insufficient Parking:** The proposed 210 spaces fall short of the 300 spaces required by the R-1 zoning code. The applicant's justification for the reduction is not supported by local traffic data or the Institute of Transportation Engineers standards.
2.  **Density Incompatible:** The proposed density of 13.16 units per acre is nearly three times the permitted density. The visual and functional character of the R-1 district would be irreparably altered by the introduction of high-density structures.
3.  **Traffic Impact Study Deficiencies:** The submitted Traffic Impact Study is flawed in its assumptions and methodology. It fails to account for the cumulative impact of the development on the local road network, specifically the intersection of River Road and Oak Street.

The review was completed on June 30, 2024. The purpose of the review is to assess the potential environmental impacts of the proposed development.

**2.0 SITE CHARACTERISTICS**

**2.1 Topography**
The site is located on a slope with a gradient of approximately 8 percent. The northern portion of the site is steeper than the southern portion. The site is located in a floodplain.

**2.2 Vegetation**
The site is covered with mixed hardwood forest. The forest is dense in the northern portion of the site. The forest is less dense in the southern portion.

**2.3 Wetlands**
The site contains a wetland buffer of approximately 1.2 acres. The wetland buffer is located on the western portion of the site. The wetland buffer is protected by state and local regulations.

**3.0 ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACTS**

**3.1 Water Quality**
The proposed development could impact water quality due to the construction of impervious surfaces. The construction of 200 units will increase the amount of impervious surface on the site. The increase in impervious surface could lead to increased runoff. The runoff could carry pollutants into the wetland buffer.

**3.2 Air Quality**
The proposed development could impact air quality due to the construction and operation of the buildings. The construction of the buildings could generate dust and noise. The operation of the buildings could generate vehicle emissions.

**3.3 Noise**
The proposed development could impact noise levels due to the construction and operation of the buildings. The construction of the buildings could generate noise. The operation of the buildings could generate noise from traffic and other sources.

**3.4 Wildlife**
The proposed development could impact wildlife due to the loss of habitat. The construction of the buildings could destroy habitat for local wildlife. The loss of habitat could lead to a decline in wildlife populations.

**4.0 MITIGATION MEASURES**

**4.1 Water Quality**
The applicant has proposed a stormwater management plan. The plan includes the installation of bioswales and retention ponds. The plan is designed to reduce the amount of runoff and to filter pollutants.

**4.2 Air Quality**
The applicant has proposed a dust control plan. The plan includes the use of water trucks and covers for construction equipment. The plan is designed to reduce the amount of dust generated during construction.

**4.3 Noise**
The applicant has proposed a noise control plan. The plan includes the use of noise barriers and limits on construction hours. The plan is designed to reduce the amount of noise generated during construction.

**4.4 Wildlife**
The applicant has proposed a wildlife mitigation plan. The plan includes the preservation of the wetland buffer and the creation of a wildlife corridor. The plan is designed to minimize the impact on wildlife.

**5.0 CONCLUSION**

The impacts could be mitigated through the implementation of the proposed mitigation measures. However, the review also indicates that the application for variance 24-010 is incomplete. The applicant has not provided a complete environmental impact study. The density is incompatible with surrounding character. The parking calculations do not meet R-1 standards. The Traffic Impact Study Deficiencies are a major concern. The recommendation is to DENY the request.
