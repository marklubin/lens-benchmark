**AGENDA ITEM 4: PRESENTATION OF GRANT ALLOCATION RECORD**
Superintendent Dr. Arthur P. Mitchell took the podium to present the Grant Allocation Record for the Special Education Funding section. He distributed copies of the document to all Board members and the public.

**Presentation Details:**
Dr. Mitchell stated that the Northwood Independent School District has successfully secured the State IDEA Allocation for the 2024-2025 fiscal year. The total amount awarded is $2,800,000. This funding is designated specifically for the improvement and expansion of special education services across the district.

The record indicates that the funds will be distributed among five (5) recipient schools. These schools were selected based on a comprehensive needs assessment conducted during the fall of 2023. The selection criteria included:
1.  The current number of students identified with disabilities.
2.  The ratio of special education staff to students.
3.  The results of the most recent district-wide audit of special education compliance.
4.  The projected enrollment growth in special education programs for the upcoming school year.

The five recipient schools are:
1.  Northwood Elementary School (Parcel ID: NES-2024-001)
2.  Northwood Middle School (Parcel ID: NMS-2024-002)
3.  Northwood High School (Parcel ID: NHS-2024-003)
4.  Westside Special Learning Center (Parcel ID: WSL-2024-004)
5.  Northwood Alternative Education Program (Parcel ID: NAE-2024-005)

Dr. Mitchell noted that the distribution of the $2,800,000 is not uniform. The allocation is weighted to reflect the specific needs of each school. Northwood Elementary School, which serves a high population of students with autism spectrum disorders, will receive a proportionally larger share to support the expansion of behavioral intervention teams. Northwood High School will receive funds to support transition services for students aging out of the special education system.

The Superintendent emphasized that this allocation is contingent upon the district maintaining full compliance with the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and the state of Texas Education Code. Any deviation from these federal and state mandates could result in the suspension of future funding.

Dr. Mitchell presented the Grant Allocation Record for review. The document details the specific line items for the $2,800,000, including:
*   Personnel costs for additional paraprofessionals.
*   Professional development for existing staff.
*   Acquisition of specialized instructional materials.
*   Assessment tools for early identification of learning disabilities.
*   Transportation costs for students requiring specialized transport.

**AGENDA ITEM 5: REVIEW OF BUDGET SUMMARY FOR IEP STAFFING**
Chief Financial Officer Linda K. Farris presented the Budget Summary for the IEP Staffing section. She provided a detailed breakdown of the proposed staffing increases and the associated cost impact.

**Budget Summary Details:**
The Board reviewed the proposal to increase the number of positions dedicated to Individualized Education Program (IEP) staffing. The proposal outlines a staff increase of 12 positions. These positions are critical to meeting the increased demand for special education services and addressing the backlog of evaluations identified in the previous fiscal year.

The cost impact of these 12 new positions is estimated at $650,000. This figure includes:
*   Base salaries for the new hires.
*   Employer contributions to retirement and health insurance.
*   Fringe benefits and payroll taxes.
*   Professional development and training costs for the new hires.

The 12 new positions are categorized as follows:
1.  **Special Education Teachers (4 positions):** These positions will be assigned to the five recipient schools to reduce class sizes for students with high-incidence disabilities. The average class size in the special education wing of Northwood Elementary School is currently 14 students, and the goal is to reduce this to 10 students per class.
2.  **Speech-Language Pathologists (3 positions):** These positions will address the shortage of speech therapists, particularly in the Northwood Middle School and Northwood High School districts. The current caseload for speech therapists is 45 students, which exceeds the recommended maximum of 35 students.
3.  **Occupational Therapists (2 positions):** These positions will focus on students with sensory processing disorders and fine motor skill deficits. The Westside Special Learning Center will be the primary location for these new hires.
4.  **Behavioral Intervention Specialists (2 positions):** These positions will support students with emotional and behavioral disorders. They will work closely with the existing school counselors to develop and implement behavior intervention plans.
5.  **Paraprofessionals (1 position):** This position will provide one-on-one support for students with severe disabilities who require constant supervision.

**Financial Impact Analysis:**
The $650,000 cost impact will be offset by the $2,800,000 State IDEA Allocation. The remaining balance of $2,150,000 will be used for other operational expenses, including curriculum materials, assistive technology, and facility maintenance.

The CFO presented a graph showing the projected expenditure over the next five years. The graph indicates a steady increase in special education spending, driven by the growing number of students identified with disabilities. The projection suggests that without the proposed staffing increases, the district will face significant legal and compliance risks.

**Motion to Approve Budget Summary:**
Motion to approve the Budget Summary for IEP Staffing, which includes a staff increase of 12 positions with a total cost impact of $650,000, to be funded by the State IDEA Allocation.
Moved by: Trustee Chang
Seconded by: Trustee Jenkins

**Discussion:**
Trustee Vance asked about the recruitment strategy for the new positions. Dr. Mitchell responded that the Human Resources Department has already begun advertising the positions. They are expecting a high volume of applications due to the competitive salary package and the opportunity to work in a well-funded district.

Trustee Rodriguez asked if the district has a plan to retain the new staff. Dr. Mitchell stated that the district offers a retention bonus for special education teachers who complete their first three years of service. Additionally, the district provides a mentorship program where new hires are paired with experienced staff members.

Trustee Okonkwo inquired about the timeline for hiring. Dr. Mitchell stated that the goal is to have all 12 positions filled by August 1, 2024, to ensure a smooth start to the school year. The district is prepared to offer temporary contracts to qualified candidates if full-time positions are not filled by the deadline.

Trustee Halloway asked if the $650,000 cost impact includes the cost of benefits for the new hires. Dr. Mitchell confirmed that the figure is inclusive of all benefits and taxes. He noted that the district's benefit package is among the most competitive in the state, which helps attract high-quality candidates.

Trustee Thorne asked about the impact on the general fund. Dr. Mitchell explained that the $650,000 is entirely covered by the State IDEA Allocation. There will be no impact on the general fund or the operating budget for the district.

**AGENDA ITEM 7: INTER-DEPARTMENTAL MEMO**
The Board reviewed an inter-departmental memo from the Director of Special Education to the Superintendent. The memo outlined the implementation plan for the new staffing and funding.

**Memo Details:**
The memo, dated February 12, 2024, detailed the steps required to implement the 12 new positions and the $2,800,000 allocation. The implementation plan includes:
1.  **Recruitment:** The Human Resources Department will post job openings on the district website and local job boards by February 20, 2024.
2.  **Interview Process:** Interviews will be conducted in March 2024. A panel of three members, including the Director of Special Education, the Principal of the recipient school, and a parent representative, will conduct the interviews.
3.  **Background Checks:** All candidates will undergo a comprehensive background check, including fingerprinting and reference verification.
4.  **Training:** New hires will attend a two-week orientation program before the start of the school year. The program will cover district policies, special education laws, and classroom management techniques.
5.  **Resource Allocation:** The Director of Special Education will work with the principals of the five recipient schools to ensure that the new staff are assigned to the appropriate classrooms and therapy rooms.
6.  **Monitoring:** The Director of Special Education will submit a monthly progress report to the Board of Trustees detailing the status of the recruitment and implementation process.

The memo also included a timeline for the disbursement of the funds. The first installment of $700,000 will be released on July 1, 2024. The remaining $2,100,000 will be released in three equal installments of $700,000 each on October 1, 2024, January 1, 2025, and April 1, 2025.

**ENVIRONMENTAL REVIEW SUMMARY**
**NORTHWOOD INDEPENDENT SCHOOL DISTRICT**
**DATE: FEBRUARY 14, 2024**
**PROJECT: SPECIAL EDUCATION FUNDING AND STAFFING**
**REVIEW ID: NISD-ENV-2024-012**

**1. PROJECT OVERVIEW:**
The Northwood Independent School District is proposing to allocate $2,800,000 from the State IDEA Allocation to fund the expansion of special education services. This includes the hiring of 12 new staff positions and the purchase of assistive technology equipment. The project will impact five schools within the district.

**2. POTENTIAL ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACTS:**
The Environmental Review Committee has assessed the potential environmental impacts of the project. The following impacts were identified:

*   **Air Quality:** No significant impacts are expected. The project does not involve any new construction or heavy machinery that would emit pollutants.
*   **Water Resources:** No significant impacts are expected. The project does not involve any changes to water usage or discharge.
*   **Waste Management:** The project will result in a slight increase in waste from the disposal of old equipment. The district will ensure that all electronic waste is recycled in accordance with state regulations.
*   **Noise:** No significant impacts are expected. The project does not involve any new equipment that would generate excessive noise.
*   **Energy Consumption:** There will be a marginal increase in energy consumption due to the use of new technology. The district has budgeted for this increase.

**3. MITIGATION MEASURES:**
The following mitigation measures have been implemented to minimize any potential environmental impacts:
*   **Waste Management:** The district will partner with a certified e-waste recycling company to ensure that all old equipment is disposed of properly.
*   **Energy Efficiency:** The district will use energy-efficient equipment and lighting in the new classrooms.
