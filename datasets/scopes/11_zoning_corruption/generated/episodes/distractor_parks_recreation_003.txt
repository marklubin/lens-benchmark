5. CAPITAL IMPROVEMENTS - CENTRAL PARK MAINTENANCE
The Director of Parks, Jennifer Lopez, presented the Capital Improvements report regarding Central Park. The report detailed the current condition of the park infrastructure and outlined the proposed maintenance schedule for the upcoming fiscal year.

Item 4A: Review of Maintenance Schedule.
The Director presented a schedule outlining routine inspections, seasonal maintenance, and emergency repair protocols. The schedule covers the period from August 1, 2023, through July 31, 2024. Specific attention was directed toward the playground equipment and the walking paths.
Commissioner Chen inquired about the frequency of equipment inspections. The Director stated that inspections occur monthly.
Commissioner Miller inquired about the budget allocation for emergency repairs. The Director confirmed that a portion of the contingency fund is designated for such repairs.

Item 4B: Asphalt Resurfacing Recommendation.
The Director presented a staff recommendation for asphalt resurfacing of the main vehicular access roads within Central Park. The recommendation included a cost estimate of $145,000. The proposed work involves grinding the existing surface, applying a tack coat, and laying a new layer of asphalt. The project is scheduled to begin in September 2023.
The staff recommendation noted that the current asphalt condition has degraded to a level requiring immediate attention to prevent further deterioration.
Commissioner Halloway asked if there were alternative materials considered. The Director stated that asphalt was selected based on durability and cost-effectiveness for the City of Oakhaven standards.
Commissioner Williams asked about the traffic impact during construction. The Director stated that traffic control measures would be implemented to maintain access for emergency vehicles and residents.
Commissioner Gonzalez asked about the timeline for completion. The Director estimated a completion date of October 15, 2023.

Item 4C: Budget Allocation for Infrastructure.
The Finance Liaison, Mark Stevens, presented the budget breakdown for the infrastructure improvements. The total allocation for the Capital Improvements Program is $2.5 million for the fiscal year. The Central Park resurfacing project accounts for 5.8% of the total allocation.
The Finance Liaison noted that the funds are available in the current budget cycle. No additional appropriations are required.

Motion: A motion was made by Commissioner Miller to approve the staff recommendation for asphalt resurfacing at Central Park, with a budget allocation of $145,000, subject to the completion timeline of October 15, 2023. The motion was seconded by Commissioner Chen.
The Chair asked for a vote on the motion.
Roll Call Vote:
Halloway: Aye
Gonzalez: Aye
Chen: Aye
Miller: Aye
Williams: Aye
Motion Carried: 5-0.

6. LANDSCAPE MAINTENANCE SERVICES CONTRACT
The Director of Parks presented the proposal for Landscape Maintenance Services. The proposal was submitted by CityScapes LLC. The Director outlined the scope of work, term, and financial terms.

Item 5A: Review of CityScapes LLC Proposal.
The proposal included a detailed scope of work covering mowing, pruning, and irrigation repair. The term of the contract is 12 months, beginning August 1, 2023, and ending July 31, 2024. The total value of the contract is $60,000.
The Director stated that CityScapes LLC was selected through a competitive bidding process. Three bids were received. CityScapes LLC submitted the lowest compliant bid.
Commissioner Halloway asked about the qualifications of the bidder. The Director confirmed that the bidder holds all necessary state licenses and insurance coverage required by municipal code.

Item 5B: Contract Term and Scope Definition.
The contract scope includes weekly mowing of all grassy areas, monthly pruning of shrubbery, and quarterly inspection of irrigation systems. The contract also includes emergency response for irrigation failures within 24 hours.
Commissioner Williams inquired about the penalty for non-compliance. The Director stated that the contract includes a liquidated damages clause for failure to meet schedule requirements.

Item 5C: Financial Terms and Payment Schedule.
Payment is to be made monthly upon submission of an invoice. The total value of $60,000 is to be paid in twelve equal installments of $5,000.
The Finance Liaison confirmed that the funds are available in the Operating Budget for Parks and Recreation.

Motion: A motion was made by Commissioner Gonzalez to approve the Landscape Maintenance Services Contract with CityScapes LLC for a term of 12 months at a total value of $60,000. The motion was seconded by Commissioner Miller.

DOCUMENT 4: STAFF RECOMMENDATION MEMORANDUM
OFFICE OF THE DIRECTOR OF PARKS AND RECREATION
CITY OF OAKHAVEN

MEMORANDUM

TO: Parks and Recreation Commission
FROM: Jennifer Lopez, Director of Parks and Recreation
DATE: July 14, 2023
SUBJECT: Capital Improvements and Maintenance Schedule - Central Park

1. PURPOSE
The purpose of this memorandum is to provide the Commission with a detailed staff recommendation regarding the maintenance schedule and capital improvements for Central Park, specifically focusing on the asphalt resurfacing project.

2. BACKGROUND
Central Park is located at 450 Park Avenue, Oakhaven, State 98201. The park serves approximately 5,000 residents within a one-mile radius. The current condition of the vehicular access roads has been assessed by the City Engineering Department. The assessment indicates significant cracking and deterioration of the asphalt surface.

3. RECOMMENDATION
The Parks and Recreation Department recommends approval of the asphalt resurfacing project. The project scope includes:
a. Removal of existing asphalt.
b. Grading and leveling of the sub-base.
c. Application of tack coat.
d. Laying of two inches of new asphalt.
e. Installation of new striping.

4. FINANCIAL IMPLICATIONS
The estimated cost for the project is $145,000. This amount is available within the Capital Improvements Fund. No additional funding is required.
The Landscape Maintenance Services Contract with CityScapes LLC is estimated at $60,000 for a 12-month term. This amount is available within the Operating Budget.

5. TIMELINE
The asphalt resurfacing is scheduled to begin on September 1, 2023. The project is expected to conclude by October 15, 2023.
The Landscape Maintenance Contract begins August 1, 2023.

6. PUBLIC IMPACT
Traffic control measures will be implemented during construction. Residents will be notified via email and posted signage at the park entrance.
Public comment regarding extended lighting hours will be reviewed by the Committee in October 2023.

7. CONCLUSION
Approval of these items is necessary to maintain the safety and functionality of Central Park.

Respectfully submitted,
Jennifer Lopez
Director of Parks and Recreation

DOCUMENT 5: LANDSCAPE MAINTENANCE SERVICES CONTRACT
OFFICE OF THE CITY CLERK
CITY OF OAKHAVEN

CONTRACT AGREEMENT
LANDSCAPE MAINTENANCE SERVICES

THIS AGREEMENT is made and entered into this 1st day of August, 2023, by and between the CITY OF OAKHAVEN, hereinafter referred to as "City," and CityScapes LLC, hereinafter referred to as "Contractor."

RECITALS
WHEREAS, the City desires to secure landscape maintenance services for City-owned properties, including Central Park; and
WHEREAS, the Contractor represents that it is qualified and experienced to provide such services; and
WHEREAS, the City has determined that the Contractor is the most suitable provider based on the competitive bidding process conducted in June 2023.

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants contained herein, the parties agree as follows:

1. SCOPE OF WORK
The Contractor shall provide the following services:
a. Mowing: Weekly mowing of all grassy areas within the designated park boundaries.
b. Pruning: Monthly pruning of shrubbery and trees to maintain safety and aesthetics.
c. Irrigation Repair: Inspection and repair of irrigation systems. Quarterly comprehensive inspection.
d. Debris Removal: Removal of leaves and debris during the autumn season.

2. TERM
The term of this Agreement shall commence on August 1, 2023, and shall continue for a period of twelve (12) months, ending on July 31, 2024.

3. COMPENSATION
The City shall pay the Contractor a total sum of Sixty Thousand Dollars ($60,000.00).
Payment shall be made in twelve (12) equal monthly installments of Five Thousand Dollars ($5,000.00).
Invoices shall be submitted by the 5th day of each month. Payment shall be issued within 30 days of invoice receipt.

4. INSURANCE
The Contractor shall maintain general liability insurance with a minimum limit of $1,000,000 per occurrence. Proof of insurance shall be provided prior to the commencement of work.

5. INDEMNIFICATION
The Contractor agrees to indemnify and hold harmless the City from any claims, damages, or liabilities arising out of the performance of this Agreement.

6. TERMINATION
Either party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days written notice. In the event of termination for cause, the City shall pay for services rendered up to the date of termination.

7. COMPLIANCE
The Contractor shall comply with all applicable laws, ordinances, and regulations of the City of Oakhaven.

8. DISPUTE RESOLUTION
Any dispute arising under this Agreement shall be resolved through mediation in Oakhaven, State.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the parties have executed this Agreement as of the date first written above.

CITY OF OAKHAVEN
By: Thomas Halloway, Chair, Parks and Recreation Commission
Date: August 1, 2023

CITYSCAPES LLC
By: Michael Roberts, CEO
Date: August 1, 2023
