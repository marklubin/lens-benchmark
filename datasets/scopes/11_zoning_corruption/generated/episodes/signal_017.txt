DOCUMENT 1: BOARD MINUTES - SPECIAL SESSION (MILLBROOK)

CITY OF CITY CENTER
ZONING BOARD OF APPEALS
SPECIAL SESSION MINUTES

DATE: July 19, 2024
TIME: 6:00 PM TO 8:00 PM
LOCATION: City Hall Room 204
MEETING ID: ZBA-07-19
RECORDER: Administrative Assistant

CALL TO ORDER
The Special Session was called to order by Chairperson Marcus Patterson at 6:00 PM. A quorum was established with the presence of seven (7) members. The following members were present and accounted for: Marcus Patterson, Elena Kowalski, David Rivera, Sarah Chen, Thompson, Martinez, and Wilson. The Zoning Board of Appeals convened for the purpose of hearing applications regarding zoning variances and land use requests.

AGENDA ITEM 1: APPLICATION 24-011
Applicant: Millbrook Community Land Trust
Property Address: 1600 Community Drive
Request Type: Setback Variance
Zoning District: R-1
Variance Request: 24-011

The Board considered the Land Trust application. Staff Report ID: PD-2024-06-20 was reviewed by the Board members. Staff Recommendation: APPROVE was noted in the official file. The application seeks a variance regarding the minimum setback requirements for the proposed affordable housing project.

PUBLIC COMMENT
The Board opened the floor for public comment. Public Speakers: 15 individuals registered to speak. Comments were recorded in the Public Comment Log attached to these minutes. Speakers included residents of the immediate vicinity, representatives of the Millbrook Community Land Trust, and members of the City Planning Department.

STAFF PRESENTATION
The Planning Department representative presented the technical findings regarding the variance request. The staff report highlighted the need for the variance to accommodate the design of the affordable housing units. The staff noted that the current zoning district R-1 allows for residential development but specifies strict setback lines.

BOARD DISCUSSION
Members discussed the implications of the variance. Discussion focused on the technical specifications of the property lines. The Board considered the technicality cited regarding the boundary alignment.

STATEMENT OF TECHNICALITY
Setback calculation was found to be non-compliant with the current code interpretation. The Board reviewed the survey data submitted by the applicant. The data indicated a discrepancy in the measured distance from the property line to the proposed structure.

MOTION AND VOTE
Motion to deny was made by Member Patterson. Seconded by Member Kowalski. The motion was placed before the Board for voting.

ROLL CALL VOTE
The Clerk called the roll for the vote on Motion 24-011-Deny.
Roll Call Vote: Yes (3)
Roll Call Vote: No (4)
Roll Call Vote: Abstain (0)

The Yes Voters were identified as Thompson, Martinez, Wilson. The No Voters were identified as Patterson, Kowalski, Rivera, Chen. The vote was 4-3 to deny. The Motion Carried.

ORDER ISSUED
Order Number: ORD-2024-19
Order Signed: July 19, 2024
Distribution: Applicant, City Clerk

The Board issued an Order denying the variance request. The denial was based on the finding that the setback calculation did not meet the minimum requirements of the Municipal Code. The Order states that the applicant may submit a revised application or appeal the decision.

APPEAL PROCESS
Appeal Deadline: July 19, 2025
Applicant Response: Pending
The Millbrook Community Land Trust was notified of the Appeal Deadline. The Applicant Response is currently Pending as of the time of this recording.

PROJECT DETAILS
Project Type: Affordable Housing
Unit Count: 40
Federal Grant ID: FHA-2024-99
Grant Impact: Contingency at Risk
Federal Grant: Contingent

The project is designated as Affordable Housing with a Unit Count of 40 units. A Federal Grant ID: FHA-2024-99 is associated with the funding for this development. The Grant Impact is noted as Contingency at Risk due to the variance denial. The Federal Grant status is Contingent upon the resolution of the zoning variance.

COMMUNITY AND MEDIA
Community Reaction: Strong Opposition
Media Coverage: Local News

The Community Reaction is recorded as Strong Opposition to the variance. Media Coverage: Local News reported on the proceedings of the meeting. The local news outlet covered the vote count and the specific details of the denial.

NEXT STEPS
Next Meeting: July 22, 2024
Location: City Hall Room 204
Contact: ZBA Clerk

The Board adjourned the session at 8:00 PM. The minutes were recorded and are subject to approval at the next meeting.

The Special Session continued for the second agenda item. The Board convened at 6:00 PM and adjourned at 8:00 PM. Quorum Established. The following members were present: Marcus Patterson, Elena Kowalski, David Rivera, Sarah Chen, Thompson, Martinez, and Wilson.

AGENDA ITEM 2: APPLICATION 24-010
Applicant: Crestview Holdings LLC
Property Address: 1500 River Road
Request Type: Variance for Density
Variance Request: 24-010

The Board considered the Crestview Holdings application. Staff Report ID: PD-2024-07-10 was reviewed by the Board members. Staff Recommendation: DENY was noted in the official file by the Planning Department. The application seeks a variance for density regarding the proposed multi-unit development.

PUBLIC COMMENT
The Board opened the floor for public comment. Public Speakers: 10 individuals registered to speak. Comments were recorded in the Public Comment Log attached to these minutes. Speakers included local business owners, residents, and representatives of the Crestview Holdings LLC.

STAFF PRESENTATION
The Planning Department representative presented the findings regarding the density variance. The staff noted insufficient parking documentation. The staff report indicated that the parking plan submitted did not meet the requirements for the proposed unit count.

BOARD DISCUSSION
Members discussed the parking requirements and the density of the proposed structure. Discussion focused on the infrastructure capacity of the surrounding area. The Board considered the implications of the density increase on local traffic patterns.

STATEMENT OF FINDINGS
Staff noted insufficient parking documentation. The Board reviewed the parking submission forms. The forms lacked the required number of spaces per unit as mandated by the Municipal Code.

MOTION AND VOTE
Motion to approve variance 24-010 was made. Seconded by Member Rivera. The motion was placed before the Board for voting.

ROLL CALL VOTE
The Clerk called the roll for the vote on Motion 24-010-Approve.
Roll Call Vote: Yes (4)
Roll Call Vote: No (3)
Roll Call Vote: Abstain (0)

The Yes Voters were identified as Patterson, Kowalski, Rivera, Chen. The No Voters were identified as Thompson, Martinez, Wilson. The vote was 4-3 to approve. The Motion Carried.

ORDER ISSUED
Order Number: ORD-2024-19-2
Order Signed: July 19, 2024
Distribution: Applicant, City Clerk

The Board issued an Order approving the variance request. The approval allows the applicant to proceed with the density variance subject to conditions.

APPEAL PROCESS
Appeal Deadline: July 19, 2025

The Appeal Deadline is July 19, 2025. Applicant Response: None was recorded at the time of the meeting.
