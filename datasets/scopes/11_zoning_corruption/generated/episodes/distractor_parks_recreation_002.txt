**Agenda Item 2: Review of Summer Sports League Scheduling and Field Allocation**

Staff Director of Facilities, David K. Miller, presented the report regarding the allocation of municipal athletic fields for the upcoming summer season, specifically addressing the Youth Baseball League. Director Miller outlined the current inventory of available playing surfaces within the Oakhaven Municipal Park District.

Director Miller stated that the total square footage of available turf and natural grass surfaces designated for baseball and softball usage currently stands at 180,000 square feet across four distinct complexes: the North Complex, the Central Green, the Riverfront Fields, and the West End Soccer Base.

The agenda item focused on the specific request from the Oakhaven Youth Baseball Association (OYBA) to utilize additional space to accommodate the growing number of registered teams. The OYBA submitted a formal request on March 10, 2023, detailing a need for 12 dedicated youth baseball fields to serve approximately 480 registered players aged 5 through 14.

Staff analysis indicated that the current inventory supports 10 full-sized fields. The request for 12 fields necessitates a reconfiguration of the play area at the Riverfront Fields site. The proposed reconfiguration involves the conversion of two auxiliary practice areas, currently designated for general recreation and dog walking, into full-sized baseball diamonds.

Director Miller presented the preliminary site plans for the Riverfront Fields reconfiguration. The plans indicated that the conversion would require the removal of existing fencing and the regrading of two 1.5-acre parcels. The estimated cost for the earthwork and fencing installation was projected at $18,500, to be funded from the 2023 Capital Improvement Budget line item 23-CIB-045.

The Commission discussed the impact of this conversion on the general public's access to the Riverfront Fields. Several members expressed concern regarding the reduction of open green space available for passive recreation.

Commissioner O'Connell questioned the necessity of expanding to 12 fields, noting that the current 10 fields had been utilized at 95% capacity during the spring season.

Director Miller responded that the utilization rate was high due to the introduction of evening league play, which was not accounted for in the spring data. He further noted that the OYBA had indicated that without the additional two fields, the league would be forced to seek private facilities at a higher cost to the families, potentially reducing participation rates.

Commissioner Jenkins inquired about the timing of the construction. Director Miller confirmed that if approval was granted at this meeting, construction could commence by April 15, 2023, in time for the May 1st start of the summer season.

Commissioner Velez raised a question regarding the safety standards of the proposed fields, specifically concerning the proximity of the new fields to the adjacent wetland buffer zone.

Director Miller referenced the Environmental Impact Statement prepared by the City's Planning Department, which was filed on January 20, 2023. The statement confirmed that the proposed 12-field configuration maintains a minimum setback of 50 feet from the wetland edge, complying with the City's Environmental Protection Ordinance Section 12.4.

Chairperson Thorne asked for a staff recommendation regarding the approval of the 12-field configuration.

Director Miller stated: "It is the recommendation of the Facilities Department that the Commission approve the allocation of 12 youth baseball fields for the summer season, subject to the completion of the earthwork and fencing at the Riverfront Fields site by April 15, 2023. This approval will ensure the continuity of the youth league program and prevent the displacement of players to private facilities."

**Agenda Item 3: Staff Recommendation Regarding Field Lighting Upgrades**

The Commission proceeded to discuss the staff recommendation regarding field lighting upgrades. This item was added to the agenda following a review of the 2023-2024 budget forecast.

Director Miller presented the findings of the Lighting Audit conducted by the Facilities Department in March 2023. The audit revealed that the existing lighting infrastructure at the Riverfront Fields and the North Complex was installed in 1998 and had reached the end of its useful life. The current metal halide fixtures were inefficient, consuming 40% more energy than modern LED alternatives, and required frequent bulb replacements due to premature failure.

The staff recommendation proposed a phased replacement of all 24 existing light poles at the Riverfront Fields with 24 new LED high-mast lighting systems. The proposal included the installation of smart controls to allow for remote dimming and scheduling, which would reduce energy consumption during non-practice hours.

The estimated cost for the full lighting upgrade was $320,000. The funds were proposed to be drawn from the 2023 Energy Efficiency Grant Fund, which was allocated by the State Department of Environmental Conservation.

Director Miller noted that the lighting upgrade was critical for the safety of the 12 newly approved youth baseball fields. Without adequate lighting, evening practice and game times would be limited to daylight hours only, which would severely restrict the league's schedule during the summer months.

Commissioner Jenkins asked for clarification on the light pollution concerns raised by residents in the vicinity of the Riverfront Fields.

Director Miller referenced the Light Pollution Control Ordinance adopted in 2020. He stated that the proposed LED fixtures were fully shielded and directed downward, with a cutoff angle of 90 degrees, ensuring that no light would spill onto adjacent residential properties. The lighting design was reviewed and approved by the City's Lighting Committee on February 10, 2023.

Commissioner O'Connell inquired about the timeline for the installation.

Director Miller stated that the procurement process for the LED fixtures was currently underway. A request for proposals (RFP) had been issued on March 25, 2023, and the contract was expected to be awarded by May 1, 2023. Installation was projected to take six weeks, with completion anticipated by July 1, 2023.

Chairperson Thorne asked for a staff recommendation regarding the approval of the lighting upgrade project.

Director Miller stated: "It is the recommendation of the Facilities Department that the Commission approve the lighting upgrade project for the Riverfront Fields, allocating $320,000 from the Energy Efficiency Grant Fund. This project is essential for the operation of the 12 youth baseball fields and aligns with the City's sustainability goals."

**DEPARTMENT OF PLANNING AND ENVIRONMENTAL PROTECTION**
**SITE IMPACT STATEMENT**

**Document ID:** EIS-2023-0610-01
**Date of Issue:** June 10, 2023
**Project Title:** Boardwalk Construction - Wetland Buffer Zone
**Project Location:** Riverfront Fields, North Sector, Parcel ID: 04-002-15-001
**Applicant:** City of Oakhaven Parks and Recreation
**Project Manager:** Dr. Aris Velez

**1. Executive Summary**

This Site Impact Statement (EIS) provides an assessment of the proposed construction of a 500-foot wooden boardwalk within the wetland buffer zone adjacent to the Riverfront Fields. The boardwalk is intended to provide public access to the wetland area for educational and recreational purposes while minimizing disturbance to the natural habitat.

The assessment was conducted in accordance with the City's Environmental Protection Ordinance Section 12.4 and the State Wetlands Protection Act. The study area encompasses a 200-foot radius around the proposed boardwalk alignment.

**2. Project Description**

The proposed project involves the installation of a 500-foot elevated boardwalk constructed from treated cedar planks. The boardwalk will be elevated 18 inches above the ground surface to prevent direct contact with the soil and vegetation.

**3. Environmental Assessment**

**3.1 Hydrology and Water Table**
A hydrological survey was conducted on May 1, 2023, by the City's Environmental Engineering Division. The survey measured the water table levels in the wetland buffer zone over a period of 30 days.

**Findings:**
*   The average water table depth during the dry season is 12 inches below the surface.
*   The proposed boardwalk elevation of 18 inches ensures that the structure does not impede natural water flow or alter the water table.
*   The installation of support pilings will be limited to 3 feet in depth, which is above the primary aquifer zone.
*   No adverse impact on the local water table is anticipated.

**3.2 Vegetation and Wildlife**
A botanical survey was conducted on April 15, 2023. The survey identified 12 native plant species within the project area, including:
*   *Typha latifolia* (Common Cattail)
*   *Spartina alterniflora* (Saltmeadow Cordgrass)
*   *Juncus effusus* (Common Rush)

**Findings:**
*   The proposed boardwalk alignment avoids the densest clusters of native vegetation.
*   The elevated design minimizes shading effects on the underlying vegetation.
*   No endangered species were identified within the project area.
*   The construction will require the temporary removal of 0.2 acres of vegetation, which will be replaced with native species upon completion.

**3.3 Soil and Erosion Control**
The proposed construction methods include the use of low-impact equipment to minimize soil compaction. Erosion control measures, including silt fences and straw bales, will be installed prior to the start of construction.

**Findings:**
*   The soil composition is stable and suitable for the proposed construction.
*   No significant erosion is anticipated during the construction phase.
*   Post-construction revegetation plans are in place to restore any disturbed areas.

**4. Mitigation Measures**

To ensure the protection of the wetland ecosystem, the following mitigation measures will be implemented:
*   **Construction Limits:** All construction activity will be confined to the designated boardwalk footprint. No equipment will be allowed to enter the wetland buffer zone outside of this footprint.
*   **Timing:** Construction will be scheduled to avoid the nesting season for local bird species (March 15 to June 30).
*   **Lighting:** No permanent lighting will be installed on the boardwalk to minimize light pollution.
*   **Signage:** Interpretive signage will be installed to educate visitors about the importance of the wetland ecosystem and to discourage off-trail walking.

**5. Regulatory Compliance**

The project complies with the following regulations:
*   City of Oakhaven Environmental Protection Ordinance Section 12.4
*   State Wetlands Protection Act
*   Clean Water Act Section 404

**6. Conclusion**

Based on the findings of this Site Impact Statement, the proposed boardwalk construction will not result in adverse impacts on the local water table, vegetation, or wildlife. The project is consistent with the City's environmental protection goals and will provide a valuable educational and recreational resource for the community.

**7. Approval Status**

**Preliminary Assessment:** APPROVED
**Conditions:**
1.  Final signage must be approved by the Planning Department prior to construction.
2.  Construction must be completed by September 1, 2023.
3.  A final inspection must be conducted by the Environmental Protection Division upon completion.

**INTER-DEPARTMENTAL MEMORANDUM**

**TO:** Parks and Recreation Commission
**FROM:** Office of the City Manager
**DATE:** June 1, 2023
**SUBJECT:** Status Update on Parks and Recreation Projects

**1. Purpose**

This memorandum serves to provide a status update on the various projects initiated by the Parks and Recreation Commission during the period of April 1, 2023, to June 15, 2023. The update covers the approval of the 12 youth baseball fields, the lighting upgrades, the temporary event permit for the Riverfront Festival, and the boardwalk construction project.

**2. Youth Baseball Fields**

The Commission's approval of the 12 youth baseball fields at the Riverfront Fields site has been recorded. The Facilities Department has initiated the earthwork and fencing contract with GreenScape Construction LLC. The contract was awarded on April 10, 2023. Construction is scheduled to commence on April 15, 2023, and is expected to be completed by May 1, 2023.

The drainage system, including the new French drain, has been designed and approved by the City Engineer. The system is designed to handle a 100-year storm event. The installation of the new fencing and earthwork will be inspected by the City Engineer upon completion.

**3. Field Lighting Upgrades**

The lighting upgrade project for the Riverfront Fields has been approved. The procurement process for the LED fixtures is ongoing. The Request for Proposals (RFP) was issued on March 25, 2023. The contract is expected to be awarded by May 1, 2023. Installation is projected to take six weeks, with completion anticipated by July 1, 2023.

The smart control system has been specified in the RFP. The system will allow for remote dimming and scheduling, with a default setting to turn off all lights at 9:00 P.M. The City Engineer will conduct a post-installation light meter reading to ensure compliance with the 10-lux limit at the property line.

**4. Riverfront Festival Permit**

The Temporary Event Permit for the 2023 Riverfront Festival has been issued to the Oakhaven Community Foundation. The permit number is TE-2023-0584-A. The event is scheduled for June 10-11, 2023.

The security vendor, Oakhaven Security Services, has been contracted to provide 12 to 16 security personnel per day. The vendor has provided a copy of their liability insurance policy. The event layout has been approved by the Fire Marshal.

The City Sanitation Department has been notified of the event and will provide waste management services. The City Traffic Division has coordinated with the Applicant to manage traffic flow.

**5. Boardwalk Construction**

The Site Impact Statement for the boardwalk construction project has been approved. The project is pending final signage approval. The Environmental Protection Division has indicated that the final signage must be approved prior to construction.

The project is scheduled to begin construction on July 1, 2023, and is expected to be completed by September 1, 2023. The Environmental Protection Division will conduct a final inspection upon completion.

**6. Financial Summary**

The following payments have been processed or are pending:
*   **Playground Equipment:** Payment of $45,000.00 to PlayTime Solutions Inc. has been processed on June 1, 2023.
*   **Riverfront Festival:** The fee of $1,200.00 has been collected from the Oakhaven Community Foundation.
*   **Lighting Upgrades:** The $320,000 grant funds have been allocated and are ready for disbursement upon contract award.

**7. Conclusion**

All projects are proceeding in accordance with the Commission's approvals and the City's budget. The Parks and Recreation Department is committed to ensuring the successful completion of these projects within the specified timelines.

Elias Thorne, City Manager

**Project:** Boardwalk Construction - Wetland Buffer Zone
**Date of Review:** June 10, 2023
**Reviewing Agency:** City of Oakhaven Environmental Protection Division

**1. Review Overview**

The Environmental Protection Division has completed the review of the Site Impact Statement for the proposed boardwalk construction. The review was conducted in accordance with the City's Environmental Protection Ordinance Section 12.4.

**2. Key Findings**

*   **Hydrology:** The proposed boardwalk elevation of 18 inches will not impede natural water flow or alter the water table.
*   **Vegetation:** The boardwalk alignment avoids the densest clusters of native vegetation.
*   **Wildlife:** No endangered species were identified within the project area.
*   **Soil:** The soil composition is stable and suitable for the proposed construction.

**3. Recommendations**

*   **Construction Limits:** All construction activity must be confined to the designated boardwalk footprint.
*   **Timing:** Construction must be scheduled to avoid the nesting season for local bird species.
*   **Lighting:** No permanent lighting shall be installed on the boardwalk.
*   **Signage:** Interpretive signage must be installed to educate visitors.

**4. Approval Status**

The project is approved pending final signage approval.

**5. Next Steps**

*   **Final Signage:** The applicant must submit the final signage design for approval.
*   **Construction:** Construction may commence upon receipt of final signage approval.
*   **Inspection:** A final inspection will be conducted upon completion of the project.
