**DOCUMENT A: OFFICIAL MINUTES OF THE NORTHWOOD INDEPENDENT SCHOOL DISTRICT BOARD OF TRUSTEES REGULAR MEETING HELD ON SEPTEMBER 15, 2023**

**CALL TO ORDER**

The Regular Meeting of the Board of Trustees of the Northwood Independent School District was called to order by President J. Anderson at 6:00 p.m. in the District Administrative Center, Board Room A, located at 1200 Education Boulevard, Northwood, Texas.

**ROLL CALL**

The following members of the Board of Trustees were present:
J. Anderson (President)
M. Chen (Vice President)
R. Davis
L. Foster
K. Gupta
T. Harris

The following district officials were present:
Dr. Elena Rostova, Superintendent of Schools
Mr. Thomas Halloway, Chief Financial Officer
Ms. Sarah Jenkins, Board Secretary
Mr. David Miller, Legal Counsel

A quorum was declared present and the meeting was deemed open for the transaction of business.

**INVOCATION AND PLEDGE OF ALLEGIANCE**

The invocation was offered by Board Member L. Foster. The Pledge of Allegiance was recited by the Board and attendees, led by Superintendent Rostova.

**APPROVAL OF AGENDA**

A motion was made by Board Member R. Davis and seconded by Board Member K. Gupta to approve the agenda for the September 15, 2023, meeting as distributed.
Motion Carried: 6-0.

**PUBLIC COMMENT – GENERAL**

The floor was opened for general public comment. Three individuals signed up to speak.

1.  **Mr. Arthur Penhaligon**, representing the Northwood Teachers Association, addressed the Board. Mr. Penhaligon stated that the upcoming budget cycle is critical for maintaining current staffing levels. He requested that the Board ensure the "Instructional Salaries" line item reflects the cost-of-living adjustments previously discussed in the fiscal year planning committee.

2.  **Ms. Linda Vargo**, a parent of a student in the Northwood High School District, spoke regarding facility maintenance. She expressed concern that budget allocations for non-instructional areas might be insufficient given the age of the district's infrastructure.

3.  **Mr. James Kowalski**, a local business owner, offered support for the proposed budget, noting the district's role as a major employer in the region.

Following the public comment period, the Board Chair thanked the speakers and noted that all comments would be added to the official record.

**CONSENT AGENDA**

A motion was made by Board Member M. Chen and seconded by Board Member T. Harris to approve the Consent Agenda, which included:
1.  Approval of the minutes from the August 18, 2023, Regular Meeting.
2.  Approval of the August 2023 Payroll Register.
3.  Approval of the September 2023 Vendor Payments totaling $4,250,000.00.
4.  Approval of the transfer of $150,000.00 from the Technology Reserve Fund to the General Fund for immediate server upgrades.

Discussion was requested regarding item 4. Mr. Thomas Halloway, Chief Financial Officer, provided a brief explanation of the server capacity issues and the necessity of the transfer.

A motion was made to separate Item 4 from the Consent Agenda for further discussion.
Motion to Separate: Carried.

The remaining items on the Consent Agenda were voted upon.
Motion to Approve Consent Agenda (excluding Item 4): Carried.

**AGENDA ITEM 1: CONSIDERATION OF FY2024 BUDGET PROPOSAL**

**Presentation by Superintendent**
Superintendent Dr. Elena Rostova called the meeting to order for the primary agenda item. She introduced Mr. Thomas Halloway, Chief Financial Officer, to present the preliminary financial statements for the Fiscal Year 2024.

Mr. Halloway presented a comprehensive overview of the proposed budget. He highlighted that the total budget for the upcoming fiscal year is set at $145,000,000. This figure represents a 4.2% increase over the FY2023 actuals, primarily driven by inflationary pressures in the energy sector and the mandatory increase in instructional salary scales.

Mr. Halloway directed the Board's attention to the Operating Expenses section of the financial statement. He specifically noted the line item for "Instructional Salaries," which is proposed at $98,500,000. He stated that this amount constitutes 68% of the total budget, maintaining the district's long-standing commitment to prioritizing direct classroom instruction over administrative overhead.

Mr. Halloway detailed the breakdown of the $98,500,000 allocation:
*   Base Salaries: $72,000,000
*   Step Increases: $12,500,000
*   Longevity Pay: $4,000,000
*   Special Education Incentives: $3,500,000
*   Teacher Retirement System Contributions: $6,500,000

He further explained that the remaining 32% of the budget is allocated to facilities maintenance, transportation, food services, technology infrastructure, and administrative operations.

**Discussion**
Board Member R. Davis asked for clarification on the 68% allocation to instructional salaries. Mr. Halloway responded that this percentage is consistent with the district's strategic plan goals to maximize classroom resources. He noted that a reduction in this percentage would require a corresponding reduction in instructional staff or an increase in class sizes, neither of which is recommended by the district's strategic planning committee.

Board Member L. Foster inquired about the contingency funds included in the budget. Mr. Halloway stated that a contingency reserve of $2.5 million is included within the general fund, to be utilized only in the event of significant revenue shortfalls or unforeseen emergency repairs.

Board Member K. Gupta questioned the impact of the proposed budget on the district's credit rating. Mr. Halloway assured the Board that the financial structure remains sound, with a debt service coverage ratio well above the required threshold for municipal bond issuance.

Board Member T. Harris raised a concern regarding the timing of the budget adoption relative to the start of the school year. Superintendent Rostova confirmed that the budget must be adopted by September 30, 2023, to ensure all payroll systems are updated for the October 1st fiscal year start.

**MOTION TO ADOPT BUDGET**
Superintendent Rostova moved to adopt the FY2024 Budget Proposal as presented, totaling $145,000,000, with the specific allocation of $98,500,000 to Instructional Salaries, representing 68% of the total budget.

A second was made by Board Member M. Chen.

The Board proceeded to a roll-call vote on the motion to adopt the FY2024 Budget.

**ROLL CALL VOTE**

1.  **J. Anderson (President):** Aye
2.  **M. Chen (Vice President):** Aye
3.  **R. Davis:** Aye
4.  **L. Foster:** Nay
5.  **K. Gupta:** Aye
6.  **T. Harris:** Nay

**VOTE RESULT**
The motion carried with a vote count of 5-2.

**ADJOURNMENT**
There being no further business, a motion was made by Board Member R. Davis and seconded by Board Member K. Gupta to adjourn the meeting.
Motion Carried.
The meeting was adjourned at 7:45 p.m.
