SUBJECT: PRELIMINARY REVIEW OF VARIANCE REQUEST 24-010

This staff report summarizes the preliminary review of the application submitted by Crestview Holdings LLC. The application was received on June 1, 2024. The request type is Variance for Density. The property address is 1500 River Road. The applicant seeks to increase the allowable density on the lot beyond the current zoning restrictions. The current zoning allows for a maximum of 10 units per acre. The applicant requests a variance to allow for 15 units per acre.

The staff reviewed the parking documentation submitted with the application. The documentation provided was incomplete. The applicant failed to provide the required parking space calculations for the additional units. The staff noted insufficient parking documentation. The current zoning code requires 1.5 parking spaces per unit. The applicant proposed 1.2 parking spaces per unit. This proposal does not meet the minimum requirement.

The site plan shows the proposed building footprint. The building footprint covers 60% of the lot area. The maximum allowed coverage is 50%. The applicant requested a variance for lot coverage as well. The site plan shows a setback of 5 feet from the property line. The required setback is 10 feet. The applicant requested a variance for the setback requirement.

The environmental review indicates no impact on flood zones. The property is not located within a floodplain. The soil report indicates stable soil conditions. The geotechnical report was submitted on May 30, 2024. The report number is GEOT-2024-0530-02. The report was reviewed by the Engineering Department. The Engineering Department has no objections to the structural design. The Planning Department has objections regarding the parking and density.

The applicant was notified of the deficiencies on June 10, 2024. The applicant was given 10 days to correct the documentation. The deadline for correction was June 20, 2024. The applicant did not submit the corrected documentation. The applicant response was None. The staff recommends denial of the variance request. The denial is based on the failure to meet the minimum parking requirements and the lack of supporting documentation for the lot coverage variance.

The staff recommends that the Zoning Board of Appeals vote to deny variance 24-010. The denial will maintain the current zoning standards for the area. The current standards protect the character of the neighborhood. The standards ensure adequate infrastructure capacity. The standards ensure safe pedestrian access. The standards ensure adequate sunlight for adjacent properties.

The staff report was distributed to the Zoning Board members on June 20, 2024. The members received the report via email and the online portal. The report includes the site plan, the parking calculations, and the soil report. The report is available for public inspection. The report is included in the meeting packet for the June 22, 2024 session. The staff recommends that the Board review the application at the regular meeting. The Board may request additional information from the applicant. The Board may request a public hearing. The Board may vote to approve or deny the request.
