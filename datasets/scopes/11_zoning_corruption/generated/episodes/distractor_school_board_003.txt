**AGENDA ITEM 1: CURRICULUM REVIEW AND ADOPTION**

**Subject:** Review and Recommendation regarding the Advanced STEM Initiative Mathematics Standards.
**Presented by:** Dr. Aris Thorne, Director of Curriculum and Instruction.

Dr. Aris Thorne took the podium to present the Curriculum Review Report regarding the proposed adoption of new mathematics standards for the Advanced STEM Initiative. He introduced the report as a comprehensive analysis of the current curriculum's alignment with state and national standards, specifically focusing on the needs of grades 9 through 12.

Dr. Thorne explained that the Advanced STEM Initiative was originally launched in the 2020-2021 academic year to provide advanced coursework for students interested in engineering, computer science, and physical sciences. Over the past three years, the initiative has seen a 35% increase in student enrollment. However, the current curriculum, which relies on a combination of three different textbook providers, has created inconsistencies in learning outcomes.

The Director presented the findings of the Curriculum Review Report, specifically the section titled "Mathematics Standards." The report detailed a gap analysis between the current district standards and the new proposed standards. The analysis indicated that the current curriculum lacks sufficient depth in calculus preparation for AP Calculus BC and does not adequately integrate computational thinking skills required for modern engineering fields.

Dr. Thorne introduced the vendor proposal submitted by **EduTech Solutions LLC**. He stated that EduTech Solutions LLC has been selected as the primary vendor for the new curriculum package based on a competitive bidding process initiated in June 2023. The vendor proposed a comprehensive suite of materials including digital textbooks, laboratory simulation software, and teacher professional development modules.

The adoption cost for the Advanced STEM Initiative, as outlined in the financial breakdown provided by the vendor, is **$1,250,000**. Dr. Thorne clarified that this figure represents the total cost for the first three years of implementation, covering the purchase of licenses, hardware integration, and the initial training for all 45 teachers assigned to the program. The cost is broken down as follows:
*   Digital Curriculum Licenses (Grades 9-12): $650,000
*   Laboratory Simulation Software: $300,000
*   Teacher Professional Development and Training: $200,000
*   Hardware Integration and Maintenance: $100,000

The report noted that the program is designated for **grade levels 9-12**. The implementation is scheduled to begin in the Spring 2024 semester for the 2024-2025 academic year, with a phased rollout starting with 9th-grade Algebra II and 10th-grade Geometry.

Dr. Thorne presented the rationale for the adoption. He cited data from the Texas Education Agency (TEA) indicating that the Northwood Independent School District ranks in the 75th percentile for mathematics proficiency in high school, but only the 40th percentile for advanced STEM course completion. The new standards proposed by EduTech Solutions LLC are designed to bridge this gap by introducing early exposure to college-level concepts and integrating real-world problem-solving scenarios.

He also highlighted the pedagogical approach of the new curriculum. The materials emphasize inquiry-based learning, where students utilize data from real-world sources to solve complex problems. The curriculum includes a dedicated module on data science and machine learning, which is not currently present in the district's standard mathematics sequence.

Dr. Thorne addressed the financial implications. He noted that the $1,250,000 cost would be drawn from the district's Technology and Innovation Fund, which has a surplus of $1,500,000 for the current fiscal year. No tax levy increase is required for this adoption. The District Finance Officer, Linda Gentry, confirmed the availability of funds in the Technology and Innovation Fund.

The Director of Curriculum and Instruction then opened the floor for a Q&A session with the Board of Trustees regarding the curriculum details.

**Trustee O'Connell** asked about the specific changes to the calculus sequence. Dr. Thorne responded that the new curriculum introduces a "Calculus Readiness" course in 11th grade, which is not currently offered. This course is designed to prepare students for AP Calculus BC in their senior year. The new standards require a 20% increase in problem-solving time during class periods, reducing direct instruction time by approximately 10 minutes per period.

**Trustee Kowalski** inquired about the compatibility of the new software with existing district hardware. Dr. Thorne assured the board that EduTech Solutions LLC has certified the software for compatibility with the current fleet of 2,500 Chromebooks and the 500 desktop computers in the science labs. A pilot program was conducted in the fall of 2023 at Northwood High School with 50 students, and the feedback indicated a 15% improvement in engagement scores compared to the previous curriculum.

**Trustee Martinez** asked about the professional development component. Dr. Thorne explained that the $200,000 allocation covers a 40-hour training program for all participating teachers. The training will be conducted in three phases: an intensive two-week summer institute in June 2024, followed by monthly workshops throughout the fall and spring semesters. The vendor will provide a dedicated curriculum coach for each high school campus to support teachers during the transition.

**Trustee Miller** raised a question regarding the long-term sustainability of the program. He asked if there are any recurring costs beyond the initial three-year license. Dr. Thorne clarified that the $1,250,000 cost includes a three-year license renewal. After the three-year period, the district would need to budget for an annual renewal fee, estimated at $250,000. This would be a recurring line item in the technology budget.

**Student Trustee Chen** asked about the impact on students who are not currently enrolled in the Advanced STEM Initiative. Dr. Thorne explained that the new standards are designed to be modular. While the core curriculum is targeted at the Advanced STEM Initiative, the district plans to create a "STEM Foundation" track for general education students that incorporates the same inquiry-based learning methods but at a lower intensity. This would allow for a broader application of the new pedagogical tools across the district.

Following the Q&A, Dr. Aris Thorne concluded his presentation. He recommended that the Board of Trustees adopt the new Mathematics Standards for the Advanced STEM Initiative, approve the vendor selection of **EduTech Solutions LLC**, and authorize the expenditure of **$1,250,000** for the implementation of the program for **grades 9-12**.

**NORTHWOOD INDEPENDENT SCHOOL DISTRICT**
**CURRICULUM REVIEW REPORT**
**SECTION: MATHEMATICS STANDARDS**
**PROGRAM NAME: ADVANCED STEM INITIATIVE**

**DATE:** November 8, 2023
**PREPARED BY:** Dr. Aris Thorne, Director of Curriculum and Instruction
**SUBMITTED TO:** Board of Trustees, Northwood Independent School District

**1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY**

This report provides a comprehensive review of the current mathematics standards utilized within the Advanced STEM Initiative at the Northwood Independent School District (NISD). The review was conducted in response to the district's strategic goal of enhancing STEM education outcomes and aligning with national standards. The report details the findings of a gap analysis, the proposed adoption of new standards, and the associated financial implications.

**2. CURRENT CURRICULUM STATUS**

The Advanced STEM Initiative, established in the 2020-2021 academic year, serves students in grades 9 through 12. The program currently utilizes a hybrid curriculum consisting of three different textbook providers. While the program has seen a 35% increase in enrollment, the current curriculum has been identified as lacking depth in several key areas, particularly in calculus preparation and the integration of computational thinking.

The gap analysis conducted by the Curriculum and Instruction department revealed the following deficiencies:
*   **Calculus Preparation:** The current curriculum does not provide sufficient preparation for AP Calculus BC, resulting in a lower pass rate compared to the state average.
*   **Computational Thinking:** There is a lack of integration of data science and machine learning concepts, which are increasingly important in the modern workforce.
*   **Inconsistency:** The use of multiple providers has led to inconsistencies in learning outcomes across different campuses.

**3. PROPOSED CURRICULUM ADOPTION**

Based on the gap analysis, the Curriculum and Instruction department recommends the adoption of new mathematics standards developed by **EduTech Solutions LLC**. The proposed standards are designed to address the identified deficiencies and align with the district's strategic goals.

**3.1. Program Details**
*   **Program Name:** Advanced STEM Initiative
*   **Grade Levels:** 9-12
*   **Vendor:** EduTech Solutions LLC
*   **Adoption Cost:** $1,250,000
*   **Implementation Timeline:** Spring 2024 (Pilot) to Fall 2024 (Full Rollout)

**3.2. Curriculum Components**
The new curriculum package includes the following components:
*   **Digital Textbooks:** Interactive digital textbooks that provide real-time feedback and adaptive learning paths.
*   **Laboratory Simulation Software:** Software that allows students to conduct virtual experiments and simulations, enhancing their understanding of mathematical concepts.
*   **Professional Development:** A comprehensive training program for teachers, including a 40-hour summer institute and monthly workshops throughout the academic year.
*   **Data Analytics Dashboard:** A dashboard that allows teachers to track student progress and identify areas of need.

**3.3. Alignment with Standards**
The new standards are aligned with the Texas Essential Knowledge and Skills (TEKS) for Mathematics and the Common Core State Standards for Mathematics. Additionally, the curriculum incorporates elements of the Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS) to ensure a cohesive STEM experience.

**4. FINANCIAL IMPLICATIONS**

The adoption of the new curriculum will require an initial investment of **$1,250,000**. This cost is broken down as follows:
*   **Digital Curriculum Licenses (Grades 9-12):** $650,000
*   **Laboratory Simulation Software:** $300,000
*   **Teacher Professional Development and Training:** $200,000
*   **Hardware Integration and Maintenance:** $100,000

The funds for this adoption will be drawn from the district's Technology and Innovation Fund, which currently has a surplus of $1,500,000. No tax levy increase is required for this adoption.

**5. IMPLEMENTATION PLAN**

The implementation of the new curriculum will be phased over a two-year period.
*   **Phase 1 (Fall 2023):** Pilot program with 50 students at Northwood High School.
*   **Phase 2 (Spring 2024):** Rollout to all 9th-grade students in the Advanced STEM Initiative.
*   **Phase 3 (Fall 2024):** Full rollout to all grade levels (9-12) in the Advanced STEM Initiative.

**6. RECOMMENDATION**

Based on the findings of this review, the Curriculum and Instruction department recommends the adoption of the new mathematics standards for the Advanced STEM Initiative. The Board of Trustees is requested to approve the vendor selection of **EduTech Solutions LLC** and authorize the expenditure of **$1,250,000** for the implementation of the program for **grades 9-12**.

**7. CONCLUSION**

The adoption of the new mathematics standards is a critical step in ensuring that Northwood Independent School District remains at the forefront of STEM education. The new curriculum will provide students with the skills and knowledge they need to succeed in college and the workforce. The district is committed to providing the necessary resources and support to ensure the successful implementation of this initiative.

**ENVIRONMENTAL REVIEW SUMMARY**
**PROJECT: ADVANCED STEM INITIATIVE**

**1. OVERVIEW**

This environmental review summary provides an assessment of the potential environmental impacts associated with the implementation of the Advanced STEM Initiative. The review was conducted in accordance with the National Environmental Policy Act (NEPA) and the Texas Environmental Policy Act (TEPA).

**2. PROJECT DESCRIPTION**

The Advanced STEM Initiative involves the adoption of new mathematics standards and the implementation of a digital curriculum. The project includes the purchase of digital textbooks, laboratory simulation software, and the provision of professional development for teachers.

**3. ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACTS**

**3.1. Air Quality**
The project is not expected to have any significant impacts on air quality. The digital nature of the curriculum will reduce the need for paper textbooks, which could lead to a slight reduction in paper waste and associated emissions from paper production.

**3.2. Water Quality**
The project is not expected to have any significant impacts on water quality. The digital curriculum will not require any additional water usage.

**3.3. Waste Management**
The project is expected to have a positive impact on waste management. The reduction in paper textbook usage will lead to a decrease in paper waste. The district will implement a recycling program to further reduce waste.

**3.4. Energy Consumption**
The project may lead to a slight increase in energy consumption due to the increased use of computers and other electronic devices. However, the district plans to implement energy-efficient practices, such as using energy-efficient computers and turning off devices when not in use.

**4. MITIGATION MEASURES**

To mitigate the potential environmental impacts, the district will implement the following measures:
*   **Paper Reduction:** The district will encourage the use of digital textbooks and reduce the use of paper materials.
*   **Energy Efficiency:** The district will implement energy-efficient practices, such as using energy-efficient computers and turning off devices when not in use.
*   **Recycling:** The district will implement a recycling program to reduce waste.

**5. CONCLUSION**

The implementation of the Advanced STEM Initiative is not expected to have any significant negative environmental impacts. The district has implemented measures to mitigate any potential impacts and is committed to minimizing its environmental footprint.

**SUBJECT:** Budget Allocation for Advanced STEM Initiative

The purpose of this memo is to confirm the availability of funds for the adoption of the new mathematics standards for the Advanced STEM Initiative.

**2. BACKGROUND**

The Curriculum and Instruction department has recommended the adoption of new mathematics standards developed by **EduTech Solutions LLC**. The total cost for the adoption is **$1,250,000**.

**3. FINANCIAL STATUS**

The district's Technology and Innovation Fund currently has a surplus of **$1,500,000**. The proposed expenditure of **$1,250,000** will leave a remaining balance of **$250,000** in the fund.

**4. RECOMMENDATION**

Based on the current financial status, the Finance Department recommends that the Board of Trustees approve the expenditure of **$1,250,000** for the adoption of the new mathematics standards.

**5. NEXT STEPS**

Upon approval, the Finance Department will process the payment to **EduTech Solutions LLC** and update the district's budget records.
