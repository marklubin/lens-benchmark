**STAFF REPORT: WINTER OPERATIONS AND INFRASTRUCTURE READINESS**
Director Thomas H. Vance took the podium to present the staff report regarding winter operations planning for the upcoming season. The report covered snow removal logistics, ice resurfacing requirements for the outdoor rink, and equipment maintenance schedules.

Director Vance stated, "The primary objective for the winter season is the preservation of safety and accessibility for our three major recreational facilities: the Central Ice Arena, the Northridge Community Park, and the Riverside Trail System. Our current inventory of snow removal equipment is aging. The plow trucks assigned to the Northridge sector have accumulated over 12,000 miles since their last major overhaul. Furthermore, the ice resurfacing machine, Unit #442, has a documented history of hydraulic fluid leaks that have required frequent patching."

The Director presented a cost-benefit analysis for the proposed equipment lease. "We are proposing a lease agreement with 'Arctic Solutions LLC' for two heavy-duty snow plows and one ice resurfacing unit for a period of twelve months, commencing November 1, 2023. The total cost of the lease is $145,000. This arrangement includes full maintenance coverage and a 24-hour response time for mechanical failures, which is critical during the peak winter months when road and facility access are compromised by weather events."

Director Vance continued, "Staff recommends this lease over a purchase because the capital expenditure required for purchasing new units exceeds the current fiscal year's capital reserve allocation. The lease option allows us to meet our operational mandates without depleting funds designated for the upcoming park renovation projects. The lease terms are non-cancelable except in the event of force majeure. The vendor, Arctic Solutions LLC, has a rating of A- from the municipal bond rating agency."

**PUBLIC COMMENT: WINTER OPERATIONS**
The floor was opened to public comment.

**Comment 1:** James P. O'Brien, resident of 402 Elm Street.
Mr. O'Brien stated, "I am concerned about the timing of the equipment lease. The city has a history of delaying snow removal until after the first major storm. If we lease this equipment, I want to know if the drivers will be trained on the new systems. The last time we had a new plow, the operator crashed it into a light pole because he wasn't familiar with the hydraulics. I request that the commission require a training certificate before the equipment is deployed."

Director Vance responded, "The lease agreement with Arctic Solutions LLC includes a mandatory three-day training program for all city operators. This is a contractual obligation. We will not deploy the new units until the training is complete and verified by the vendor."

**Comment 2:** Maria Gonzalez, resident of 889 Oakhaven Drive.
Ms. Gonzalez stated, "The ice resurfacing machine is the heart of our winter season. If that machine fails, the rink is closed. I am worried that a lease might mean we are renting a machine that is already at the end of its lifecycle. I suggest we look at a purchase option at the end of the year instead."

Commissioner Chen asked, "Does the lease include a purchase option at the end of the term?"
Director Vance replied, "Yes, Commissioner. The lease includes a fair market value purchase option at the end of the twelve-month term. This gives the city flexibility to buy the equipment if it proves reliable, or return it if it does not meet our needs."

**Comment 3:** Robert Miller, resident of 112 Pine Lane.
Mr. Miller stated, "I have been a park user for twenty years. I believe the city is spending too much on equipment and not enough on the actual ice quality. The ice has been thin the last two years. I think we should divert some of this lease money to a new ice plant rather than just renting a plow."

Commissioner Rodriguez interjected, "Mr. Miller, please address the motion at hand. We are discussing the lease for snow removal and ice resurfacing equipment, not the ice plant."

**STAFF RECOMMENDATION**
Director Vance concluded the presentation by stating, "Staff recommends the approval of the lease agreement with Arctic Solutions LLC for the sum of $145,000. This is necessary to ensure the continuity of winter operations and the safety of park users. The Finance Director has verified that the funds are available within the Parks and Recreation Operating Budget under Line Item 405-22. The contract will be reviewed by the City Attorney prior to execution."

**MOTION TO APPROVE EQUIPMENT LEASE**
Commissioner Thorne moved to approve the lease agreement with Arctic Solutions LLC for the sum of $145,000, as recommended by staff, with the condition that the training program be completed prior to deployment.
Commissioner Jenkins seconded the motion.

**DEBATE**

However, I understand the cash flow constraints. I will vote nay on this motion because I believe we should pursue a different financing strategy, perhaps a capital bond measure."

Commissioner Chen: "I support the motion. The lease provides immediate access to reliable equipment. The training requirement addresses the safety concerns raised by Mr. O'Brien. The purchase option provides future flexibility."

Commissioner Jenkins: "I support the motion. The staff analysis clearly shows that purchasing is not feasible within the current budget. We must ensure the rink is open."

Commissioner Thorne: "I support the motion. The conditions regarding training are acceptable. We are moving forward with the lease."

### DOCUMENT 2: PERMIT APPLICATION AND ARCHITECTURAL SUMMARY
**CITY OF OAKHAVEN**
**BUILDING AND SAFETY DEPARTMENT**
**PERMIT APPLICATION SUBMISSION**

**APPLICATION NUMBER:** 2023-BLD-11-0058
**DATE SUBMITTED:** November 5, 2023
**DATE OF RECEIPT:** November 5, 2023
**STATUS:** Under Review / Pending Final Approval

**PROJECT DETAILS**
**Project Name:** Central Ice Arena Mechanical System Upgrade
**Applicant:** City of Oakhaven, Parks and Recreation Department
**Property Owner:** City of Oakhaven
**Property Address:** 500 Civic Center Drive, Oakhaven, CA 94000
**Parcel ID:** 9900-Alpha
**Project Type:** Public Facility Renovation / Mechanical System Upgrade
**Estimated Completion Date:** February 28, 2024
**Total Estimated Cost:** $180,000.00
**Funding Source:** Parks and Recreation Operating Budget, Line Item 405-22

**SCOPE OF WORK**
The applicant requests a building permit to renovate the mechanical refrigeration and ventilation systems of the Central Ice Arena. The existing system, installed in 1998, has reached the end of its operational life cycle. The proposed work includes the removal of the current ammonia-based refrigeration units, the installation of a new direct-expansion glycol system, and the upgrade of the ventilation ductwork to meet current energy efficiency standards.

**TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS**
1.  **Refrigeration System:** Installation of a new 150-ton Direct Expansion (DX) Glycol Chiller Unit. The new unit will utilize R-448A refrigerant, which is compliant with the latest Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) regulations regarding ozone depletion. The system will be connected to the existing concrete foundation with new vibration isolation pads.
2.  **Piping and Valving:** Replacement of all copper and steel piping within the arena floor slab. New piping will consist of high-density polyethylene (HDPE) insulated tubing with a 2-inch diameter. All valves will be ball valves with remote actuation capabilities for emergency shut-off.
3.  **Ventilation:** Upgrade of the HVAC system to include variable air volume (VAV) boxes. The new system will maintain a relative humidity level between 40% and 60% and a temperature of 55°F (12.8°C) within the arena.
4.  **Electrical:** Installation of a new 480V, 3-phase electrical panel dedicated to the mechanical system. This includes the replacement of all control wiring and the integration of a Building Automation System (BAS) for remote monitoring.
5.  **Ice Rink Surface:** Minor resurfacing of the concrete slab to accommodate the new piping layout. The existing urethane coating will be stripped and replaced with a new high-gloss urethane finish.

**ARCHITECTURAL SUMMARY**
**Prepared by:** Oakhaven Architectural Group, LLC
**Architect of Record:** Jonathan P. Miller, AIA, License No. CA-23456
**Date of Architectural Summary:** November 1, 2023

**1.0 Existing Conditions**
The Central Ice Arena is a single-story structure with a steel truss roof and concrete block walls. The building footprint is 120 feet by 60 feet. The current mechanical room is located in the northeast corner of the building. The existing refrigeration system is housed in a separate exterior enclosure that has shown signs of corrosion. The interior of the arena features a 20,000 square foot ice surface surrounded by a 10-foot wide spectator walkway. The existing ventilation system is insufficient to handle the humidity load generated by the ice, leading to condensation on the ceiling and walls.

**2.0 Proposed Modifications**
The proposed modifications involve the interior of the mechanical room and the sub-slab area. No changes are proposed to the exterior façade, roof structure, or building footprint. The new mechanical equipment will be installed within the existing mechanical room footprint. The new refrigeration unit will be placed on a raised concrete pad to facilitate maintenance access. The ventilation ductwork will be rerouted through the existing ceiling plenum.

**2.1 Mechanical Room Layout**
The new refrigeration unit will occupy 15% of the existing mechanical room floor space. The remaining space will be utilized for the new electrical panel and the BAS control cabinet. The layout ensures a minimum clearance of 3 feet around all equipment for maintenance access, in compliance with the International Mechanical Code (IMC) Section 304.

**2.2 Sub-Slab Piping**
The new HDPE piping will be installed in a serpentine pattern beneath the concrete slab. The depth of the piping will be 12 inches below the top of the slab. The piping will be encased in a 2-inch layer of sand to prevent abrasion. The connection points to the ice surface will be sealed with a waterproof epoxy compound.

**2.3 Ceiling and Ventilation**
The existing ceiling tiles will be removed to allow for the installation of new ventilation ducts. The new ducts will be insulated with 2-inch fiberglass insulation to prevent condensation. The ducts will be suspended from the existing steel trusses using vibration isolators. The air supply will be directed towards the spectator areas to minimize drafts on the ice surface.

**3.0 Compliance and Standards**
The proposed design complies with the following codes and standards:
*   California Building Code (CBC) 2022 Edition
*   International Mechanical Code (IMC) 2021 Edition
*   National Electrical Code (NEC) 2020 Edition
*   City of Oakhaven Zoning Ordinance
*   American Society of Heating, Refrigerating and Air-Conditioning Engineers (ASHRAE) Standard 90.1-2019

**4.0 Structural Considerations**
A structural engineer's report has been prepared by Structural Dynamics, Inc. The report confirms that the existing foundation and roof structure can support the additional weight of the new mechanical equipment. The new equipment will be distributed evenly across the mechanical room floor to prevent point loading. The roof trusses will not be modified.

**5.0 Environmental Considerations**
The new refrigeration system utilizes R-448A, a low-global warming potential (GWP) refrigerant. The system is designed to operate at 20% higher efficiency than the current system, resulting in a projected energy savings of 15,000 kWh per year. The project will not generate any hazardous waste, as the old refrigerant will be recovered and disposed of by a certified recycling firm.

**6.0 Construction Timeline**
The construction is scheduled to begin on December 15, 2023, and is expected to be completed by February 28, 2024. The work will be performed during off-hours (10:00 P.M. to 6:00 A.M.) to minimize disruption to public use of the facility. The ice resurfacing machine will be leased during the construction period to ensure the rink can be prepared for the final ice pour upon completion.

**7.0 Safety Measures**
The contractor will implement the following safety measures:
*   Barricading of the work area to prevent public access.
*   Use of personal protective equipment (PPE) including hard hats, safety glasses, and gloves.
*   Continuous air monitoring for refrigerant leaks.
*   Fire suppression system activation during welding operations.

**8.0 Cost Estimate**
The total estimated cost of the project is $180,000. This includes:
*   Equipment and Materials: $110,000
*   Labor: $55,000
*   Permits and Fees: $5,000
*   Contingency (10%): $10,000

**CITY OF OAKHAVEN**

**INTER-DEPARTMENTAL MEMORANDUM**

**TO:**
Director Thomas H. Vance, Parks and Recreation

**FROM:**

**DATE:** December 1, 2023
**SUBJECT:** Safety Protocol Update - Park Access Points
**REFERENCE:** City Council Resolution 2023-15
**ATTACHMENTS:**
1.  Site Plan for North Entrance Signage
2.  Lighting Inspection Checklist

**1.0 PURPOSE**
The purpose of this memorandum is to establish updated safety protocols for the North Entrance of the Central Park and to coordinate the installation of new signage and lighting improvements. This initiative is in response to a recent increase in after-hours incidents at the park and the City Council's directive to enhance public safety in recreational areas.

**2.0 SIGNAGE INSTALLATION**
The Parks and Recreation Department is responsible for the design and installation of new signage at the North Entrance. The signage will include:
*   Park Hours of Operation (6:00 A.M. to 10:00 P.M.)
*   Prohibited Activities (Alcohol, Pets, Fires)
*   Emergency Contact Information (911 and Park Ranger Dispatch)
*   "No Trespassing" Warning

The signage will be manufactured from high-density polyethylene (HDPE) with reflective lettering to ensure visibility during low-light conditions. The signs will be mounted on galvanized steel posts, 8 feet in height, and will be installed at a distance of 50 feet from the entrance gate.

**3.0 LIGHTING INSPECTION AND COORDINATION**
The Public Works Department, in coordination with the Police Department, is responsible for the inspection of the existing lighting fixtures at the North Entrance. The current lighting system consists of four 150-watt metal halide fixtures, which are approaching the end of their useful life.

**3.1 Inspection Protocol**
The Police Department will conduct a joint inspection with Public Works on December 5, 2023. The inspection will focus on:
*   Illumination levels at the entrance and surrounding pathways.
*   Functionality of all lighting fixtures.
*   Presence of shadows or dark spots that could conceal unauthorized activity.
*   Condition of the electrical wiring and control boxes.

**3.2 Upgrade Requirements**
If the inspection reveals deficiencies, the Public Works Department will replace the existing fixtures with LED luminaires. The new fixtures will provide 2,000 lumens of light and will be equipped with motion sensors to reduce energy consumption during off-peak hours. The installation will be completed by December 15, 2023.

**4.0 POLICE DEPARTMENT COORDINATION**
The Police Department is requested to:
*   Increase patrol frequency at the North Entrance between the hours of 9:00 P.M. and 10:00 P.M.
*   Review the park's security camera footage for the past three months to identify any patterns of activity.
*   Provide a written report on the effectiveness of the new lighting and signage by January 15, 2024.

**5.0 TIMELINE**
*   **December 5, 2023:** Joint lighting inspection.
*   **December 8, 2023:** Finalize signage design and order materials.
*   **December 12, 2023:** Install new signage.
*   **December 15, 2023:** Complete lighting upgrades (if necessary).
*   **January 15, 2024:** Submit final report to the City Manager.

**6.0 BUDGET IMPLICATIONS**
The cost of the signage installation is estimated at $2,500. The cost of the lighting upgrade is estimated at $4,000. These costs will be covered by the Public Safety Fund, Line Item 305-10.

### DOCUMENT 6: ENVIRONMENTAL REVIEW SUMMARY
**CITY OF OAKHAVEN**
**ENVIRONMENTAL REVIEW OFFICE**
**ENVIRONMENTAL ASSESSMENT SUMMARY**

**PROJECT:** Central Ice Arena Mechanical System Upgrade
**PROJECT ID:** 2023-ENV-11-005
**DATE OF PREPARATION:** November 10, 2023
**PREPARED BY:** Environmental Review Office, City of Oakhaven

**1.0 PROJECT DESCRIPTION**
The proposed project involves the renovation of the mechanical refrigeration and ventilation systems of the Central Ice Arena. The project includes the removal of the existing ammonia-based refrigeration units and the installation of a new direct-expansion glycol system. The project also includes the upgrade of the ventilation ductwork and the installation of a new electrical panel.

**2.0 ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACTS**
**2.1 Air Quality**

The construction phase will involve the use of diesel-powered equipment, which may result in temporary increases in particulate matter and nitrogen oxides. **2.5 Energy Consumption**
