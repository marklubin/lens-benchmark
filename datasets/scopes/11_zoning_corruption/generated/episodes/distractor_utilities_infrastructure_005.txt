### DOCUMENT A: PERMIT APPLICATION AND ARCHITECTURAL SUMMARY
**FORM:** ZONING-UTIL-2024
**DATE OF FILING:** June 1, 2024
**APPLICATION NUMBER:** 24-005-UTIL
**APPLICANT:** City of Oakhaven, Department of Public Works
**REPRESENTATIVE:** Marcus Thorne, Senior Civil Engineer
**PROJECT TITLE:** Structural Reinforcement and Pump Replacement at Wastewater Station C
**PARCEL IDENTIFICATION:** WW-5522
**ZONING DESIGNATION:** I-2 (Industrial, Heavy)
**ADDRESS:** 4400 Industrial Parkway, Oakhaven, CA (Sector 4)

**I. APPLICATION SUMMARY**

The Department of Public Works submits this application for a Special Use Permit and Building Permit modification regarding the existing Wastewater Station C facility located on Parcel ID: WW-5522. The subject property is currently utilized for the collection, pumping, and preliminary treatment of municipal wastewater. The facility is situated in a designated industrial zone, bordered by the Oakhaven River to the south, the R-3 residential zone to the north (separated by a 150-foot buffer), and the R-2 residential zone to the west.

The proposed work constitutes a capital improvement project aimed at addressing critical infrastructure degradation and modernizing pump efficiency. The application requests approval for structural reinforcement of the existing concrete pump house and the complete replacement of the primary lift station pumps.

**II. PROJECT SCOPE AND TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS**

**A. Structural Reinforcement and Concrete Repair**
The existing pump house structure, constructed in 1988, has exhibited signs of concrete spalling and rebar corrosion due to prolonged exposure to hydrogen sulfide gas and moisture. The proposed scope includes the demolition and removal of approximately 450 square feet of deteriorated concrete wall sections. New structural reinforcement will be installed using high-strength, sulfate-resistant concrete mix (designated as Type II) with a compressive strength of 5,000 psi. The reinforcement protocol involves the installation of epoxy-coated steel rebar cages and the application of a silicate-based penetrating sealer to the exterior and interior surfaces. All demolition and reconstruction activities are to be conducted within the existing building footprint; no expansion of the building envelope is proposed.

**B. Pump Replacement and Variable Frequency Drive (VFD) Installation**
The project scope mandates the removal of three (3) existing submersible centrifugal pumps, which have reached the end of their operational lifespan. These units are being replaced with three (3) new high-efficiency, non-clogging centrifugal pumps manufactured by Grundfos, Model U-5000. Each new pump is rated for a flow capacity of 1,200 gallons per minute (GPM) at a total dynamic head of 85 feet.

A critical component of this project is the installation of Variable Frequency Drives (VFDs) for all three pump units. The VFDs will allow for precise control of pump speed based on real-time flow data and tank levels, eliminating the energy inefficiencies associated with traditional fixed-speed motor operation. The electrical upgrade includes the installation of a new 480-volt distribution panel, dedicated transformer upgrades, and the integration of the VFDs with the facility's existing SCADA (Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition) system.

**C. Odor Control and Ventilation**
To address historical odor complaints and ensure compliance with state air quality standards, the project scope includes the installation of a new negative pressure ventilation system. This system will utilize activated carbon filtration units and bio-scrubbers to treat exhaust air. The design intent is to significantly reduce the concentration of hydrogen sulfide and other volatile organic compounds (VOCs) released from the pump station.

**D. Timeline and Phasing**
The total project duration is estimated at 18 months from the date of permit issuance. The project is divided into three distinct phases:
1.  **Phase I (Months 1-6):** Mobilization, site setup, structural demolition, and concrete repair.
2.  **Phase II (Months 7-12):** Installation of new pump units, electrical upgrades, and VFD integration.
3.  **Phase III (Months 13-18):** Testing, commissioning, odor control system activation, and final site restoration.

**III. COST ESTIMATES AND FUNDING**

The total estimated cost for the project is $980,000. This figure includes all labor, materials, equipment, engineering oversight, permitting fees, and contingency reserves.

**Cost Breakdown:**
*   **Demolition and Site Preparation:** $125,000
*   **Concrete Repair and Structural Reinforcement:** $210,000
*   **Pump Equipment (3 units):** $285,000
*   **Electrical and VFD Systems:** $175,000
*   **Ventilation and Odor Control:** $110,000
*   **Engineering and Project Management:** $55,000
*   **Contingency (10%):** $20,000

Funding for this project will be drawn from the Municipal Infrastructure Reserve Fund, specifically Allocation 2024-09 (Capital Improvements). No additional tax levies or bond measures are required for this specific undertaking.

**IV. COMPLIANCE AND ZONING ANALYSIS**

The proposed work is consistent with the City of Oakhaven Zoning Ordinance Section 15.04.030 regarding Industrial Utility Facilities. The project does not alter the use of the parcel, nor does it increase the density or intensity of operations beyond current permitted levels. The structural modifications are internal and do not affect the building's external appearance or setbacks.

The project complies with the California Building Code (CBC) and the National Electrical Code (NEC). The engineering design has been reviewed and approved by the City's Structural Division. The project meets the requirements for environmental screening as outlined in the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA) guidelines, specifically the determination of a negative declaration, as no significant environmental impacts are anticipated (refer to attached Environmental Review Summary, Document B).

### DOCUMENT B: ENVIRONMENTAL REVIEW SUMMARY
**FORM:** ENV-2024-REVIEW
**DATE OF PREPARATION:** June 10, 2024
**PREPARED BY:** Environmental Compliance Division
**REVIEWING OFFICER:** Dr. Elena Soto, Environmental Scientist
**PROJECT REFERENCE:** 24-005-UTIL
**LOCATION:** Wastewater Station C, Parcel ID: WW-5522
**SUBJECT:** Odor Control Impact Assessment and Air Quality Monitoring

**I. INTRODUCTION**

This Environmental Review Summary provides an assessment of the potential environmental impacts associated with the proposed Wastewater Pumping Station Overhaul project at Parcel ID: WW-5522. The primary focus of this review is the impact of construction activities and the operational changes resulting from the installation of new ventilation systems and pump equipment on local air quality and adjacent residential properties.

The review was conducted in accordance with the City of Oakhaven Environmental Policy Manual and the State CEQA Guidelines. The project involves the replacement of aging infrastructure and the implementation of advanced odor control technologies.

**II. EXISTING CONDITIONS**

Wastewater Station C is a critical component of the municipal sewer system. Historical data indicates that the facility has been a source of intermittent hydrogen sulfide (H2S) emissions, primarily during periods of high flow and pump cycling. The existing ventilation system, installed in 1995, is no longer sufficient to maintain negative pressure within the pump house, leading to occasional odor release into the surrounding area.

Adjacent land uses include:
*   **North:** R-3 Residential Zone (average distance: 150 feet).
*   **West:** R-2 Residential Zone (average distance: 120 feet).
*   **South:** Oakhaven River (public access).
*   **East:** Industrial Light Zone (I-1).

Current air quality monitoring data from the nearest City station (Station 4, located 0.5 miles away) shows that H2S levels at the perimeter of WW-5522 occasionally exceed the threshold for "nuisance" complaints but remain below the regulatory limit for health hazards.

**III. PROPOSED MITIGATION MEASURES**

The project scope includes specific engineering controls designed to mitigate environmental impacts. The primary mitigation strategy is the installation of a new, high-efficiency ventilation system coupled with activated carbon filtration.

**A. Odor Control Impact Assessment**
Engineering calculations indicate that the new ventilation system, operating at a designed flow rate of 15,000 cubic feet per minute (CFM), will reduce hydrogen sulfide emissions by 90% compared to the baseline conditions. The system utilizes a dual-stage filtration process:
1.  **Stage 1:** Bio-scrubbing unit to remove bulk organic compounds.
2.  **Stage 2:** Activated carbon beds to adsorb trace H2S molecules.

The design pressure within the pump house will be maintained at -0.5 inches of water column to ensure that air flows inward from the exterior, preventing the escape of odors.

**B. Construction Phase Impacts**
Construction activities, including concrete demolition and equipment installation, will generate temporary dust and noise. The following mitigation measures are mandated:
*   **Dust Control:** All demolition areas must be wetted down prior to work. Dust suppression systems will be deployed on the site.
*   **Noise Control:** Heavy equipment operation will be restricted to the hours of 7:00 AM to 6:00 PM, Monday through Friday. No work will be conducted on weekends or holidays.
*   **Emissions:** All construction equipment must meet Tier 4 emission standards. No idling of generators or heavy machinery is permitted.

**IV. OPERATIONAL IMPACTS**

Upon completion, the new pump units and VFDs will operate more efficiently, reducing the frequency of pump cycling and the associated aeration of wastewater. This reduction in turbulence will further decrease the off-gassing of volatile compounds.

The installation of air quality monitoring stations at the perimeter of the facility is a key component of the project. Three (3) new monitoring stations will be installed:
1.  **Station NW:** Located at the northwest corner of the property, 10 feet from the R-3 boundary.
2.  **Station W:** Located at the western property line, 10 feet from the R-2 boundary.
3.  **Station N:** Located at the northern property line, 10 feet from the R-3 boundary.

These stations will continuously monitor concentrations of H2S, ammonia, and methane. Data will be transmitted in real-time to the Department of Public Works SCADA system and will be accessible to the public via the City's online environmental dashboard.

**V. CONCLUSION AND DETERMINATION**

Based on the technical analysis and the implementation of the proposed mitigation measures, the Environmental Compliance Division has determined that the project will have no significant impact on the environment.

Specifically:
*   **Air Quality:** The reduction in emissions by 90% ensures that air quality standards will be met and exceeded.
*   **Residential Impact:** No impact on adjacent residential properties is expected. The distance of the project from the nearest residential structures, combined with the enhanced odor control measures, mitigates any potential for nuisance or health concerns.
*   **Noise:** Construction noise will be temporary and within acceptable limits. Operational noise from the new pumps is expected to be lower than the existing equipment due to the VFDs.

**VI. RECOMMENDATION**

It is the recommendation of the Environmental Compliance Division that the Environmental Impact Report (EIR) be waived and a Negative Declaration be issued. The project is consistent with the City's environmental goals and will result in an improvement in local air quality.

**SUBJECT:** Vendor Selection for WW-5522 (Wastewater Station C Overhaul)

**I. BACKGROUND**

This memorandum serves to formally document the selection of the successful vendor for the Wastewater Station C Overhaul project (Parcel ID: WW-5522). The Engineering Division initiated a competitive bidding process, designated as Bid #4402, on March 15, 2024, to solicit proposals for the structural reinforcement, pump replacement, and electrical upgrades required for the project.

The bid solicitation was sent to pre-qualified vendors in the industrial construction and water utility sectors. A total of seven (7) bids were received by the deadline of May 30, 2024.

**II. BID EVALUATION PROCESS**

A bid evaluation committee, consisting of representatives from the Engineering Division, the Department of Public Works, and the City Attorney's Office, reviewed all submissions. The evaluation criteria included:
1.  **Technical Compliance:** Adherence to the specifications outlined in the permit application and architectural drawings.
2.  **Cost:** Total contract value and breakdown of costs.
3.  **Schedule:** Ability to meet the 18-month project timeline.
4.  **Experience:** Past performance on similar municipal wastewater projects.
5.  **Safety Record:** OSHA compliance history.

**III. BID SUBMISSIONS**

The following bids were received:
1.  **Industrial Fluid Systems:** $980,000 (Technical: Compliant; Schedule: 18 months; Experience: 15 years).
2.  **Apex Construction Group:** $1,045,000 (Technical: Compliant; Schedule: 20 months; Experience: 8 years).
3.  **Metro Water Solutions:** $995,000 (Technical: Non-Compliant - VFD specifications deviate from standard; Schedule: 18 months; Experience: 10 years).
4.  **Citywide Infrastructure:** $1,120,000 (Technical: Compliant; Schedule: 18 months; Experience: 20 years).
5.  **Reliable Pumps & Repair:** $985,000 (Technical: Compliant; Schedule: 19 months; Experience: 5 years).
6.  **GreenTech Utilities:** $1,010,000 (Technical: Compliant; Schedule: 18 months; Experience: 12 years).
7.  **Summit Engineering & Build:** $1,055,000 (Technical: Compliant; Schedule: 21 months; Experience: 7 years).

**IV. ANALYSIS AND RECOMMENDATION**

**Industrial Fluid Systems** was identified as the most responsive and responsible bidder.
*   **Cost Analysis:** The bid amount of $980,000 is the lowest compliant bid. It matches the total cost estimate provided in the permit application (Document A).
*   **Technical Compliance:** The vendor confirmed full compliance with the structural reinforcement specifications, the use of Grundfos U-5000 pumps, and the installation of the specified VFDs.
*   **Schedule:** The vendor committed to the 18-month timeline, with a detailed phasing plan attached to their proposal.
*   **Experience:** Industrial Fluid Systems has completed five (5) similar projects for municipal clients in the last decade, including the Oakhaven Water Treatment Plant expansion in 2019.

**Metro Water Solutions** was eliminated due to technical non-compliance regarding the VFD specifications. Their proposal suggested a different brand of drive that required additional engineering review, which was not feasible within the current budget constraints.

**Reliable Pumps & Repair** was eliminated due to a 1-month delay in the proposed schedule and a lower experience rating compared to the selected vendor.

**V. VENDOR SELECTION**

The Engineering Division recommends the award of the contract to **Industrial Fluid Systems**.

**Contract Details:**
*   **Vendor Name:** Industrial Fluid Systems
*   **Contract Value:** $980,000
*   **Project Scope:** Structural reinforcement, concrete repair, pump replacement, VFD installation, and ventilation system upgrade at Wastewater Station C (Parcel ID: WW-5522).
*   **Contract Duration:** 18 months from Notice to Proceed.

**Payment Terms:**
The contract shall be executed with the following payment schedule:
1.  **Mobilization Payment:** 10% of the contract value ($98,000) upon signing of the contract and submission of the performance bond.
2.  **Progress Payments:** 80% of the contract value ($784,000) paid in monthly installments based on the percentage of work completed, verified by the Project Engineer.

**PREPARED BY:** Industrial Fluid Systems
**PROJECT:** Wastewater Station C Overhaul (Parcel ID: WW-5522)

**I. SAFETY OBJECTIVES**

The primary objective of this safety plan is to ensure the health and safety of all workers and the public during the Wastewater Station C Overhaul project. The plan adheres to all OSHA regulations and City of Oakhaven safety standards.

**II. HAZARD IDENTIFICATION**

*   **Confined Space:** The pump house will be classified as a confined space.
*   **Hazardous Materials:** Hydrogen sulfide and other gases may be present.
*   **Heavy Machinery:** Excavators and cranes will be used.
*   **Electrical Hazards:** High-voltage electrical systems will be present.

**III. MITIGATION MEASURES**

*   **Confined Space Entry:** All workers entering the pump house must undergo confined space training. Atmospheric testing will be conducted before entry.
*   **Gas Detection:** Portable gas detectors will be used at all times.
*   **PPE:** All workers must wear hard hats, safety glasses, gloves, and high-visibility vests. Respiratory protection will be provided as needed.
*   **Traffic Control:** A traffic control plan will be implemented to protect workers and the public.
*   **Emergency Response:** An emergency response plan will be in place, including first aid and evacuation procedures.

**IV. TRAINING**

All workers will receive safety training prior to the start of work. The training will cover hazard recognition, PPE usage, and emergency procedures.

**V. INSPECTIONS**

Daily safety inspections will be conducted by the site supervisor. Any hazards identified will be corrected immediately.
