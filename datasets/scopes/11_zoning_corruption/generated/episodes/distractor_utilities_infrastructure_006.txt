CALL TO ORDER
The meeting was called to order by Chairperson Eleanor Vance at 6:02 PM. The Chairperson confirmed the presence of a quorum. The Clerk of the Commission verified the attendance of the following members: Councilor Marcus Thorne, Councilor Sarah Jenkins, Councilor David Wu, and Alternate Member Robert Hayes. The City Manager, James OMalley, was present. The Utilities Director, Linda Kowalski, was present. The City Clerk, Patricia Green, recorded the minutes.

ROLL CALL
Councilor Thorne: Present.
Councilor Jenkins: Present.
Councilor Wu: Present.
Councilor Hayes: Present.
Director Kowalski: Present.
City Manager OMalley: Present.

OPENING REMARKS
The Chairperson stated that the purpose of the meeting was to review the proposed Municipal Lighting Grid Modernization Project. The Chairperson noted that the project involves significant infrastructure changes within the city limits. The Chairperson invited the Utilities Director to present the initial findings regarding the energy infrastructure assessment.

PRESENTATION BY UTILITIES DIRECTOR
Director Kowalski presented a visual aid regarding the current state of the streetlighting infrastructure. The presentation outlined the current inventory of lighting fixtures across the municipal right-of-way. The presentation included data regarding the age of the fixtures and the current energy consumption rates. Director Kowalski stated that the current system utilizes high-pressure sodium technology. The presentation highlighted the operational costs associated with the current system. Director Kowalski presented data regarding maintenance frequency. The presentation included a comparison of the current system against proposed LED technology. The proposed system includes smart-grid capable LED units. The presentation detailed the proposed installation locations. The proposed installation locations cover 450 intersections. The presentation included a timeline for the removal and replacement process.

MOTION TO APPROVE PERMIT APPLICATION
Councilor Thorne moved to approve the permit application for the LED streetlight retrofit. The motion specified that the project is to proceed across 450 intersections. The motion referenced Parcel IDs: LG-001 to LG-450. The motion referenced the contractor BrightPath Municipal Services. The motion referenced the project budget of $1,650,000. The motion stated that the project includes removal of 450 high-pressure sodium fixtures and installation of smart-grid capable LED units.

SECONDING OF MOTION
Councilor Jenkins seconded the motion.

DEBATE
Councilor Wu addressed the motion. Councilor Wu requested clarification regarding the disposal of the old fixtures. Director Kowalski responded that the disposal would be handled in accordance with hazardous waste regulations. Councilor Wu requested clarification regarding the timeline. Director Kowalski responded that the installation would begin in the third quarter of the fiscal year. Councilor Jenkins addressed the motion. Councilor Jenkins requested clarification regarding the energy savings projection. Director Kowalski responded that the LED retrofit is projected to reduce annual electricity consumption by 60%. Director Kowalski noted the estimated annual savings of $220,000. Councilor Thorne noted the payback period of 7.5 years.

VOTE ON MOTION
The Chairperson called for a vote on the motion to approve the permit application.
Councilor Thorne: Yea.
Councilor Jenkins: Yea.
Councilor Wu: Yea.
Councilor Hayes: Yea.
The motion carried by a vote of 4 to 0.

MOTION TO APPROVE BUDGET ALLOCATION
Councilor Wu moved to approve the budget allocation for the project. The motion specified the total amount of $1,650,000. The motion specified that funds are to be allocated to BrightPath Municipal Services. The motion specified that funds are to be allocated for the removal of 450 high-pressure sodium fixtures and installation of smart-grid capable LED units.

SECONDING OF MOTION
Councilor Thorne seconded the motion.

DEBATE
The Chairperson opened the floor for public comment. Three members of the public spoke during the public comment session. The first member raised concerns regarding light pollution. The second member raised concerns regarding construction noise. The third member raised concerns regarding the timeline of the work. The Chairperson acknowledged the comments and directed the Utilities Director to note them in the record.

VOTE ON BUDGET MOTION
The Chairperson called for a vote on the motion to approve the budget allocation.
Councilor Thorne: Yea.
Councilor Jenkins: Yea.
Councilor Wu: Yea.
Councilor Hayes: Yea.
The motion carried by a vote of 4 to 0.

MOTION TO AUTHORIZE PROPERTY TRANSFER
Councilor Jenkins moved to authorize the property transfer of old fixture inventory. The motion specified the transfer from City of Oakhaven to BrightPath Municipal Services. The motion specified the consideration of $12,500 (scrap value). The motion specified the items as 450 high-pressure sodium lamps and 450 ballasts.

SECONDING OF MOTION
Councilor Wu seconded the motion.
