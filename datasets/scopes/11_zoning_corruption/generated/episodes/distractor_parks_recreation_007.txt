AGENDA ITEM 2: INFRASTRUCTURE AUDIT REVIEW
Director Thomas R. Evans presented the Infrastructure Audit Report for the fiscal year 2024. The report detailed the results of the annual facility safety inspections conducted by the City Engineering Division between May 1, 2024, and June 30, 2024. The inspection covered all public parks, recreation centers, and athletic complexes under the jurisdiction of the Parks and Recreation Commission.

The report identified three (3) specific structures requiring immediate repairs to maintain compliance with the City Safety Code Section 14.2. The structures were listed as follows:
1. Structure A: Oakhaven Community Center, North Wing Roof Truss System. Inspection Date: May 15, 2024. Condition Rating: Critical. Defect: Corrosion detected on load-bearing steel supports.
2. Structure B: Westside Athletic Complex, Dugout Facility No. 2. Inspection Date: May 22, 2024. Condition Rating: High Risk. Defect: Structural instability in concrete foundation near drainage outlet.
3. Structure C: Riverside Park Playground Equipment, Climbing Tower Unit 4. Inspection Date: June 5, 2024. Condition Rating: Moderate to High Risk. Defect: Degradation of safety surfacing material and loose anchoring bolts.

Director Evans recommended immediate repairs for all three structures to ensure public safety and prevent liability. The staff recommendation included a budget allocation of $185,000 for the immediate repairs. The funds were to be drawn from the Capital Improvement Reserve Fund.

Commissioner Robert T. Williams requested clarification on the timeline for the repairs. Director Evans stated that the City Engineering Division could begin work within 14 days of contract approval. Commissioner Williams asked if the repairs would require the closure of the affected facilities. Director Evans responded that Structure C (Playground Equipment) would require closure during the anchoring repair process, estimated to take three (3) days. Structure A and Structure B could be repaired with partial access to the facilities.

Commissioner Jennifer K. O’Connell asked about the long-term maintenance plan for the affected structures. Assistant Director Amanda S. Lewis provided a summary of the preventative maintenance schedule, noting that the current inspection cycle had been extended by two years due to budget constraints in the prior fiscal year. The new recommendation restored the standard one-year inspection cycle.

A motion to approve the staff recommendation for immediate repairs to the three identified structures was made by Vice-Chairperson David L. Chen and seconded by Member Sarah J. Miller.
Motion 2024-07-10-B: Approval of Infrastructure Audit Repairs.
Text: To approve the staff recommendation for immediate repairs to 3 structures identified in the Infrastructure Audit Report dated July 10, 2024.
Vote Count: 5-0.
Result: Approved.
The motion carried unanimously. The City Clerk recorded the vote count as 5-0.

AGENDA ITEM 3: BUDGET REVIEW AND QUARTERLY REPORTS
The Chief Financial Officer, James H. Wilson, presented the Quarterly Financial Report for the second quarter of fiscal year 2024. The report indicated that the Parks and Recreation Bureau had spent 45% of the allocated budget for operations and 30% of the allocated budget for capital improvements. The variance from the projected expenditure was within the acceptable margin of 5%.

Commissioner Sarah J. Miller inquired about the allocation for the upcoming summer programming. Director Evans confirmed that the summer programming budget remained stable at $320,000. The funds cover youth sports leagues, senior fitness classes, and special events at the city parks.

DOCUMENT 2: CONTRACT AGREEMENT
CONTRACT NUMBER: CW-2024-0805-IR
EFFECTIVE DATE: AUGUST 5, 2024
TERMINATION DATE: AUGUST 4, 2025

THIS AGREEMENT (the "Contract") is made and entered into by and between the CITY OF OAKHAVEN (hereinafter referred to as the "City"), a municipal corporation organized under the laws of the State of Oakhaven, and WATERWISE SOLUTIONS, LLC (hereinafter referred to as the "Contractor"), a limited liability company organized under the laws of the State of Oakhaven.

RECITALS:
WHEREAS, the City desires to maintain the irrigation systems located within City parks and recreational facilities; and
WHEREAS, the Contractor represents that it possesses the necessary expertise, equipment, and personnel to perform the maintenance services described herein; and
WHEREAS, the City has determined that the Contractor is qualified to perform the services pursuant to the Request for Proposal issued on May 1, 2024.

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants contained herein, the parties agree as follows:

SECTION 1: SCOPE OF SERVICES
The Contractor shall provide maintenance services for the irrigation systems across all City parks. The Scope of Work includes, but is not limited to:
1.1 Sensor Installation: Installation of moisture sensors at key irrigation zones within Oakhaven Community Park, Riverside Park, and Westside Athletic Complex.
1.2 Leak Detection: Conducting bi-weekly inspections for leak detection throughout the system.
1.3 System Optimization: Adjusting water pressure and flow rates based on sensor data and seasonal weather patterns.
1.4 Reporting: Submission of monthly reports detailing water usage, system status, and maintenance actions taken.
1.5 Emergency Response: Provision of 24-hour emergency response for major irrigation failures.

SECTION 2: COMPENSATION
In consideration for the performance of the Services, the City shall pay the Contractor a total Value of $25,000 (Twenty-Five Thousand Dollars).
2.1 Payment Schedule: Payment shall be made in four (4) equal installments of $6,250.00.
2.2 First Payment: Due upon execution of this Contract.
2.3 Subsequent Payments: Due on the first day of each subsequent quarter.
2.4 Final Payment: Due upon completion of all services and submission of the final report.

SECTION 3: TERM AND TERMINATION
3.1 Term: This Contract shall commence on August 5, 2024, and shall terminate on August 4, 2025, unless terminated earlier in accordance with this Section.
3.2 Termination for Cause: The City may terminate this Contract for cause if the Contractor fails to perform the Scope of Services.
3.3 Termination for Convenience: The City may terminate this Contract for convenience upon thirty (30) days written notice.

SECTION 4: INDEMNIFICATION AND INSURANCE
4.1 Insurance: The Contractor shall maintain general liability insurance with a minimum coverage of $1,000,000 per occurrence.
4.2 Indemnification: The Contractor agrees to indemnify and hold harmless the City from any claims arising out of the Contractor’s negligence.

SECTION 5: COMPLIANCE WITH LAWS
The Contractor shall comply with all applicable federal, state, and local laws, ordinances, and regulations, including but not limited to environmental protection standards regarding water usage.

SECTION 6: DISPUTE RESOLUTION
Any disputes arising out of this Contract shall be resolved through mediation in the County of Oakhaven. If mediation is unsuccessful, the dispute shall be resolved in the courts of the State of Oakhaven.

SECTION 7: SIGNATURES
IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the parties have executed this Contract as of the Effective Date.

PURPOSE:
The purpose of this memorandum is to establish schedule alignment for bike lane construction near park entrance. This coordination is necessary to minimize disruption to park visitors and ensure public safety during construction activities.

BACKGROUND:
The Transportation Department has identified a need to upgrade the bike lane infrastructure connecting Main Street to the Westside Athletic Complex. The proposed route passes directly adjacent to the park entrance. The Parks and Recreation Department manages the pedestrian access points and landscaping in this area.

TECHNICAL DETAILS:
1. Construction Location: 50-foot section of bike lane on the east side of the park entrance.
2. Construction Duration: Estimated 10 working days, starting September 20, 2024.
3. Traffic Control Plan: A traffic control plan has been prepared by the City Engineer. The plan includes lane closures, detour signage, and pedestrian rerouting.
4. City Engineer Approval: The Traffic Control Plan was approved by the City Engineer on September 5, 2024.

COORDINATION REQUIREMENTS:
1. Schedule Alignment: Construction activities must cease between 6:00 PM and 8:00 PM to accommodate evening park usage.
2. Access Maintenance: Pedestrian access to the park must remain open at all times. A temporary walkway will be installed during construction.
3. Noise Control: Construction equipment must adhere to the City Noise Ordinance limits.
4. Signage: Parks Department signage must be updated to reflect the temporary detour.

APPROVAL STATUS:
The Parks and Recreation Department acknowledges receipt of this memo. The Department agrees to the schedule alignment. The Transportation Department will coordinate with the Parks Department to post signage one week prior to construction.

DOCUMENT 7: ENVIRONMENTAL REVIEW SUMMARY
PROJECT: IRRIGATION SYSTEM MAINTENANCE
DOCUMENT TYPE: ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACT SUMMARY
DATE: AUGUST 15, 2024
PREPARED BY: Environmental Protection Division

PROJECT TITLE: WATERWISE SOLUTIONS IRRIGATION MAINTENANCE CONTRACT
PARCEL ID: 04-02-100-055
LOCATION: OAKHAVEN COMMUNITY PARK AND SURROUNDING PARKS

SCOPE OF REVIEW:
This summary reviews the potential environmental impacts of the Maintenance Services Contract awarded to WaterWise Solutions. The scope includes sensor installation and leak detection.

ENVIRONMENTAL FACTORS:
1. Water Usage: The installation of moisture sensors is designed to optimize water usage. The expected outcome is a reduction in water waste by approximately 10%.
2. Chemical Usage: No chemical treatments are involved in the maintenance scope. The work is mechanical and electronic.
3. Soil Disturbance: Sensor installation requires minor excavation. The excavation is limited to 12 inches depth.
4. Noise: Construction noise will be limited to daytime hours.
5. Waste: Old sensors will be recycled in accordance with City Waste Management Policy.

MITIGATION MEASURES:
1. Excavation will be backfilled immediately after installation.
2. Work will be conducted outside of bird nesting seasons where possible.
3. Water runoff from excavation will be contained to prevent sediment entry into storm drains.

CONCLUSION:
The proposed action does not have a significant adverse effect on the environment. The project complies with the City Environmental Protection Ordinance. No further environmental review is required.
