PROJECT DESCRIPTION
Application for trenching and conduit installation for fiber optic network expansion. Target area: Residential Zone 7, Parcel IDs: FO-101 through FO-115. Contractor: NetLink Solutions. Total estimated cost: $2,450,000. Route includes 8 miles of underground conduit.

TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS
The proposed project involves the excavation of existing soil strata to accommodate fiber optic cabling infrastructure. The excavation depth is standardized at 36 inches below the existing grade to ensure frost protection and structural integrity of the cabling. The conduit material selected is High-Density Polyethylene (HDPE) with a diameter of 2 inches. This material was selected based on tensile strength ratings and chemical resistance properties relative to local soil composition.

The route traverses multiple residential streets within the designated zone. Street names include Maple Avenue, Birch Lane, Cedar Drive, and Elm Street. The total linear footage of the trench is calculated at 42,240 feet, equating to 8 miles of underground conduit. The trenching process utilizes hydraulic augers and backhoes equipped with vacuum excavation capabilities to minimize impact on surrounding vegetation.

ZONING COMPLIANCE
The project area falls exclusively within Residential Zone 7 (R-7). This zoning designation permits utility infrastructure installation subject to Department of Public Works approval. The applicant has submitted a site plan overlaying the municipal grid. The overlay indicates no interference with existing street right-of-way boundaries. All construction activities are proposed to occur between the hours of 7:00 AM and 5:00 PM on weekdays to reduce noise impact on residents.

FINANCIAL STATEMENT
Total estimated cost: $2,450,000. This figure includes labor, materials, equipment rental, insurance premiums, and municipal permit fees. The applicant has provided a certificate of insurance naming the City of Oakhaven as an additional insured party. The policy limit is set at $5,000,000 per occurrence.

CONSTRUCTION SCHEDULE

Excavation Phase: 2024-06-02 to 2024-08-15
Cabling Installation Phase: 2024-06-10 to 2024-08-20

PROJECT DESCRIPTION
Supplemental application for sidewalk restoration. Restoration of 12,000 sq. ft. of asphalt and concrete sidewalks damaged during initial trenching. Budget allocation for restoration: $180,000. Completion deadline: June 1, 2024.

RESTORATION SCOPE
The initial trenching activities associated with the fiber optic expansion resulted in surface disturbance to existing pedestrian walkways. The specific areas requiring restoration are located adjacent to Parcel IDs FO-101, FO-105, FO-110, and FO-115. The total surface area affected is 12,000 sq. ft. The surface materials consist of a mixture of asphalt and concrete slabs.

The restoration plan involves the removal of damaged sections, preparation of the subgrade, and installation of new surface materials. For concrete sections, the specification requires a compressive strength of 3,500 PSI. The mix design includes a polymer additive for increased durability. For asphalt sections, the specification requires a binder grade of PG 76-22. The thickness of the new asphalt layer is specified at 3 inches.

TRAFFIC CONTROL PLAN
During the restoration phase, temporary traffic control measures will be implemented. Barricades and signage will be placed to delineate the work zone. Pedestrian rerouting will be available via temporary walkways where sidewalks are completely removed. The work is scheduled to commence immediately following the completion of the conduit installation in the affected zones.

MATERIAL SPECIFICATIONS
Concrete Mix Design:
- Cement: Type I Portland
- Aggregate: 3/4 inch crushed limestone
- Water-Cement Ratio: 0.45
- Admixture: Water reducer and air entraining agent

Asphalt Mix Design:
- Binder: Asphalt Cement PG 76-22
- Aggregate: Crushed granite
- Air Voids: 4.0% to 5.0%

BUDGET BREAKDOWN

COMPLETION DEADLINE
Completion deadline: June 1, 2024. This date was established based on the projected timeline for conduit installation and backfilling. Delays in the primary trenching project will necessitate a formal amendment to this restoration permit.

WARRANTY
NetLink Solutions provides a one-year warranty on all restoration work. This warranty covers defects in workmanship and material failure under normal use conditions. Any repairs required under warranty will be conducted at no additional cost to the property owner or the municipality.

INTERNAL MEMORANDUM

FROM: Telecommunications Coordinator
RE: Coordination with utility locators

SUMMARY
This memorandum serves to document the coordination efforts between the Telecommunications Department and the Public Works Department regarding the proposed fiber optic expansion in Residential Zone 7.

UTILITY LOCATOR REPORT
All existing underground utilities (gas, water, electric) have been marked with paint. No conflicts detected with the proposed fiber route. The utility locator team conducted a comprehensive scan of the 8-mile corridor prior to the commencement of any excavation activities.

MARKING SPECIFICATIONS
The following paint colors were utilized to denote specific utilities:
- Red: Electric power lines, cables, and conduit
- Yellow: Gas, oil, steam, petroleum, or gaseous materials
- Orange: Communications, alarm, or signal lines
- Blue: Potable water
- Green: Sewer and drain lines
- Purple: Reclaimed water, irrigation, or unknown

The marked lines correspond to the municipal utility map records on file. The coordinates for the marked lines were verified using GPS surveying equipment. The accuracy of the markings was assessed at +/- 12 inches within the trench area.

CONFLICT RESOLUTION
During the scanning process, three minor discrepancies were noted between the utility locator records and the physical markings. These discrepancies were resolved by re-scanning the specific locations. The final report indicates that the proposed fiber route does not intersect with any active high-pressure gas lines or primary water mains.

SCHEDULE ALIGNMENT
The excavation schedule for the fiber optic expansion aligns with the maintenance windows for the water and gas departments. No shutdowns of municipal services are anticipated during the construction period. The Public Works Department has been notified of the daily work zones to ensure ongoing safety compliance.

DOCUMENT 4: ENVIRONMENTAL REVIEW SUMMARY

DEPARTMENT OF ENVIRONMENTAL COMPLIANCE
PROJECT REVIEW FILE: ENV-2024-112
DATE OF REVIEW: 2024-05-20
PROJECT TITLE: Fiber Optic Expansion Proposal - Residential Zone 7

SCOPE OF REVIEW
The Environmental Compliance Division reviewed the proposed trenching and conduit installation project for adherence to the Municipal Environmental Protection Ordinance. The review focused on soil disturbance, noise levels, and potential impact on local flora and fauna.

SOIL DISTURBANCE
The project involves the excavation of 8 miles of underground conduit. The total volume of soil to be displaced is estimated at 15,000 cubic yards. The excavated soil will be stockpiled adjacent to the trench line during the cabling phase. Stockpiling will be limited to a maximum height of 4 feet to prevent soil erosion. Silt fencing will be installed at the perimeter of the stockpile areas.

NOISE IMPACT
Noise monitoring equipment will be deployed at the property lines of the most sensitive receptors. The equipment is calibrated to measure decibel levels at 50 Hz to 8,000 Hz. The maximum allowable noise level during construction hours is 85 decibels. The equipment used for trenching includes acoustic dampening features to reduce noise emissions.

VEGETATION IMPACT
The project route traverses existing greenbelts along Maple Avenue and Birch Lane. A total of 45 trees are located within 10 feet of the proposed trench line. These trees are marked for preservation. Root protection zones will be established around each tree. No heavy machinery will be operated within the root protection zones.

WATER RUNOFF
Stormwater runoff management plans are in place. Temporary diversion channels will be constructed to direct surface water away from the excavation site. This prevents sedimentation of nearby storm drains. Sediment basins will be installed at the outlet points of the diversion channels.

RECOMMENDATIONS
The review concludes that the project meets the requirements of the Municipal Environmental Protection Ordinance provided that all mitigation measures are implemented. The Environmental Compliance Division will conduct spot checks during the excavation phase.

APPROVAL STATUS
APPROVED WITH CONDITIONS
CONDITION 1: Noise monitoring logs must be submitted weekly.
CONDITION 2: Vegetation damage reports must be submitted within 24 hours of discovery.
CONDITION 3: Silt fencing must be inspected prior to any rainfall event.
