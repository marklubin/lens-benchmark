MEMORANDUM

SUBJECT: Legal Opinion - Crestview Holdings

ATTACHMENTS:
1. Verified LLC Formation Documents (Crestview Holdings LLC)

1.0 INTRODUCTION AND PURPOSE
This memorandum serves to provide a formal legal opinion regarding the corporate ownership structure of Crestview Holdings LLC and its associated entities in relation to the pending zoning variance application for the Target Parcel (Parcel ID: 44-2024-MLK-99). The Legal Department has conducted a comprehensive review of public records, business filings, and registered agent information to determine the true control and beneficial ownership of the applicant.

2.0 SCOPE OF REVIEW
The review was conducted between July 27, 2024, and July 28, 2024. The scope included:
- Verification of the legal status of Crestview Holdings LLC.
- Identification of all intermediary Limited Liability Companies (LLCs) linked to the primary applicant.
- Analysis of registered agent addresses and commonalities.
- Cross-referencing of ownership data with campaign finance records.
- Assessment of disclosure requirements under the Municipal Ethics Ordinance.

3.0 FINDINGS OF FACT

3.1 Primary Entity Status
Crestview Holdings LLC is currently registered as an Active entity with the State Secretary of State. The entity was formed on January 15, 2024. The principal listed on the initial formation documents is Marcus Webb. The legal status is confirmed as Active. The Tax ID is distinct from other entities.

3.2 Ownership Verification
Legal review confirms common agent for a network of entities. Marcus Webb controls Crestview Holdings and three intermediary LLCs that share the same registered agent. The intermediary entities are listed below:
- Intermediary 1: Lakeside Properties Group
- Intermediary 2: Greenfield Development Partners
- Intermediary 3: Summit Point Capital

3.3 Registered Agent Analysis
All four entities (Crestview Holdings LLC, Lakeside Properties Group, Greenfield Development Partners, and Summit Point Capital) utilize the same registered agent.
Registered Agent Name: Common Agent Services (CA-Services)
Agent Address: 500 Industrial Way
Agent City: Millbrook
Agent State: State
Agent Zip: 12345

The use of a single registered agent for multiple entities in the same industry and geographic area is a documented pattern in corporate structuring. The address "500 Industrial Way" is a known commercial office building in Millbrook that houses multiple professional service firms.

3.4 Ownership Structure Obscuration
The ownership structure obscures true control. The use of multiple LLCs with a common agent creates a layer of separation between the individual controlling the assets (Marcus Webb) and the public record of the primary applicant. This structure allows for the segregation of financial liabilities and the compartmentalization of property acquisitions.

3.5 Financial Connections
Cross-referencing with campaign finance records reveals that contributions totaling $14,000 were made by entities associated with this network to Zoning Board of Appeals members.
- Contribution 1: $5,000 from Lakeside Properties Group to Board Member A.
- Contribution 2: $3,000 from Greenfield Development Partners to Board Member B.
- Contribution 3: $6,000 from Summit Point Capital to Board Member C.
Total Contributions: $14,000.
Timing: Pre-Vote.
Voters: Yes.
No Voters: No.

4.0 LEGAL ANALYSIS

4.1 Disclosure Requirements
Under Section 4.2 of the Millbrook Ethics Ordinance, any person or entity with a financial interest in a zoning matter must disclose all related business entities and individuals with common control. The current application by Crestview Holdings LLC fails to list the three intermediary LLCs or the common agent.
Disclosure required for public record. The omission of these entities constitutes a failure to comply with the full disclosure requirement. The network is identified, and connections are verified. The agent is common, and the address matches.

4.2 Control and Influence
The Legal Department finds that Marcus Webb exercises control over the network. The common agent serves as the central point of contact for all filings, suggesting a centralized management structure. The pattern is clear. The timing of the property transfers and the contributions aligns with the pre-vote period.

4.3 Legal Risk Assessment
The Legal Department assesses the legal risk associated with the current application as Low regarding the validity of the zoning request itself, as the underlying property ownership appears to be legally transferred. However, the risk regarding procedural compliance and ethical disclosure is High. The failure to disclose the full network of entities creates a potential for the board to be found in violation of the Ethics Ordinance if the vote proceeds without full transparency.

5.0 RECOMMENDATIONS

5.1 Mandatory Disclosure
The Zoning Board of Appeals is advised to require Crestview Holdings LLC to file an amended application that explicitly lists all three intermediary LLCs (Lakeside Properties Group, Greenfield Development Partners, Summit Point Capital) and identifies Marcus Webb as the controlling individual.

5.2 Recusal Consideration
Board members who received contributions from the identified intermediary entities are advised to consider recusal from the vote, in accordance with Section 7.1 of the Ethics Ordinance. The relationship is spousal or direct financial, and the disclosure date is July 28, 2024.

5.3 Public Notice
Public notice is required. The Board should issue a public notice stating that the application is under review for full compliance with disclosure requirements. The disclosure level is Public.

6.0 CONCLUSION
The Legal Department concludes that the ownership structure of the applicant is designed to obscure true control. Legal review confirms common agent. The network is identified, and connections are verified. The agent is common, and the address matches. The pattern is visible. The evidence is strong. The case is closed for the purpose of this opinion, but the report is filed.
