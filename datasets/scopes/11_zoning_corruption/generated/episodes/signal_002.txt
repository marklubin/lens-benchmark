ITEM 7: ADVERTISEMENT FOR BIDS – DOWNTOWN STREET RESURFACING PROJECT

Planning Director Elena R. Rossi presented the proposal for the Downtown Street Resurfacing Project. The project encompasses the resurfacing of Main Street from 1st Avenue to 5th Avenue, including the installation of new curb ramps, repainting of crosswalks, and the replacement of three sections of storm drain grates. The total estimated cost is $425,000. The funding source is the State Transportation Improvement Grant (STIG) 2023-2024, with a local match of 10%.

ITEM 11: ZONING BOARD OF APPEALS HEARING – VARIANCE REQUEST FOR 450 RIVER ROAD

Planning Director Elena R. Rossi introduced the matter regarding a variance request submitted by the owner of 450 River Road, a property currently zoned R-2 (Single Family Residential). The applicant, Mr. James P. Henderson, is seeking a variance to allow the construction of a two-story accessory dwelling unit (ADU) and a detached garage structure that exceeds the current setback requirements by 4.5 feet. The proposed structure is to be used for short-term rental purposes, subject to the City's short-term rental ordinance.

The applicant states that the property is located in a high-demand area for short-term rentals due to its proximity to the Millbrook River Trail. The applicant argues that the variance is necessary to maximize the utility of the property and that the structure will not negatively impact the character of the neighborhood. The applicant has submitted a traffic impact study, an architectural rendering, and a letter of support from the immediate neighbors.

Staff Recommendation: The Planning Department recommends the denial of the variance request. The staff report notes that the proposed setback variance of 4.5 feet does not meet the criteria for a variance under Section 14.05 of the Millbrook Zoning Ordinance. The staff argues that the property is not unique in its physical characteristics and that the hardship is self-imposed. Furthermore, the staff notes that the proposed use as a short-term rental may create a nuisance for the surrounding residential area.

Zoning Board Administrator Marcus T. Webb stated that the Zoning Board of Appeals will hear the case on June 18, 2024. The Board will review the application, hear testimony from the applicant and the public, and render a decision. The Board may approve the variance with conditions, deny the variance, or remand the application for further review.

Public Comment:
Council Member Vance asked if the applicant has addressed the noise concerns raised in the traffic impact study.
Director Rossi responded that the applicant has proposed a sound barrier wall along the property line, which is included in the architectural rendering.
Council Member O'Malley asked if the short-term rental license has been issued.
Director Rossi stated that the short-term rental license has not been issued as the variance is a prerequisite for the license.
Council Member Thompson asked if the neighbors have signed the letter of support.
Director Rossi confirmed that the letter of support was signed by the owners of 448 and 452 River Road.

Motion by Council Member Chen, seconded by Council Member Miller, to forward the variance request for 450 River Road to the Zoning Board of Appeals for a hearing on June 18, 2024, and to direct the Planning Department to prepare a comprehensive staff report for the hearing.

ITEM 12: ENVIRONMENTAL REVIEW SUMMARY – MILLBROOK WASTEWATER TREATMENT PLANT UPGRADE

Public Works Director James P. Garris presented the Environmental Review Summary for the Millbrook Wastewater Treatment Plant Upgrade Project. The project involves the installation of new filtration systems, the expansion of the sludge dewatering facility, and the upgrade of the disinfection system to comply with the new State Environmental Protection Standards. The total estimated cost is $2,850,000. The funding source is a combination of State Environmental Improvement Grants and City General Fund reserves.

The Environmental Impact Statement (EIS) was completed on May 15, 2024. The EIS concluded that the project would have a minimal impact on the local ecosystem. The primary concerns addressed in the EIS were potential air quality impacts during construction and the disposal of sludge. The EIS recommends the implementation of dust control measures and the use of a certified waste disposal facility for sludge.

Staff Recommendation: The Public Works Department recommends the approval of the Environmental Review Summary and the authorization to proceed with the design phase of the project. The design phase is expected to take six months. Construction is scheduled to begin in January 2025.

Vice Chairperson Johnson noted that the property is not unique in its physical characteristics.
