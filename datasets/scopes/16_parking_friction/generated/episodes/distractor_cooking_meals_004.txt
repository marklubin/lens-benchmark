[09:25] User: Perfect. Thanks! Okay, also, quick question — every time I do tofu, it turns soggy after like 30 seconds 😩. How do I actually get crispy tofu that stays crispy?  
[09:27] Assistant: Ah, the classic tofu dilemma. The key is pressing out as much moisture as possible first. Wrap the tofu block in a clean towel and put something heavy on top for at least 15-20 minutes. Then, cut it into even pieces and toss it in a starch—cornstarch or potato starch both work well. The coating creates that crispy crust. Make sure your pan is hot before adding the tofu—medium-high heat is ideal. If you’re oven-roasting, spread the tofu on a lined baking sheet and bake at around 400°F, flipping halfway.  
[09:29] User: Does it matter if I use cornstarch or potato starch? Which’s better?  
[09:30] Assistant: Both work, but potato starch tends to get crispier and is more resistant to moisture, so your tofu stays crunchier longer. Cornstarch is fine too, especially if you don’t have potato starch, but if you want maximum crisp, go with potato starch.  
[09:32] User: And if I use an air fryer, does the method change?  
[09:33] Assistant: Not much—just toss the coated tofu in the air fryer basket in a single layer. Cook at 400°F for about 12-15 minutes, shaking halfway through. Keep an eye on it—air fryers vary. It’s best to spray lightly with oil before cooking to help crispiness. The coating should stay crispy for quite a while, especially if you serve with a separate dipping sauce.
