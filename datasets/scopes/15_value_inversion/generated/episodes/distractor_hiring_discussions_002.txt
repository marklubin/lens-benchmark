PHONE SCREEN NOTES (Marcus, May 18, 40 minutes):
- Excellent. She works on Stripe's real-time fraud detection pipeline — high-throughput, low-latency streaming systems. She described the system at a high level (she was careful about NDA boundaries, which I respect): it processes card transactions in real-time, applies a series of ML-based risk models, and returns a fraud score within a tight latency SLA.
- Deep experience with Redis, Kafka, and PostgreSQL. Described how their fraud detection system makes decisions in <50ms using an in-memory event store with Kafka-backed persistence. She walked me through the trade-offs: Redis for hot data (last 24 hours of transactions per card), Kafka for the durable event log, PostgreSQL for historical analysis and model training data.
- When I described our architecture, she immediately identified a potential bottleneck in our connection pool management (she was right — this is the issue we hit on May 19). She said: "If your Kafka consumers are sharing a PostgreSQL connection pool with your API layer, you'll hit contention under load. The consumers will hold connections during batch writes, starving the API of connections for read queries." Exactly the issue we've been investigating. I didn't tell her this — she inferred it from the architecture diagram I described verbally.
- I asked about her approach to system design. She described a methodical process: start with the data model, then work outward to the APIs and processing layers. She mentioned she always writes an architecture decision record (ADR) before starting a major project. "It forces you to think through the trade-offs before you write code, and it gives the team a record of why decisions were made."
- Salary expectations: $190K + wants to understand equity structure better. She's currently at approximately $280K total comp at Stripe (salary + RSUs). Similar to Olivia, she understands the startup trade-off. She asked specifically about the vesting schedule, cliff, and acceleration clauses. I told her we do standard 4-year vesting with 1-year cliff and single-trigger acceleration on acquisition. She said that was reasonable.
- Motivation: wants to be a technical leader at a smaller company. At Stripe, she's one of hundreds of senior engineers. Here she'd be one of two. "I want to own the architecture, not just a slice of it. At Stripe, I own one service in a pipeline of twenty. Here I'd own the pipeline." She also mentioned that she misses the pace of a smaller company — at PayStack before the acquisition, she could ship a feature in a week. At Stripe, the same feature takes a quarter after reviews, approvals, and compliance checks.
- Availability: she needs to give 3 weeks notice at Stripe. She said she could potentially start mid-June if we move fast, but July 1 is more realistic.
