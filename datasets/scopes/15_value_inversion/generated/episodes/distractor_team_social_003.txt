KAYAKING OFFSITE RECAP

From: Lina Choi
To: team@verdana-analytics.io
CC: events-committee@verdana-analytics.io
Date: June 14, 2025, 10:30 PM
Subject: Kayaking Recap — what a day!

Team,

The June offsite was perfect. The weather cooperated (78 degrees, light breeze from the southwest at maybe 5-8 mph), the Charles River was calm, and nobody capsized. I know some of you were worried after the "canoe incident" from the 2023 retreat but I promise — no repeats. Here's the full recap for posterity and for anyone who wants to relive the best Saturday this team has had in a while.

PRE-LAUNCH LOGISTICS (12:00 PM - 1:00 PM):
- We met at the Kendall Square kayak rental dock (Community Boating annex, not the main dock — sorry to those who went to the wrong one initially, looking at you Derek and Sarah)
- Derek texted the group chat at 11:50 asking "which dock" and I realized I hadn't specified. That's on me. They found us by 12:15.
- Helmets and life jackets were distributed. Everyone passed the brief safety orientation given by the rental staff member, whose name was Tyler. Tyler was very patient with our group, which was asking a lot of questions about river current speed and whether there were fish that could "bother" us (Amir's question).
- Yuki helped adjust PFD straps for about four people because the rental staff was overwhelmed with a second group arriving at the same time.
- Omar tested his waterproof Bluetooth speaker before we launched. It played three seconds of a cumbia track at full volume across the dock. Tyler did not seem impressed. We apologized.
- Nadia was the only person who brought her own paddle. She has a carbon fiber Werner paddle that she says cost more than her first car. Nobody questioned this.

THE KAYAKING (1:00 PM - 3:15 PM):
- 16 kayakers launched from the Kendall Square location at approximately 1:05 PM after Tyler gave us the go-ahead
- Tandem pairings were mostly self-selected:
  * Tomoko + Grace (Team Steady) — they called themselves this unprompted and it stuck
  * Derek + Sarah — natural pairing, they've kayaked together before on vacation in Maine
  * Amir + Sam (Team Beginners) — both first-time kayakers, grouped together for mutual support and entertainment value
  * Jess + Chris — Jess took the stern position and was very vocal about stroke timing, Chris was remarkably patient about it
  * Marcus + Priya — this pairing will be discussed further below
  * Yuki + Elena — Yuki has sea kayaking experience from her time in Hokkaido and basically served as Elena's personal instructor the whole time
  * Kevin + Rafael — Kevin in the stern, Rafael in the bow, Kevin providing a running commentary on everything they passed like a tour guide who has never been to Boston
  * Omar + Lina — I was in a kayak with the DJ, which means I had the best musical experience of anyone on the water
  * Nadia solo kayak — Nadia requested a solo kayak and the rental place had exactly one available. She was visibly relieved. Nadia is an experienced paddler and spent most of the trip weaving between the tandem kayaks offering unsolicited but genuinely helpful tips.

- We paddled upstream to the BU Bridge and back — approximately 4 miles round trip. The plan was originally to go all the way to the Eliot Bridge but we realized at the 45-minute mark that half the group was already tiring and we still had to turn around. Wise decision.

- Yuki gave an impromptu kayaking lesson to Amir and Sam at the put-in. She covered paddle grip, torso rotation, and how to do a basic forward stroke without splashing yourself in the face. Both survived with dignity. Amir only splashed himself twice during the actual lesson, which Yuki said was "better than most beginners." Sam picked it up faster but veered left consistently for the first twenty minutes because her right side is stronger than her left.

- Omar's waterproof speaker worked perfectly. We were the kayak group with a soundtrack. Playlist included a mix of Latin pop, some Khruangbin, a few Frank Ocean tracks, and at one point the Jurassic Park theme when we floated past the MIT sailing pavilion (Omar's joke — he'd queued it up specifically). Other kayakers either loved it or hated it. We choose to believe they loved it. One person in a single kayak gave us a thumbs up near the Harvard Bridge, so at least one stranger was on our side.

- Marcus and Priya's kayak was consistently the fastest, which Marcus attributes to his "superior paddle technique" and Priya attributes to "Marcus refusing to stop paddling for even one second." They were always at least 30 yards ahead of the main group. At one point Jess yelled "Marcus, this isn't a race!" from three kayak-lengths behind. Marcus yelled back "Everything is a race!" Priya looked tired. She later confirmed she was tired. Marcus showed no signs of fatigue at any point and this was annoying to everyone.

- Grace spotted a great blue heron near the BU Bridge. She was so excited she almost stood up in the kayak. Tomoko saved them both by grabbing Grace's PFD strap and pulling her back down while saying "Grace, no" in a very calm voice. The heron was standing in the shallows on the Cambridge side, about 15 feet from the bank, completely unbothered by our flotilla of 9 kayaks drifting past. Grace took approximately 40 photos with her phone in a waterproof case. She has promised to share the best ones.

- Chris spotted a turtle sunning on a log near the MIT boathouse on the return trip. Less dramatic than the heron but still appreciated.

- The return paddle was faster thanks to a light current assist and also because everyone was hungry and Lina had mentioned the sandwich situation waiting at Magazine Beach about six times.

- We returned all kayaks by 3:10 PM. No damage, no capsizes, no lost paddles. Tyler said we were "one of the better groups" he'd had this season. High praise from Tyler.

THE PICNIC (3:30 PM - 5:30 PM):
- Magazine Beach Park, under the big oak tree near the eastern edge of the lawn area. Nadia had gone ahead in her car to claim the spot and lay out two large picnic blankets plus a folding table she brought from home. This was heroic and she deserves recognition.
- Sandwiches from Clover (veggie options: impossible burger, chickpea fritter, egg & cheese) and Bon Me (banh mi: lemongrass chicken, five-spice pork, tofu) — enough for everyone with leftovers. I ordered 20 sandwiches for 16 people because I've learned from past events that someone always wants a second half. Correct call: five people had seconds.
- Additional food: Kevin brought a bag of kettle chips and two containers of hummus. Elena brought a fruit salad (watermelon, blueberries, strawberries) that was gone in approximately eight minutes. Jess brought homemade brownies that were, and I quote the group consensus, "unreasonably good." Jess says the secret is brown butter and espresso powder. She would not elaborate further.
- Drinks: Two coolers. Cooler one had sparkling water (LaCroix variety pack — the pamplemousse was the first to go, as always), iced tea, and lemonade. Cooler two had a few beers and hard seltzers for those who wanted them. Sam mixed lemonade and iced tea together and called it an "Arnold Palmer" and then spent five minutes explaining to Rafael who Arnold Palmer was.
- Kevin's sunscreen supply was critical. He arrived with three bottles — SPF 30, SPF 50, and SPF 70. He applied SPF 50 to Rafael's arms, neck, and face personally, as if Rafael were a child who could not be trusted. Rafael is not sunburned. This is a miracle. Kevin also provided sunscreen to Amir, Sam, Chris, and Derek, who had all forgotten to bring any. At one point Kevin was walking around the picnic like a sunscreen vendor at a baseball game. "SPF! Get your SPF here!" He actually said this.
- Grace brought a cornhole set — regulation size boards with a bean bag set she got from Amazon last year. She'd brought it to the last two events and it didn't get used, so she was very pleased when people actually wanted to play this time. She set it up on the flat section of grass about 20 feet from the picnic blankets.

CORNHOLE TOURNAMENT DETAILS:
- Format: Double elimination bracket (Grace insisted on proper structure)
- Teams of two, randomly drawn from a hat (Nadia's sun hat, specifically)
- Round 1 matchups and results:
  * Marcus/Amir vs Jess/Yuki — Score: 14-21. Jess and Yuki were surgical. Marcus was competitive but Amir's throws were wildly inconsistent. He overshot the board three times. Winners: Jess/Yuki.
  * Derek/Sarah vs Elena/Rafael — Score: 17-21. Close game. Derek has a surprisingly smooth underhand toss. Elena was the star of her team — she landed four bags in the hole in a row during one stretch. Sarah said "that's disgusting" in an admiring way. Winners: Elena/Rafael.
  * Sam/Omar vs Tomoko/Nadia — Score: 21-15. Omar has clearly played cornhole before and was not disclosing his experience level. Tomoko was consistent but Nadia was distracted by a dog that wandered over from another picnic group. She spent half the game petting the dog. The dog's name was Biscuit. Winners: Sam/Omar.
  * Kevin/Lina vs Priya/Grace — Score: 21-16. Kevin's throws were accurate if unspectacular. I (Lina) had two lucky shots that went straight through the hole from an awkward angle. Grace was not happy about this because she owns the cornhole set and feels she should have home court advantage. Winners: Kevin/Lina.
- Semifinals and finals happened but nobody can agree on the results because we lost track after the second round of lemonade. There may have been some beer involved in the scorekeeping deterioration. The winners' bracket pitted Jess/Yuki against Elena/Rafael, and Kevin/Lina against Sam/Omar. I remember winning our semifinal. I do not remember what happened after that. This is the official record and it is incomplete. I accept responsibility.
- Highlight moment: Amir attempted to catch a frisbee (which had appeared from somewhere — possibly the group next to us), missed entirely, and fell into a bush. He emerged with a leaf in his hair and immediately said "I meant to do that." He did not mean to do that. Derek caught the whole thing on his phone camera. The video is in the shared drive. It is 11 seconds long and it is the funniest thing I have seen this month.

OTHER PICNIC MOMENTS:
- Tomoko and Chris had a long conversation about fermentation — Tomoko has been making her own miso at home and Chris just started a sourdough starter. They exchanged notes and Tomoko offered to give Chris some koji spores from her supply. This is apparently a generous offer in fermentation circles.
- Grace showed anyone who would listen her heron photos. The best one is genuinely stunning — the bird is mid-stride with its reflection perfectly mirrored in the water. She's talking about getting it printed and framed. Several people encouraged this.
- Omar played acoustic guitar for about 20 minutes near the end of the picnic. He brought a small travel guitar that fits in a backpack case. He played a mix of bossa nova and what he described as "campfire classics." Elena and Nadia sang along to "Wagon Wheel" and it was actually good. Marcus attempted to sing along to "Redemption Song" and it was not good, but his enthusiasm was appreciated.
- Priya found a four-leaf clover on the picnic blanket and immediately pressed it into her notebook. She said she'd never found one before. This felt like a significant life event for her.
- Yuki did some stretching on the grass and three other people joined her. She led an informal 10-minute stretch session that she said would help with the post-kayaking soreness. Amir participated and said afterward that he felt "30% better," which is a specific and probably fabricated number but we let it stand.

CLEANUP:
- Everyone helped. All trash bagged and carried to the park dumpster. Blankets shaken out and folded. Cornhole boards loaded into Grace's car. Coolers divided between two vehicles. We left the spot cleaner than we found it. Magazine Beach Park staff: you're welcome.

COSTS:
- Kayak rentals (9 boats — 8 tandem, 1 solo, 2 hours): $400 (company card)
- Sandwiches + snacks (Clover order + Bon Me order): $138 (company card)
- Drinks (sparkling water, iced tea, lemonade, beer/seltzer): $42 (company card)
- Picnic supplies (plates, napkins, cups, trash bags): reused from office supply closet, $0
- Total: $580 (slightly over the $550 estimate — sorry Priya! The overage was because I added two extra sandwiches last minute when Chris mentioned he was bringing his appetite. I will absorb the $30 difference if needed but Priya said it's fine.)

[11:32 AM] TOMOKO ABE: Oleana in Cambridge. Mediterranean-inspired, incredible food, great atmosphere.
